### Spotlight: T (AT&T) — the one thesis-relevant development today

On 2026-06-29 T closed at **$21.82 (−3.9%)** and set a fresh 52-week low of **$21.28** intraday on a three-part catalyst:
1. **SpaceX/Starlink** — a *Financial Times* report (2026-06-26) that SpaceX President Gwynne Shotwell told IPO-roadshow investors SpaceX intends to launch a **Starlink-branded retail mobile service for U.S. consumers** (and may build its own terrestrial network) — i.e. shifting from carrier *partner* to direct *competitor* for AT&T's ~109M mobile subs ([Yahoo Finance](https://finance.yahoo.com/markets/stocks/articles/t-hits-52-week-low-192659845.html); [CoinCentral](https://coincentral.com/att-t-stock-falls-to-52-week-low-as-spacex-enters-mobile-market/)).
2. **Russell Top 50 index removal** in the latest reconstitution — mechanical/forced index selling.
3. **Oppenheimer downgrade** (Outperform → Perform) citing the LEO-satellite threat.

**Decision: keep T as a top pick (#6) but lower conviction.** The index removal is a price-insensitive technical seller (it *fits* the value thesis), and Starlink direct-to-consumer terrestrial mobile is capacity-constrained and years from scale versus a 109M-sub, spectrum-deep, fiber-converged network — so the headline caps the *multiple* via sentiment well before it dents *cash flows*. But it is a genuine, newly-concrete long-term competitive risk (no longer hand-wave "Starlink fear"), so I elevated it to T's lead value-trap item, lowered conviction a notch, and held intrinsic value at a deliberately conservative ~$26–28 (declining to chase a falling quote). The ~11.5% FCF yield and well-covered ~4.9% dividend still pay you to wait. T is now the most contestable name on the list — sized for the elevated competitive uncertainty.
