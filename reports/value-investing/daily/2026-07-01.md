### Spotlight A: META (Meta Platforms) — the core mispricing began to reframe

META rose **+8.8% to $612.92** on 2026-07-01 on a **Bloomberg report** that Meta is building a cloud/infrastructure business to **sell (rent out) its excess AI-compute capacity** to outside customers — potentially turning the maligned $125–145B AI-capex build into a *directly-billed* revenue stream competing with AWS/Azure/GCP, rather than an ad-only indirect payoff ([24/7 Wall St](https://247wallst.com/investing/2026/07/01/meta-platforms-jumps-9-on-potential-plans-to-sell-ai-compute-challenging-amazon-microsoft-google/); [Yahoo Finance](https://finance.yahoo.com/technology/ai/articles/meta-platforms-jumps-9-potential-143138489.html)). Supporting items: ~1.6GW of Crusoe AI compute locked (TX/MO) and a ~$900M stake in India's Cred (founder to lead WhatsApp).

**Decision: keep META at #7, do NOT raise intrinsic value.** This is precisely the concern the META file identified as the source of the de-rating (unmonetized AI infra), so the report *validates the qualitative thesis*. But (a) **Meta has not confirmed the plan** (Reuters could not verify), and (b) the +8.8% pop compressed the discount to my unchanged ~$720–780 IV to ~15–17% — so the *value case* is now thinner even as the *thesis* strengthened. META is the least-cheap name on the list; position discipline (starter-to-medium, dual-class-control cap) unchanged. The real test is Q2 earnings on 2026-07-29.

### Spotlight B: T (AT&T) — the slide continued to another fresh low

T fell to a **2026-07-01 close of $20.49** and printed a new 52-wk low of **$20.29** intraday — a cumulative ~6.1% drop over two sessions from the 06-29 $21.82 reference. **No genuinely new company-specific news:** the driver remains the SpaceX/Starlink direct-to-consumer mobile threat plus Russell-index forced selling and rate/bond-proxy sentiment.
