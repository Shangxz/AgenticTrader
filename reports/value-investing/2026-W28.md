## Short Market Context

The U.S. equity market looks selective rather than broadly cheap. Several current market signals point to elevated broad-market risk: Business Insider reported that the S&P 500 Momentum Index's April-May move exceeded dot-com-era extremes and that valuation measures such as Shiller P/E, price-to-book, and price-to-sales look stretched, while MarketWatch reported a rotation out of high-momentum semiconductor winners and into financials, healthcare, and consumer staples. Vanguard's spring valuation work also said U.S. equities remained above long-term fair value, while U.S. value and small-cap stocks offered more attractive relative valuations.

Implication for this run: favor durable cash generators and temporarily out-of-favor quality businesses over the hottest AI/momentum winners. I screened for a combination of current valuation, business quality, balance sheet/cash-flow support, and a plausible medium-term re-rating path.

## Today's Top Ideas

### 1. MarketAxess Holdings (MKTX)

- **Why it looks undervalued:** MKTX traded around $117.14 with a market cap near $4.1 billion and a trailing P/E of about 13.9x as of the run. MarketWatch noted the stock was roughly 47% below its 52-week high, giving a large valuation reset for a still-profitable electronic trading franchise.
- **Why it still qualifies as quality:** MarketAxess remains a high-margin market-infrastructure business. Q1 2026 revenue was reported at $233.4 million, up 11.9% year over year, with a pre-tax margin around 44.5% and adjusted EPS of $2.25.
- **Main risks or disconfirming factors:** The market may be discounting real competitive pressure from larger trading venues and banks, possible share loss, and cyclicality in bond trading volumes. A low multiple is only attractive if electronic credit trading growth and margins stabilize.
- **Notable valuation signals:** Current quote data showed a 13.9x trailing P/E; recent daily market data put the stock far below its prior-year high; Q1 margins remain strong despite the drawdown.
- **Confidence:** Medium-high. The valuation reset is meaningful and quality metrics remain strong, but competitive concerns need monitoring.

### 2. A. O. Smith (AOS)

- **Why it looks undervalued:** AOS traded around $59.47 with a market cap near $8.2 billion and a trailing P/E of about 15.9x. That is a reasonable price for a branded, cash-generative industrial with recurring replacement demand.
- **Why it still qualifies as quality:** A. O. Smith has durable positions in water heaters and water treatment. Q1 2026 sales were $946 million, net earnings were $118 million, and free cash flow was $118.9 million, helped by working-capital discipline.
- **Main risks or disconfirming factors:** Management lowered its full-year 2026 outlook, so the market may be correctly discounting weaker end-demand, housing sensitivity, and China pressure. This is not a high-growth idea.
- **Notable valuation signals:** Current quote data showed a mid-teens P/E; Q1 free cash flow covered net income, supporting the quality side of the case.
- **Confidence:** Medium-high. Attractive if an investor wants cash conversion and balance-sheet resilience more than rapid growth.

### 3. Elevance Health (ELV)

- **Why it looks undervalued:** ELV traded around $416.08. Against management's raised 2026 adjusted diluted EPS guidance of at least $26.75, that implies roughly 15.6x forward adjusted earnings, a modest valuation for a scaled health insurer and benefits platform.
- **Why it still qualifies as quality:** Elevance has scale, diversified health-plan exposure, and meaningful earnings power. Q1 2026 operating revenue was $49.5 billion; adjusted diluted EPS was $12.58, and management raised full-year adjusted EPS guidance.
- **Main risks or disconfirming factors:** The CMS matter, medical-cost trend, Medicare/Medicaid policy, and political scrutiny can all overwhelm near-term valuation support. The idea depends on cost actions and rate/pricing discipline remaining effective.
- **Notable valuation signals:** Current quote data showed a trailing P/E of about 17.6x; the guidance-based multiple is lower and more relevant if adjusted earnings prove durable.
- **Confidence:** Medium. Valuation is appealing, but managed-care regulatory and utilization risk keeps this below the top two.

### 4. Berkshire Hathaway (BRK.B)

- **Why it looks undervalued:** BRK.B traded around $494.79 with a trailing P/E near 14.3x. Barron's reported Class B shares recently near $505.59, less than 1% higher for the year, and estimated valuation around 1.45x book value, below the 2025 peak.
- **Why it still qualifies as quality:** Berkshire remains a diversified collection of insurance, railroad, energy, manufacturing, service, and retail businesses with exceptional liquidity. Recent reporting put cash and Treasury holdings at record levels, roughly $380 billion to nearly $400 billion depending on the source and measurement date.
- **Main risks or disconfirming factors:** Succession under Greg Abel, cash drag, limited buybacks, and fewer obvious acquisition opportunities may keep the valuation range-bound. A huge cash pile is valuable optionality but can also dilute returns if opportunities remain scarce.
- **Notable valuation signals:** Current quote data showed a low-teens P/E; Barron's estimated roughly 1.45x book value; Q1 reporting showed strong operating earnings and record liquidity.
- **Confidence:** Medium. Berkshire is the lowest-drama quality-value compounder on the list, but upside may be steadier than explosive.

### 5. Comcast (CMCSA)

- **Why it looks undervalued:** CMCSA traded around $23.19 with a market cap near $82.8 billion and a trailing P/E near 4.5x. The valuation is extremely low for a company still producing large free cash flow.
- **Why it still qualifies as quality:** Comcast generated Q1 2026 adjusted EBITDA of $7.9 billion and free cash flow of $3.9 billion, despite profitability pressure. Its cable, broadband, media, and theme-park assets still have scale and cash-generation capacity.
- **Main risks or disconfirming factors:** This is the clearest potential value trap in the top group. Broadband competition, video cord-cutting, media disruption, and lower Q1 EBITDA/free cash flow are real structural issues. The reported NBCUniversal spinoff may help reveal value, but it does not remove operating pressure.
- **Notable valuation signals:** Current quote data showed a very low P/E; Q1 free cash flow remained substantial; Barron's reported the media/entertainment spinoff could improve strategic focus and valuation transparency.
- **Confidence:** Medium-low. Cheap enough to monitor closely, but quality is more contested than the names above.

### 6. Nike (NKE)

- **Why it looks undervalued:** NKE traded around $42.89, with the stock reportedly down more than 75% from its 2021 peak. Morningstar recently included Nike among undervalued wide-moat stocks, but the current trailing P/E of about 28.4x means this is more a brand-turnaround value idea than a statistically cheap stock.
- **Why it still qualifies as quality:** Nike retains one of the strongest global sportswear brands, enormous scale, and a wholesale recovery path. Fiscal Q4 2026 wholesale revenue rose 4% reported, and gross margin improved sharply to 49.2%.
- **Main risks or disconfirming factors:** The operating picture is still mixed: fiscal Q4 revenue declined 1% reported and 4% currency-neutral; Nike Direct declined 7% reported; Converse declined 32% reported. The gross-margin improvement was heavily helped by an expected tariff recovery, so underlying brand and channel momentum still needs proof.
- **Notable valuation signals:** Large multi-year share-price drawdown and wide-moat status are positives; current P/E is not obviously cheap until earnings recover.
- **Confidence:** Medium-low. Worth watching as a quality turnaround, but not as compelling as MKTX, AOS, or ELV on today's numbers.
