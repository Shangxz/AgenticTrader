## PROMPT 1: BUY-SIDE MISPRICING ANALYSIS

*Acting as an experienced buy-side analyst specialising in mispriced, undervalued companies.*

**Valuation context:** CMCSA trades at ~5.2x adjusted earnings / ~6.8x forward / ~5.2x NTM EV/EBITDA and **below book value (0.91x)** with a ~5.9% dividend yield — versus telecom peers Verizon (~11x P/E, ~6.5x EV/EBITDA) and AT&T (~7.4x P/E, ~6.0x EV/EBITDA), and media peer Disney (~16.6x P/E) (Robinhood, 2026-06-22).

**1. Business model clarity.** Comcast is genuinely *complex* — that is part of the mispricing. It is four very different businesses under one ticker: (a) residential & business connectivity (broadband + the fast-growing Xfinity Mobile MVNO), (b) NBCUniversal media/networks, (c) Studios, and (d) Theme Parks (Universal, including Epic Universe, opened May 2025). Analysts struggle to value a melting-ice-cube fear (cable broadband) bolted to a growth asset (theme parks) and a turnaround (Peacock/streaming). The market appears to be applying a "secular-decline cable multiple" to the *whole* company while giving little credit to parks, wireless, or content.

**2. Earnings quality.** Reported FY2025 GAAP EPS ($5.39) *overstates* run-rate power because of the $9.4B Versant gain; adjusted EPS of $4.31 is the fair reflection, and post-spin earnings lose Versant's networks contribution. Against that, **free cash flow of $19.2B** on an $80B market cap (~24% FCF yield, pre-spin) is high-quality and cash-backed, and theme-park EBITDA is real and growing fast (+24% in Q4 2025, +33% to $551M in Q1 2026; WDW News Today, 2026: https://wdwnt.com/2026/01/universal-theme-park-revenue-increases-22-thanks-to-epic-universe/ ; Comcast Q1 2026 release: https://www.cmcsa.com/news-releases/news-release-details/comcast-reports-1st-quarter-2026-results). Earnings quality is *good*; the headline multiple is just noisy.

**3. Hidden assets / optionality.** Significant: (a) **Universal/Epic Universe** — theme parks generated $3.1B adjusted EBITDA in FY2025 and are growing double digits; a standalone parks business would command a far higher multiple than 5x. (b) **Xfinity Mobile** — 9.3M wireless lines, +1.5M net adds in 2025, >15% penetration of broadband base (gurufocus call highlights, 2026: https://www.gurufocus.com/news/8815309/comcast-corp-cmcsa-q1-2026-earnings-call-highlights-record-wireless-growth-and-olympic-boost) — a growing, under-monetized asset. (c) **Peacock** approaching profitability (TIKR, 2026: https://www.tikr.com/blog/comcast-has-fallen-33-from-its-high-peacock-turning-profitable-in-q2-could-be-the-turning-point). None of these are reflected in a sub-book, 5x-earnings valuation.

**4. Recent sentiment.** The stock fell ~13% *after* a Q1 2026 earnings *beat* ($0.79 vs $0.72 expected, revenue +11%; Investing.com transcript, 2026: https://www.investing.com/news/transcripts/earnings-call-transcript-comcast-q1-2026-beats-expectations-stock-surges-93CH-4632898 — note: initial pop, then sold off; TIKR, 2026: https://www.tikr.com/blog/comcast-beat-q1-2026-earnings-and-still-fell-13-heres-why-the-selloff-was-overdone). The entire cable group de-rated violently: Charter (CHTR) collapsed from a $422 52-week high to ~$124 (P/E ~3.4x, also a 52-week low on 2026-06-22; Robinhood). This looks like *sector capitulation* over broadband competition (fixed-wireless access, fiber overbuild) rather than a Comcast-specific fundamental break.

**5. Who is likely selling and why.** Income/"safe telecom-media" holders capitulating on the broadband-saturation narrative; index and sector funds dumping the whole cable complex; momentum sellers exiting a stock at new lows. These are sentiment- and flow-driven sellers, not fundamental ones — broadband subscriber losses actually *narrowed* by 117k YoY to just 65k in Q1 2026 (Comcast Q1 2026 transcript, Motley Fool, 2026: https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2026/04/23/comcast-cmcsa-q1-2026-earnings-transcript/).

**What the market must believe for today's valuation to be justified:** that connectivity FCF declines structurally and *permanently*, that wireless and theme parks cannot offset it, and that ~$19B of FCF erodes materially over time — i.e., that this is a melting ice cube. The data (narrowing broadband losses, +1.5M wireless lines, double-digit parks growth, Peacock nearing breakeven) contradicts the terminal-decline thesis. **The belief required to justify a below-book, ~5x valuation looks too pessimistic.**

## PROMPT 2: COMPARABLE COMPANY ANALYSIS

*Acting as a valuation analyst building comps for an investment committee.*

**Subject:** CMCSA at **~5.2x adjusted earnings / ~5.2x NTM EV/EBITDA / 0.91x book**.
**Sector:** Cable / connectivity + diversified media & theme parks.

**Step 1 — Peer group.** Comcast straddles two groups; the right comp set blends both:
- **Charter Communications (CHTR)** — closest pure broadband/cable + MVNO comp. *Best structural comp, though it lacks media/parks.*
- **Verizon (VZ)** — large connectivity, dividend-anchored, FWA broadband competitor. *Good connectivity comp.*
- **AT&T (T)** — connectivity + fiber + wireless. *Good connectivity comp.*
- **Walt Disney (DIS)** — comp for the parks + media + streaming side. *Best comp for the "experiences + content" portion.*
- *Flag as poor comps:* Warner Bros. Discovery (WBD, negative earnings, no connectivity), and pure-streamers like Netflix (no connectivity/parks, very different margin/growth profile).

**Step 2 — The valuation gap (Robinhood 2026-06-22; EV/EBITDA from valueinvesting.io/stockanalysis 2026):**

| Company | Trailing P/E | P/Book | EV/EBITDA (approx) | Div yield |
|---------|-------------|--------|--------------------|-----------|
| CHTR | 3.41 | 0.95 | ~5.5x | — |
| VZ | 11.06 | 1.83 | ~6.5x | 6.1% |

Connectivity-peer P/E median (CHTR/VZ/T) ≈ **7.4x**; EV/EBITDA median ≈ **6.0x**. Comcast trades **below** that connectivity median on EV/EBITDA despite *also* owning theme parks and media that Verizon and AT&T do not. Against a blended fair multiple that gives any credit to parks (Disney-like) and wireless growth, Comcast's discount is **~25–40%**.

**Step 3 — Explaining the gap.** Some discount is warranted: cable broadband faces real FWA/fiber competition, and media/linear networks are in secular decline. But the *size* of the discount is not explained by fundamentals — Comcast's broadband losses are narrowing, its wireless is growing faster than peers', and its parks segment is a genuine growth asset. The gap is better explained by (a) sector-wide capitulation tarring all cable names (see Charter's collapse), (b) complexity/conglomerate discount, and (c) the Versant spin muddying reported numbers. Verizon's *higher* multiple despite slower growth shows the discount is sentiment-driven, not return-driven.

**Step 4 — What closes the gap.** To re-rate toward even a 7–8x adjusted-earnings / 6.5x EV-EBITDA blend (telecom-peer parity), Comcast needs to (1) show broadband subscribers stabilizing/inflecting (already narrowing), (2) keep compounding wireless lines and parks EBITDA, and (3) get Peacock to profitability. KeyBanc's $47 price target (vs ~$22 today) implies ~110% upside and credits Epic Universe alone with ~$10/share (AInvest, 2025: https://www.ainvest.com/news/comcast-epic-universe-catalyst-unlocking-47-theme-park-dominance-cable-investments-drive-growth-2505/). A more conservative re-rate to ~7–8x adjusted EPS (~$30–35) looks achievable on the current trajectory.

## PROMPT 3: CATALYST & VALUE-TRAP ANALYSIS

*Acting as a value-oriented PM evaluating a concentrated-portfolio addition.*

**Thesis:** The market is pricing Comcast as a terminally declining cable company at ~5x adjusted earnings and below book, while ignoring ~$19B of free cash flow, a fast-growing Xfinity Mobile (9.3M lines), and a double-digit-growth theme-park business (Epic Universe). Stabilizing broadband plus continued parks/wireless growth should drive a re-rate.
**Approx. discount to my intrinsic estimate (~$33–35):** **~35%.**

**Catalyst analysis:**
1. **Specific repricing events:** (a) A quarter of *flat-to-positive* broadband net adds (losses already narrowing); (b) Peacock reaching profitability (flagged for ~Q2 2026); (c) continued double-digit Theme Parks EBITDA growth as Epic Universe ramps a full year; (d) accelerated buybacks at sub-book prices (Comcast returned $2.5B to shareholders in Q1 2026); (e) further portfolio simplification after the Versant spin.
2. **Likelihood / timeframe:** Parks growth and buybacks are *high-probability* near-term (12 months). Peacock profitability is *medium-high* (2026). Broadband inflection to growth is *medium* and the key swing factor (12–24 months).
3. **Biggest-impact catalyst:** Broadband subscriber stabilization — it is the crux of the bear thesis; proof that connectivity FCF is durable would force a re-rate of the whole multiple.
4. **Near-term catalysts working *against* the thesis:** Continued FWA/fiber competitive intensity pressuring broadband ARPU/units; heavy capex on Epic Universe and network upgrades weighing on near-term FCF; linear-media advertising/affiliate decline accelerating.

**Value-trap check:**
5. **Three common reasons these stocks never re-rate, and which apply:** (i) *Secular terminal decline of the core* — the central risk; cable broadband faces real competition, though data shows deceleration of losses, not collapse. (ii) *Conglomerate/complexity discount that never lifts* — applies; the market may persistently refuse to credit parts (the Versant spin is management's attempt to address this). (iii) *Capital sunk into low-return defense* — partial risk via heavy capex, but Epic Universe is a *growth* investment already producing rising EBITDA, not pure defense.
6. **Governance / capital allocation:** Comcast is controlled by the Roberts family (super-voting structure), which limits outside-shareholder influence — a real governance flag. Offsetting it, capital allocation has been shareholder-friendly: a ~5.9% dividend, consistent buybacks (now at sub-book prices), and a tax-free spin to simplify. The governance risk is "control overhang," not value destruction.

**Risk/reward verdict:** Buying a ~$19B-FCF business with growing wireless and theme-park assets at below book and ~5x adjusted earnings offers a wide margin of safety, paid to wait via a ~5.9% yield and supported by sub-book buybacks. The honest risk is that broadband is a slow melt and the conglomerate discount persists — this is the screen's *deepest-value, highest-debate* name. **Risk/reward is attractive for a value mandate willing to underwrite broadband stabilization and tolerate a controlled-company structure**, but it carries more genuine value-trap risk than WFC and should be sized accordingly.
