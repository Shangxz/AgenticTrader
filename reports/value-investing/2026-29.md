# Value Investing Report - ISO Week 2026-29

Latest run date: 2026-07-18

This report is research support only, not personal financial advice. Each update starts from a fresh quality-value review of US-listed public equities. Earlier entries from the same week are preserved as a record, but they are not used to steer the current ranking.

## Market Context

The US equity market still looks selectively priced rather than broadly cheap. FactSet's latest Earnings Insight, reviewed July 18, shows the S&P 500 forward 12-month P/E at 20.3, above both the 5-year average of 19.9 and the 10-year average of 19.0. That backdrop keeps the margin of safety thin at the index level and raises the hurdle for quality-value stock selection.

The latest market action also supports a stock-specific approach. Friday, July 17 market coverage showed pressure in several AI and semiconductor-linked winners, while broader equal-weighted market breadth held up better than the cap-weighted technology leadership. That rotation can create opportunities in fallen quality franchises, but it also means valuation work must separate durable free-cash-flow businesses from cyclicals whose low multiples mainly reflect peak earnings.

## Current Fresh Ranking - 2026-07-18

The July 18 review started from a fresh screen across US-listed large and mid-cap equities, emphasizing valuation, free cash flow, returns on capital, balance-sheet risk, business durability, and current public evidence. Prices and valuation ratios below use the latest available public data checked July 18, generally reflecting the July 17 market close.

### 1. Accenture (ACN)

**Why it looks undervalued:** ACN remains the strongest current quality-value candidate. StockAnalysis data checked July 18 shows a trailing P/E of 11.48, forward P/E of 10.01, P/FCF of 6.98, EV/EBITDA of 6.65, and EV/FCF of 6.84 after a 48.66% 52-week share-price decline. That is unusually low for a global consulting and technology-services franchise if current cash conversion and client relevance prove durable.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** ACN has ROE of 24.41%, ROIC of 27.09%, interest coverage of 43.37x, debt/FCF of 0.67, and an Altman Z-score of 4.79. It generated $12.58 billion of trailing free cash flow, has a 17.21% FCF margin, and has reduced share count by 1.53% year over year.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** The market may be discounting real pressure from slower discretionary consulting demand, AI-driven delivery disruption, pricing pressure, and the difficulty of managing a very large labor base. Piotroski F-score is only 5, so the quality signal is strong but not pristine.

**Notable valuation signals:** FCF yield is 14.32%, earnings yield is 8.87%, shareholder yield is 6.04%, and analyst consensus remains Buy with a price target 24.77% above the current price. ACN keeps the top rank because the cash-flow multiple, ROIC, low leverage, dividend support, and still-positive 3-year revenue/EPS forecasts combine better than the rest of the screen.

### 2. Arch Capital Group (ACGL)

**Why it looks undervalued:** ACGL remains one of the cleanest quality-value names in the current screen. StockAnalysis data checked July 18 shows a trailing P/E of 7.77, forward P/E of 10.87, P/FCF of 6.02, EV/EBITDA of 6.29, and EV/FCF of 6.33. The stock is up 13.79% over the last 52 weeks, but the cash-flow and earnings valuation remain low for its recent underwriting profitability.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** ACGL shows ROE of 21.31%, ROIC of 18.41%, operating margin near 29%, profit margin near 24%, and very strong free-cash-flow generation. Balance-sheet risk looks modest: debt/equity is 0.11, debt/FCF is 0.47, and interest coverage is 38.15x. Share count is down 3.09% year over year.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** Insurance earnings are cyclical and catastrophe-sensitive. The low multiple may partly reflect concerns that underwriting margins, reserve development, or investment income are above normalized levels. The 3-year revenue forecast is negative, so this thesis depends more on underwriting discipline, capital allocation, and valuation support than on growth.

**Notable valuation signals:** FCF yield is 16.61%, earnings yield is 13.70%, buyback yield is 3.09%, and analyst consensus remains Buy. ACGL stays second because it is exceptionally balanced, but ACN's combination of price decline, cash-flow yield, net cash position, and non-insurance business durability gives it slightly more upside asymmetry today.

### 3. Adobe (ADBE)

**Why it looks undervalued:** ADBE remains a strong fallen-quality candidate. StockAnalysis data checked July 18 shows a trailing P/E near 13, forward P/E around 9, P/FCF around 9, and double-digit FCF yield. For a software company with very high margins and high returns on invested capital, those multiples look materially discounted if generative AI risk is manageable.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** ADBE has ROE above 60%, ROIC above 60%, gross margin near 89%, operating margin near 37%, profit margin near 29%, and FCF margin above 40%. Balance-sheet risk appears manageable, with low debt relative to free cash flow, high interest coverage, an Altman Z-score of 8.35, and Piotroski F-score of 7.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** The central risk is competitive and technological disruption from AI-native creative tools, plus the possibility that customers resist subscription pricing or bundled economics. Analyst consensus is Hold rather than Buy, which signals uncertainty around the durability of growth assumptions.

**Notable valuation signals:** FCF yield is 10.90%, buyback yield is 5.84%, and analyst forecasts show 3-year revenue growth of 9.71% and EPS growth of 14.53%. ADBE ranks ahead of several classic value names because business quality is exceptional and the valuation is now low enough to compensate for some, though not all, AI uncertainty.

### 4. The Cigna Group (CI)

**Why it looks undervalued:** CI remains the best healthcare services value candidate in the screen. StockAnalysis data checked July 18 shows a trailing P/E around 12, forward P/E around 9, P/FCF below 10, and EV/EBITDA in the high-single digits. The stock is not deeply distressed, but the forward earnings and cash-flow multiples are attractive for a large recurring healthcare franchise.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** CI has ROE of 16.27%, ROIC of 14.34%, meaningful trailing free cash flow, and a 7.03% shareholder yield from dividends plus buybacks. Share count is down 4.83% year over year. Leverage is meaningful but still manageable, with debt/EBITDA around 2.3x and interest coverage above 8x.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** Healthcare insurers and pharmacy benefit managers face regulatory, reimbursement, medical-cost, and political risk. Margins are structurally thin, so adverse policy or cost surprises can move earnings quickly.

**Notable valuation signals:** FCF yield is 10.29%, earnings yield is 8.45%, dividend yield is 2.22%, analyst consensus is Buy, and the average price target is 21.13% above the current price. CI remains selected because the valuation, cash flow, and shareholder return are compelling, though the regulatory risk makes it less clean than ACN, ACGL, and ADBE.

### 5. PayPal Holdings (PYPL)

**Why it looks undervalued:** PYPL remains in the top list as a lower-confidence but statistically compelling value idea. StockAnalysis data checked July 18 shows a trailing P/E around 10.6, forward P/E around 10.5, P/FCF around 9, and EV/FCF below 10 after a more than 20% 52-week share-price decline. The valuation is low for a profitable digital payments franchise if transaction margins and engagement stabilize.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** PYPL has ROE above 25%, ROIC above 22%, interest coverage above 13x, and has generated more than $5 billion of trailing free cash flow. It also has a double-digit FCF margin and reduced share count by 7.10% year over year.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** The payments market is intensely competitive, branded checkout can lose share to wallets and platform-native solutions, and analyst consensus is Hold. The average analyst price target is below the current price, so public sell-side expectations do not currently provide much outside confirmation.

**Notable valuation signals:** FCF yield is 11.03%, earnings yield is 10.14%, dividend yield is 0.99%, buyback yield is 7.10%, and shareholder yield is 8.09%. PYPL stays fifth because the valuation and buyback support are real, but moat durability is more debated than for the top four.

### 6. Everest Group (EG)

**Why it looks undervalued:** EG remains compelling on insurance valuation metrics. StockAnalysis data checked July 18 shows a trailing P/E of 7.80, forward P/E of 7.25, P/B of 1.00, P/TBV of 0.99, and EV/EBIT of 6.77. The stock is still priced around tangible book despite double-digit returns on equity and a Buy analyst consensus.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** Everest shows ROE of 13.82%, ROIC of 11.50%, debt/equity of 0.23, debt/EBITDA of 1.38, and interest coverage of 17.17x. It also offers a 2.09% dividend yield, 3.70% buyback yield, and 5.85% shareholder yield.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** Underwriting cyclicality and catastrophe losses can distort normalized profitability. Free-cash-flow data are less clean for this insurer, the Piotroski F-score is only 4, and the 3-year revenue forecast is negative.

**Notable valuation signals:** Earnings yield is 13.28%, the stock trades slightly below tangible book value, and the 3-year EPS growth forecast is 15.11%. EG remains selected, but only sixth because free-cash-flow visibility is weaker than the non-insurance candidates and the stock is already up 15.26% over the last 52 weeks.

## High-Quality Names Considered But Not Selected

- **Comcast (CMCSA):** Still statistically cheap on free cash flow and shareholder yield, but structural cable/media pressure, negative growth expectations, consensus Hold, and leverage make it more likely to be a value-trap candidate than the selected names.
- **Bristol Myers Squibb (BMY):** Still reasonable on forward earnings and cash flow with an attractive dividend, but the stock's advance, Hold consensus, negative forecast growth, and patent/pipeline risk reduce the margin of safety.
- **Constellation Brands (STZ):** A good branded beverage franchise with strong returns and shareholder yield, but leverage and higher EV/FCF leave less valuation upside than the selected candidates.
- **Zoetis (ZTS):** A high-quality animal-health franchise with strong margins and Buy consensus, but cash-flow valuation is not cheap enough versus the top ideas.
- **Gilead Sciences (GILD):** Quality and cash generation remain solid, but the valuation is not as compelling as the selected ideas and pharma pipeline concentration still matters.
- **QUALCOMM (QCOM):** Business quality remains high, but the lower multiples may reflect semiconductor cyclicality and customer concentration rather than a clean margin of safety.
- **Microsoft (MSFT), Broadcom (AVGO), Charles Schwab (SCHW), Clorox (CLX), Devon Energy (DVN), Danaher (DHR), and Zimmer Biomet (ZBH):** These appeared in current external undervaluation lists or quality-value screens, but today's strict review found either richer cash-flow valuation, more financial complexity, weaker near-term free-cash-flow support, or more company-specific risk than the selected names.

## Conclusion

The strongest current quality-value candidates are **Accenture (ACN)**, **Arch Capital Group (ACGL)**, and **Adobe (ADBE)**. ACN screens best today because the stock's sharp decline has created an unusually low FCF and enterprise-value multiple for a high-ROIC, low-leverage services franchise. ACGL remains the most balanced insurance candidate, with excellent valuation, free cash flow, and conservative leverage. ADBE is now cheap enough on earnings and free cash flow to overcome, though not erase, the AI disruption debate.

**Cigna (CI)** remains the best healthcare services value idea in the current ranking. **PayPal (PYPL)** and **Everest Group (EG)** round out the selected list with good valuation support but clearer business-model or underwriting-cycle caveats. The main takeaway is unchanged from Friday: the best current opportunities are not the statistically cheapest stocks in the market, but quality franchises where the valuation now offers enough room for uncertainty.
