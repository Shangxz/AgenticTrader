# Value Investing Report - ISO Week 2026-29

Run date: 2026-07-13

This report is research support only, not personal financial advice. The shortlist below was built from a fresh quality-value review of US-listed public equities using valuation, free cash flow, returns on capital/equity, balance-sheet risk, business durability, and current market context.

## Market Context

The broad US market is not obviously cheap. FactSet's July 2026 earnings snapshot puts the S&P 500 at a forward 12-month P/E of about 20.5, above its 5-year and 10-year averages, with Energy and Financials showing the lowest sector forward multiples. Recent market commentary also continues to flag AI-led concentration and valuation risk, which makes this a better environment for selective stock picking than for paying up for broad index growth exposure.

Against that backdrop, today's best quality-value candidates came mostly from insurance, health care services/pharma, and consumer staples. I deliberately avoided making today's ranking depend on earlier picks or past report content; this is the first entry for the week.

## Today's Top Ideas

### 1. Arch Capital Group (ACGL)

**Why it looks undervalued:** ACGL trades at about 7.8x trailing earnings, 10.8x forward earnings, 6.0x price/free cash flow, and 6.3x EV/free cash flow. Those are unusually low multiples for a profitable specialty insurer with strong recent earnings and cash generation.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** The quality signals are strong: ROE of 21.3%, ROIC of 18.4%, operating margin of 28.9%, profit margin of 24.6%, and free cash flow margin near 29.6%. Debt/equity is only 0.11, interest coverage is high, and the company has been shrinking share count.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** Insurance earnings can be cyclical and catastrophe-sensitive. The low multiple may partly reflect investor skepticism that underwriting margins and investment income are near cyclical highs. PEG is not attractive on the screen, so future earnings growth needs to be watched.

**Notable valuation signals:** Earnings yield is about 13.7% and FCF yield about 16.7%, with a buyback yield near 3.1%. This is the cleanest blend of value, quality, and balance-sheet strength in today's screen.

### 2. Everest Group (EG)

**Why it looks undervalued:** EG trades near 7.6x trailing earnings and just under book value, with a P/B ratio around 0.96. It also appeared in a July 2026 screen of low-P/E, dividend-paying S&P 500 names with manageable debt.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** The company shows ROE of 13.8%, ROIC of 11.5%, operating margin of 14.8%, and interest coverage above 17x. Debt/equity is only 0.23, and the stock offers both a dividend and buybacks.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** As with ACGL, underwriting cyclicality and catastrophe losses can distort normalized earnings. Analyst revenue-growth forecasts look weak, so the case depends more on disciplined capital returns, underwriting quality, and valuation support than on strong top-line growth.

**Notable valuation signals:** P/B below 1.0, earnings yield around 13.7%, dividend yield about 2.2%, buyback yield about 3.7%, and shareholder yield around 5.9%.

### 3. The Cigna Group (CI)

**Why it looks undervalued:** CI trades around 12.4x trailing earnings, 9.5x forward earnings, 10.1x price/free cash flow, and 13.1x EV/free cash flow. That is a modest valuation for a large, cash-generative healthcare franchise.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** ROE is 16.3% and ROIC is 14.3%, comfortably above the company's reported WACC. Free cash flow over the last 12 months was about $7.7 billion, and shareholder returns are meaningful through dividends and repurchases.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** Healthcare benefit managers and insurers face regulatory, reimbursement, pricing, and political risk. Margins are structurally thin, so small changes in medical costs, rebates, or policy rules can move earnings materially.

**Notable valuation signals:** FCF yield is about 9.9%, earnings yield about 8.1%, dividend yield about 2.1%, buyback yield about 4.8%, and shareholder yield close to 7.0%.

### 4. Bristol Myers Squibb (BMY)

**Why it looks undervalued:** BMY trades around 16.1x trailing earnings, 9.4x forward earnings, 9.9x price/free cash flow, and 12.9x EV/free cash flow. The current price still assigns a low multiple to a large cash-flow base.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** The company generated about $11.9 billion of free cash flow over the last 12 months, with ROE of 38.7%, ROIC of 20.9%, operating margin of 31.6%, and FCF margin of 24.6%. Its gross margins and cash generation remain high quality.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** Patent cliffs, pipeline execution, acquisition integration, and leverage are the central risks. Debt/equity is high at 2.31, so this is less balance-sheet-clean than ACGL, EG, or CI. Recent external commentary is more constructive on BMY than Pfizer, but the recovery case still depends on the growth portfolio offsetting legacy-product erosion.

**Notable valuation signals:** Forward P/E below 10x, FCF yield roughly 10%, and a substantial dividend, supported by a large FCF base but requiring continued portfolio renewal.

### 5. Constellation Brands (STZ)

**Why it looks undervalued:** STZ trades around 12.8x trailing earnings, 11.3x forward earnings, 12.5x price/free cash flow, and 9.6x EV/EBITDA after a roughly 21% 52-week share-price decline.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** The company has strong brand economics in beer, attractive margins, and solid cash conversion: ROE of 23.7%, ROIC of 11.9%, operating margin of 33.6%, profit margin of 20.1%, and FCF margin of 20.3%.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** Debt is meaningful, with debt/equity around 1.23 and net debt above $10 billion. The market is likely discounting slower growth, beer-category uncertainty, and consumer spending pressure. This is a good value candidate only if brand durability and cash flow hold up.

**Notable valuation signals:** FCF yield about 8.0%, earnings yield about 8.0%, dividend yield about 3.1%, buyback yield about 3.4%, and shareholder yield above 6%.

### 6. Principal Financial Group (PFG)

**Why it looks undervalued:** PFG trades at about 16.1x trailing earnings, 11.8x forward earnings, 6.7x price/free cash flow, and 6.7x EV/free cash flow. The free-cash-flow yield screen is especially attractive.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** ROE is 13.4%, ROIC is 10.0%, debt/equity is 0.34, and free cash flow over the last 12 months was about $3.6 billion. The company also has a long dividend-growth record and ongoing repurchases.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** This is lower conviction than ACGL or CI because the stock has already risen strongly over the last year and the analyst consensus is only Hold, with the average price target below the current share price. Asset-management and retirement earnings are sensitive to markets, rates, and flows.

**Notable valuation signals:** FCF yield about 15.0%, dividend yield about 2.9%, buyback yield about 3.9%, and shareholder yield about 6.8%.

## High-Quality Names Considered But Not Selected

- **Danaher (DHR):** High-quality life-sciences franchise and a Morningstar value-list name, but the current valuation is not compelling enough for today's top shortlist: roughly 38.6x trailing earnings, 23.2x forward earnings, and 26.6x price/free cash flow, with ROIC below 7% on current reported numbers.
- **Thermo Fisher Scientific (TMO):** Durable franchise, but at roughly 29x trailing earnings and 29x price/free cash flow, the value case is thinner than the selected names.
- **Alphabet (GOOGL) and Meta Platforms (META):** Excellent business quality, but current valuation and AI-capex uncertainty make them less attractive from a strict quality-value lens today. Alphabet's multiple has expanded sharply, and Meta's FCF multiple is no longer obviously cheap.
- **Global Payments (GPN):** Cheap-looking in some screens, but high debt, recent GAAP losses, and execution risk around Worldpay make it a watchlist idea rather than a top-quality value pick today.
- **Futu Holdings (FUTU):** Very strong profitability and a low earnings multiple, but non-US operating/regulatory exposure, high short interest, and less complete FCF data reduce confidence for this US-market report.

## Conclusion

The strongest current quality-value candidates are **Arch Capital Group (ACGL)** and **Everest Group (EG)** because they combine low earnings/cash-flow or book-value multiples with strong returns and conservative leverage. **Cigna (CI)** and **Bristol Myers Squibb (BMY)** are attractive cash-flow value ideas but carry more regulatory, patent, or leverage risk. **Constellation Brands (STZ)** and **Principal Financial Group (PFG)** are credible secondary ideas, with good shareholder yield but clearer cyclical or balance-sheet caveats.
