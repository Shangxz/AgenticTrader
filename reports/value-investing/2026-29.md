# Value Investing Report - ISO Week 2026-29

Latest run date: 2026-07-15

This report is research support only, not personal financial advice. Each update starts from a fresh quality-value review of US-listed public equities. Earlier entries from the same week are preserved as a record, but they are not used to steer the current ranking.

## Market Context

The US market remains selectively priced rather than broadly cheap. FactSet's July 10, 2026 Earnings Insight shows the S&P 500 forward 12-month P/E at 20.5, above the 5-year average of 19.9 and the 10-year average of 19.0. FactSet also shows trailing 12-month P/E at 28.0, with Energy and Financials still the lowest-multiple sectors and Consumer Discretionary and Industrials the highest-multiple sectors.

Second-quarter earnings season is now underway. FactSet reported that only 4% of S&P 500 companies had reported through July 10, but 89% had beaten EPS estimates and 72% had beaten revenue estimates. July 15 market coverage points to a firmer open after bank earnings, lower inflation data, and continuing focus on whether earnings and cash flow can support elevated index valuations. That backdrop favors a strict quality-value filter: low multiples matter, but only when paired with durable cash generation, returns on capital, and tolerable balance-sheet risk.

## Current Fresh Ranking - 2026-07-15

The July 15 screen reviewed US-listed large and mid-cap candidates using valuation, cash conversion, returns on capital, balance-sheet risk, shareholder yield, business durability, and current public evidence. I also checked current external undervaluation lists from Morningstar/Business Insider and Investing.com as idea sources, then independently filtered candidates against the quality-value rules.

### 1. Arch Capital Group (ACGL)

**Why it looks undervalued:** ACGL remains the strongest quality-value blend in today's review. StockAnalysis shows a trailing P/E of 7.78, forward P/E of 10.89, P/FCF of 6.03, EV/EBITDA of 6.30, and EV/FCF of 6.34. That is a low cash-flow valuation for a specialty insurer with strong recent underwriting profitability.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** ACGL shows ROE of 21.31%, ROIC of 18.41%, operating margin of 28.94%, profit margin of 24.63%, and FCF margin of 29.63%. Balance-sheet risk appears modest for the business model: debt/equity is 0.11, debt/FCF is 0.47, and interest coverage is 38.15x. Share count is down 3.09% year over year.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** Insurance earnings can be cyclical and catastrophe-sensitive. The low multiple may partly reflect concerns that underwriting margins, reserve development, and investment income are above normalized levels. Three-year revenue growth forecasts are negative, so the thesis depends more on underwriting discipline, capital allocation, and valuation support than on growth.

**Notable valuation signals:** Earnings yield is 13.68%, FCF yield is 16.58%, and buyback yield is 3.09%. ACGL remains the top current candidate because the valuation, returns, cash generation, and leverage profile all line up.

### 2. The Cigna Group (CI)

**Why it looks undervalued:** CI still screens as a strong healthcare services value candidate: trailing P/E of 12.85, forward P/E of 9.82, P/FCF of 10.48, EV/EBITDA of 7.71, and EV/FCF of 13.48. The stock is not optically distressed, but the forward multiple and cash-flow yield are attractive for a large, recurring healthcare franchise.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** CI has ROE of 16.27% and ROIC of 14.34%. It generated $7.66 billion of trailing free cash flow, reduced share count by 4.83% year over year, and combines a 2.06% dividend yield with a 4.83% buyback yield. Debt/EBITDA is 2.31 and interest coverage is 8.33x, which is manageable but not trivial.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** Healthcare insurers and pharmacy benefit managers face regulatory, reimbursement, medical-cost, and political risk. Margins are structurally thin, so adverse policy or cost surprises can move earnings quickly.

**Notable valuation signals:** FCF yield is 9.55%, earnings yield is 7.84%, and total shareholder yield is 6.88%. CI holds the number two spot because its cash-flow profile is clearer than many low-multiple healthcare names and its earnings-growth outlook is not as impaired as the more patent-sensitive pharma candidates.

### 3. Everest Group (EG)

**Why it looks undervalued:** EG remains compelling on insurance valuation metrics: trailing P/E of 7.58, forward P/E of 7.03, PEG ratio of 0.57, P/B of 0.97, and P/TBV of 0.96. The stock is priced around book value despite double-digit returns on equity.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** Everest shows ROE of 13.82%, ROIC of 11.50%, debt/equity of 0.23, debt/EBITDA of 1.38, and interest coverage of 17.17x. It also has a 2.15% dividend yield and 3.70% buyback yield, for a shareholder yield of 5.82%.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** Underwriting cyclicality and catastrophe losses can distort normalized profitability. StockAnalysis shows negative three-year revenue growth forecasts, and the Piotroski F-score is only 4, so the quality signal is not as clean as ACGL's.

**Notable valuation signals:** Earnings yield is 13.66%, and the stock trades below tangible book. EG remains a top-three idea, but I continue to rank it below ACGL because free-cash-flow data are less clean for this insurer and the growth outlook is weaker.

### 4. Comcast (CMCSA)

**Why it looks undervalued:** CMCSA is the largest new addition to the current top list. It screens as statistically very cheap: trailing P/E of 4.53, forward P/E of 6.61, P/B of 0.94, P/FCF of 4.06, EV/EBITDA of 4.75, and EV/FCF of 8.24. The stock is down 33.72% over the last 52 weeks, which has pushed free-cash-flow and shareholder-yield metrics to unusually high levels.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** Comcast generated $20.38 billion of trailing free cash flow on $125.28 billion of revenue, with a 16.27% FCF margin. ROE is 20.92%, the Piotroski F-score is 7, and the company reduced share count by 4.90% year over year. The dividend yield is 5.69%, buyback yield is 4.90%, and total shareholder yield is 10.59%.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** This is not a clean compounder. Cable subscriber pressure, broadband competition, media disruption, heavy debt, and weak growth forecasts are real issues. Debt is $94.61 billion, debt/FCF is 4.64, interest coverage is only 4.30x, and the Altman Z-score is 1.43. The low multiple may be the market pricing structural decline rather than temporary pessimism.

**Notable valuation signals:** FCF yield is 24.60% and earnings yield is 22.69%. CMCSA earns a top-list spot because the cash-flow valuation is extremely low, but confidence is below ACGL, CI, and EG because the business-quality debate is much more contested.

### 5. Bristol Myers Squibb (BMY)

**Why it looks undervalued:** BMY continues to screen as a cash-flow value candidate. It trades at 15.95x trailing earnings, 9.30x forward earnings, 9.77x P/FCF, 8.00x EV/EBITDA, and 12.75x EV/FCF. The market is still applying a low forward multiple to a large pharma cash-flow base.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** BMY generated $7.28 billion of net income and substantial trailing free cash flow, with ROE of 38.73%, ROIC of 20.88%, and a Piotroski F-score of 7. It also offers a 4.43% dividend yield.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** Patent cliffs, pipeline execution, acquisition integration, and leverage remain central risks. Debt/equity is 2.31, debt/FCF is 3.90, and the Altman Z-score is 2.48. StockAnalysis shows negative three-year revenue and EPS growth forecasts and only a Hold analyst consensus.

**Notable valuation signals:** FCF yield is 10.24%, forward P/E is below 10x, and EV/EBITDA is 8.00. BMY remains selected, but it is a lower-confidence idea than the top three because the risks are fundamental rather than merely cyclical.

### 6. Constellation Brands (STZ)

**Why it looks undervalued:** STZ trades at 12.54x trailing earnings, 11.09x forward earnings, 12.23x P/FCF, and 9.51x EV/EBITDA after a 23.72% 52-week decline. The valuation is modest for a branded beverage franchise if beer-category share and cash conversion remain durable.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** STZ has ROE of 23.69%, ROIC of 11.93%, operating margin of 33.60%, profit margin near 20%, and a 6.50% shareholder yield. The company also has a 3.14% dividend yield and a 3.36% buyback yield.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** Debt is meaningful: total debt is $10.53 billion, debt/equity is 1.23, and debt/FCF is 5.74. Revenue and EPS growth forecasts are modest, and category pressure could make the current multiple a fair discount rather than a bargain.

**Notable valuation signals:** FCF yield is 8.18%, earnings yield is 8.13%, and analyst price-target upside is about 30%. STZ stays on the list, but it ranks below CMCSA today because the cash-flow valuation is less compelling and leverage remains a bigger constraint.

## High-Quality Names Considered But Not Selected

- **QUALCOMM (QCOM):** Excellent economics, with ROE of 36.08%, ROIC of 32.94%, debt/FCF of 1.22, Altman Z-score of 5.76, 6.66% FCF yield, and 5.71% shareholder yield. It was not selected today because 19.46x trailing earnings, 18.07x forward earnings, and 15.01x P/FCF leave a thinner valuation margin than the selected names.
- **Gilead Sciences (GILD):** Strong quality indicators: ROE of 43.36%, ROIC of 27.70%, Piotroski F-score of 8, debt/FCF of 2.17, and analyst consensus Buy. It stays on the watchlist because P/FCF of 15.78 and EV/FCF of 16.89 are reasonable but not clearly cheap enough versus BMY.
- **Devon Energy (DVN):** Morningstar highlighted Devon as undervalued and low cost in US shale, and StockAnalysis shows forward P/E of 8.13 with Strong Buy analyst consensus. It is not selected because trailing P/FCF of 20.66 and EV/FCF of 23.52 are too high for a commodity producer under this quality-value framework.
- **Microsoft (MSFT):** Morningstar views MSFT as materially undervalued versus fair value and the company has elite quality metrics, including ROIC of 27.24% and interest coverage above 50x. It is not selected because P/FCF of 39.22 and EV/FCF of 39.86 are too high for a strict value shortlist, even if the business quality is exceptional.
- **Charles Schwab (SCHW):** A good franchise and an external undervaluation candidate, but 15.28x forward earnings, 18.08x P/FCF, debt/FCF of 6.12, and financial-sector balance-sheet complexity keep it below today's top ideas.
- **Clorox (CLX):** A defensive brand with a long dividend record and 25.65% ROIC, but P/FCF of 30.15, EV/FCF of 38.82, high leverage metrics, and negative EPS growth forecasts make it too expensive for this screen.

## Changes From Earlier Updates This Week

- **ACGL remains the highest-conviction idea** after an independent July 15 review. Its valuation, profitability, cash conversion, and low leverage still form the best all-around quality-value profile.
- **CI and EG remain in the top three.** CI keeps the second spot because its free-cash-flow and shareholder-yield signals are clearer, while EG remains attractive near tangible book but has weaker growth and quality-score signals.
- **CMCSA is added to the top list.** The July 15 screen gives more weight to Comcast's very low P/FCF, high FCF yield, and double-digit shareholder yield. It is explicitly a higher-risk value idea because of leverage and structural cable/media pressure.
- **QCOM moves from the top list to watchlist.** Its business quality remains strong, but today's fresh screen found wider valuation spreads in CMCSA, BMY, and STZ.
- **BMY and STZ remain selected but caveated.** BMY is cheap on forward earnings and FCF but faces patent/pipeline risk. STZ is a branded cash-flow idea with leverage and muted growth risk.
- **PFG remains off the current list.** It was selected on July 13, but the July 14 and July 15 reviews found stronger quality-value combinations elsewhere.

## Conclusion

The strongest current quality-value candidates are **Arch Capital Group (ACGL)**, **The Cigna Group (CI)**, and **Everest Group (EG)**. ACGL remains the best overall blend of valuation, returns, free cash flow, and balance-sheet strength. CI is the best healthcare services cash-flow value. EG remains attractive near book value, though underwriting cyclicality and weaker growth signals keep it behind ACGL and CI.

**Comcast (CMCSA)** is the most notable new value candidate today: statistically cheap and highly cash-generative, but with enough leverage and structural pressure that it should be treated as higher risk. **Bristol Myers Squibb (BMY)** and **Constellation Brands (STZ)** remain credible lower-ranked value ideas with company-specific caveats.
