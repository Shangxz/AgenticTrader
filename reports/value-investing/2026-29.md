# Value Investing Report - ISO Week 2026-29

Latest run date: 2026-07-17

This report is research support only, not personal financial advice. Each update starts from a fresh quality-value review of US-listed public equities. Earlier entries from the same week are preserved as a record, but they are not used to steer the current ranking.

## Market Context

The US equity market still looks selectively priced rather than broadly cheap. FactSet's latest Earnings Insight, reviewed today, shows the S&P 500 forward 12-month P/E at 20.5, above both the 5-year average of 19.9 and the 10-year average of 19.0. That leaves less margin for broad-index value arguments and raises the hurdle for individual stock selection.

The July 17 backdrop also shows more rotation beneath the surface. MarketWatch reported that Truist upgraded financials and healthcare to attractive after underperformance versus the S&P 500, while current market coverage points to softness in some technology and AI-linked hardware names and better relative interest in non-tech sectors. That said, today's independent screen found that a few large-cap technology and services franchises have also become statistically cheap enough to re-enter the quality-value conversation.

## Current Fresh Ranking - 2026-07-17

The July 17 review started from a fresh screen across US-listed large and mid-cap equities, emphasizing valuation, free cash flow, returns on capital, balance-sheet risk, business durability, and current public evidence. External lists from Morningstar, Forbes, Investing.com, Business Insider, Barron's, and market coverage were used as idea sources only; the ranking below is based on today's quality-value evidence.

### 1. Accenture (ACN)

**Why it looks undervalued:** ACN is the strongest new quality-value candidate in today's screen. StockAnalysis data checked July 17 shows a trailing P/E of 11.57, forward P/E of 10.09, P/FCF of 7.03, EV/EBITDA of 6.70, and EV/FCF of 6.89 after a 47.62% 52-week share-price decline. That is unusually low for a global consulting and technology-services franchise if current margins and cash conversion prove durable.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** ACN has ROE of 24.41%, ROIC of 27.09%, interest coverage of 43.37x, debt/FCF of 0.67, and an Altman Z-score of 4.79. Free cash flow was $12.59 billion over the trailing 12 months, FCF margin was 17.22%, and share count fell 1.53% year over year.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** The market may be discounting real pressure from slower discretionary consulting demand, AI-driven delivery disruption, pricing pressure, and the difficulty of managing a very large labor base. Piotroski F-score is only 5, so the quality signal is strong but not flawless.

**Notable valuation signals:** FCF yield is 14.22%, earnings yield is 8.64%, shareholder yield is 4.20%, and analyst consensus remains Buy. The combination of low cash-flow valuation, high ROIC, modest leverage, and positive 3-year revenue/EPS growth forecasts makes ACN today's top current candidate.

### 2. Arch Capital Group (ACGL)

**Why it looks undervalued:** ACGL remains one of the cleanest quality-value names in the screen. StockAnalysis data checked July 17 shows a trailing P/E of 7.67, forward P/E of 10.73, P/FCF of 5.94, EV/EBITDA of 6.21, and EV/FCF of 6.25. The stock is no longer down over 52 weeks, but the cash-flow valuation is still low for its recent underwriting profitability.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** ACGL shows ROE of 21.31%, ROIC of 18.41%, operating margin of 28.94%, profit margin of 24.63%, and FCF margin of 29.63%. Balance-sheet risk looks modest: debt/equity is 0.11, debt/FCF is 0.47, and interest coverage is 38.15x. Share count is down 3.09% year over year.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** Insurance earnings are cyclical and catastrophe-sensitive. The low multiple may partly reflect concerns that underwriting margins, reserve development, or investment income are above normalized levels. The 3-year revenue forecast is negative, so this thesis depends more on discipline, capital allocation, and valuation support than on growth.

**Notable valuation signals:** FCF yield is 16.83%, earnings yield is 13.88%, and buyback yield is 3.09%. ACGL moves to second only because ACN's valuation-quality spread now looks unusually compelling with a more explicit growth forecast.

### 3. Adobe (ADBE)

**Why it looks undervalued:** ADBE is another major new addition. StockAnalysis data checked July 17 shows a trailing P/E of 13.46, forward P/E of 9.07, P/FCF of 9.10, EV/EBITDA of 9.76, and EV/FCF of 9.24. For a software business with very high margins and high returns on invested capital, those multiples look materially discounted if generative AI risk is manageable.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** ADBE has ROE of 62.95%, ROIC of 60.74%, gross margin of 89.40%, operating margin of 36.72%, profit margin of 28.69%, and FCF margin of 40.80%. Debt risk appears low: debt/FCF is 0.69, interest coverage is 35.45x, Altman Z-score is 8.35, and Piotroski F-score is 7.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** The central risk is competitive and technological disruption from AI-native creative tools, plus the possibility that customers push back on pricing or bundled subscription economics. Analyst consensus is Hold despite the low forward multiple, which signals uncertainty around the durability of growth assumptions.

**Notable valuation signals:** FCF yield is 10.99%, buyback yield is 5.84%, and analyst forecasts show 3-year revenue growth of 9.71% and EPS growth of 14.53%. ADBE ranks ahead of several classic value names because quality is exceptional and the valuation is no longer demanding.

### 4. The Cigna Group (CI)

**Why it looks undervalued:** CI remains a strong healthcare services value candidate. StockAnalysis data checked July 17 shows a trailing P/E of 12.03, forward P/E of 9.19, P/FCF of 9.80, EV/EBITDA of 7.33, and EV/FCF of 12.81. The stock is not distressed, but the forward earnings and cash-flow multiples are attractive for a large recurring healthcare franchise.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** CI has ROE of 16.27%, ROIC of 14.34%, $7.66 billion of trailing free cash flow, and a 6.92% shareholder yield from dividends plus buybacks. Share count is down 4.83% year over year. Leverage is meaningful but manageable: debt/EBITDA is 2.31 and interest coverage is 8.33x.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** Healthcare insurers and pharmacy benefit managers face regulatory, reimbursement, medical-cost, and political risk. Margins are structurally thin, so adverse policy or cost surprises can move earnings quickly.

**Notable valuation signals:** FCF yield is 10.20%, earnings yield is 8.38%, analyst consensus is Buy, and the 3-year EPS growth forecast is 7.44%. CI remains one of the better healthcare quality-value ideas, but it ranks below ACN, ACGL, and ADBE because the margin structure and regulatory risk are less forgiving.

### 5. PayPal Holdings (PYPL)

**Why it looks undervalued:** PYPL enters the current top list after today's broader review. StockAnalysis data checked July 17 shows a trailing P/E of 10.63, forward P/E of 10.46, P/FCF of 9.09, EV/EBITDA of 7.97, and EV/FCF of 9.52 after a 22.25% 52-week share-price decline. The valuation is low for a profitable digital payments franchise if transaction margins and engagement stabilize.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** PYPL has ROE of 25.12%, ROIC of 22.94%, debt/FCF of 2.12, and interest coverage of 13.66x. It generated $5.50 billion of trailing free cash flow, has a 16.31% FCF margin, and reduced share count by 7.10% year over year.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** The payments market is intensely competitive, branded checkout can lose share to wallets and platform-native solutions, and the stock's analyst price target sits below the current price. The Altman Z-score of 2.03 and Piotroski F-score of 6 are adequate but not top-tier.

**Notable valuation signals:** FCF yield is 11.00%, earnings yield is 10.11%, and shareholder yield is 8.09%. PYPL is included because the valuation, buyback, and returns on capital are compelling, but confidence is lower than in ACN and ADBE because the moat debate is more unsettled.

### 6. Everest Group (EG)

**Why it looks undervalued:** EG remains compelling on insurance valuation metrics: trailing P/E of 7.60, forward P/E of 7.07, P/B of 0.97, and P/TBV of 0.96. The stock is still priced around tangible book despite double-digit returns on equity and a Buy analyst consensus.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** Everest shows ROE of 13.82%, ROIC of 11.50%, debt/equity of 0.23, debt/EBIT of 1.40, and interest coverage of 17.17x. It also offers a 2.14% dividend yield, 3.70% buyback yield, and 5.90% shareholder yield.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** Underwriting cyclicality and catastrophe losses can distort normalized profitability. Free-cash-flow data are less clean for this insurer, the Piotroski F-score is only 4, and the 3-year revenue forecast is negative.

**Notable valuation signals:** Earnings yield is 13.61% and the stock trades slightly below tangible book value. EG remains selected, but it falls to sixth because today's screen found several cash-flow-supported quality ideas with cleaner non-insurance metrics.

## High-Quality Names Considered But Not Selected

- **Comcast (CMCSA):** Still statistically very cheap, with P/FCF of 4.22, EV/FCF of 8.48, FCF yield of 23.68%, dividend yield of 5.48%, and shareholder yield of 10.37%. It is moved out of the top list today because structural cable/media pressure, negative 3-year revenue and EPS forecasts, consensus Hold, and debt/FCF of 4.64 make it more likely to be a value-trap candidate than the new ACN/ADBE/PYPL additions.
- **Bristol Myers Squibb (BMY):** Still reasonable at 9.89x forward earnings and 10.38x P/FCF, with strong ROIC and a 4.17% dividend yield. It is not selected today because the stock is up 30.33% over 52 weeks, analyst consensus is Hold, 3-year revenue and EPS forecasts are negative, and patent/pipeline risk remains central.
- **Constellation Brands (STZ):** A good branded beverage franchise with 23.69% ROE, 11.93% ROIC, 20.25% FCF margin, and 6.40% shareholder yield. It falls below the top list because EV/FCF is 18.33, debt/FCF is 5.74, and the valuation is less compelling than the higher-return technology/services candidates now available.
- **Zoetis (ZTS):** A high-quality animal-health franchise with strong margins, Buy consensus, and estimated price-target upside, but P/FCF and EV/FCF are less attractive than the selected names and debt remains meaningful.
- **Gilead Sciences (GILD):** Quality is strong, including 34.40% FCF margin and 6.05% FCF yield, but valuation is not cheap enough versus selected candidates and pharma pipeline concentration still matters.
- **QUALCOMM (QCOM):** Business quality remains high, with 32.94% ROIC and 28.10% FCF margin, but P/FCF of 14.38 and Hold consensus leave less margin of safety.
- **Microsoft (MSFT), Broadcom (AVGO), Charles Schwab (SCHW), Clorox (CLX), Devon Energy (DVN), Danaher (DHR), and Zimmer Biomet (ZBH):** These appeared in current external undervaluation lists or quality-value screens, but today's strict review found either richer cash-flow valuation, more financial complexity, weaker near-term free-cash-flow support, or more company-specific risk than the selected names.

## Conclusion

The strongest current quality-value candidates are **Accenture (ACN)**, **Arch Capital Group (ACGL)**, and **Adobe (ADBE)**. ACN screens best today because the stock's sharp decline has created an unusually low FCF and enterprise-value multiple for a high-ROIC, low-leverage services franchise. ACGL remains the most balanced insurance candidate, with excellent valuation, free cash flow, and leverage metrics. ADBE is now cheap enough on earnings and free cash flow to overcome, though not erase, the AI disruption debate.

**Cigna (CI)** remains the best healthcare services value idea in the current ranking. **PayPal (PYPL)** and **Everest Group (EG)** round out the selected list with good valuation support but clearer business-model or underwriting-cycle caveats. CMCSA, BMY, and STZ remain worth monitoring, but today's stronger quality-value evidence sits elsewhere.
