# Value Investing Report - ISO Week 2026-29

Latest run date: 2026-07-19

This report is research support only, not personal financial advice. Each update starts from a fresh quality-value review of US-listed public equities. Earlier entries from the same week are preserved as a record, but they are not used to steer the current ranking.

## Market Context

The US equity market remains selectively priced rather than broadly cheap. FactSet's latest Earnings Insight, reviewed July 19, shows the S&P 500 forward 12-month P/E at 20.3, above both the 5-year average of 19.9 and the 10-year average of 19.0. That keeps the index-level margin of safety thin and makes business quality especially important when looking for value.

Market action into the July 17 close supports a stock-specific approach. AP reported that the S&P 500 fell 1.0% on July 17, the Nasdaq fell 1.4%, and the week produced losses of 1.6% for the S&P 500 and 2.9% for the Nasdaq, with AI and semiconductor concerns driving much of the pressure. MarketWatch also noted that the average S&P 500 stock was holding up better than the cap-weighted index, while the PHLX Semiconductor Index moved into bear-market territory. The practical implication for this screen is that valuation opportunities are appearing in several places, but the best candidates still need durable cash flow and balance-sheet support rather than just a lower share price.

## Current Fresh Ranking - 2026-07-19

The July 19 review started from a fresh screen across US-listed large and mid-cap equities, emphasizing valuation, free cash flow, returns on capital, balance-sheet risk, business durability, and current public evidence. Because today is Sunday, prices below generally reflect the July 17 market close, while StockAnalysis valuation and statistics pages were last checked July 19.

### 1. Accenture (ACN)

**Why it looks undervalued:** ACN remains the strongest current quality-value candidate. StockAnalysis data checked July 19 shows a trailing P/E of 11.48, forward P/E of 10.01, P/FCF of 6.98, EV/EBITDA of 6.65, and EV/FCF of 6.84 after a 48.66% 52-week share-price decline. That is an unusually low cash-flow and enterprise-value valuation for a global consulting and technology-services franchise if client demand and cash conversion remain resilient.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** ACN has ROE of 24.41%, ROIC of 27.09%, interest coverage of 43.37x, debt/FCF of 0.67, and an Altman Z-score of 4.79. It generated $12.58 billion of trailing free cash flow, has a 17.21% FCF margin, holds a small net cash position, and reduced share count by 1.53% year over year.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** The low multiple may be discounting more than temporary softness. Key risks include slower discretionary consulting demand, AI-driven delivery disruption, pricing pressure, execution risk from a very large labor base, and only middling Piotroski F-score support at 5.

**Notable valuation signals:** FCF yield is 14.32%, earnings yield is 8.87%, dividend yield is 4.54%, shareholder yield is 6.07%, and analyst consensus remains Buy with an average price target 24.77% above the current price. ACN keeps the top rank because the cash-flow yield, ROIC, low leverage, dividend support, and still-positive 3-year revenue and EPS forecasts make the valuation gap unusually compelling.

### 2. Arch Capital Group (ACGL)

**Why it looks undervalued:** ACGL remains one of the cleanest quality-value names in the current screen. StockAnalysis data checked July 19 shows a trailing P/E of 7.77, forward P/E of 10.87, P/FCF of 6.02, EV/EBITDA of 6.29, and EV/FCF of 6.33. The stock is up 13.79% over the last 52 weeks, but the free-cash-flow and earnings valuation remain low for its recent underwriting profitability.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** ACGL shows ROE of 21.31%, ROIC of 18.41%, operating margin of 28.94%, profit margin of 24.63%, and FCF margin of 29.63%. Balance-sheet risk looks modest, with debt/equity of 0.11, debt/FCF of 0.47, and interest coverage of 38.15x. Share count is down 3.09% year over year.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** Insurance earnings are cyclical and catastrophe-sensitive. The low multiple may partly reflect concern that underwriting margins, reserve development, or investment income are above normalized levels. The 3-year revenue forecast is negative, and the next earnings report is scheduled for July 28, so near-term confirmation matters.

**Notable valuation signals:** FCF yield is 16.61%, earnings yield is 13.70%, buyback yield is 3.09%, and analyst consensus remains Buy. ACGL stays second because it is exceptionally balanced, but ACN still offers more fallen-franchise upside and a cleaner non-insurance cash-flow profile today.

### 3. Adobe (ADBE)

**Why it looks undervalued:** ADBE remains a strong fallen-quality candidate. StockAnalysis data checked July 19 shows a trailing P/E of 13.57, forward P/E of 9.15, P/FCF of 9.17, EV/EBITDA of 9.84, and EV/FCF of 9.31 after a 34.42% 52-week share-price decline. For a software company with very high margins and high returns on invested capital, those multiples look materially discounted if generative AI risk is manageable.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** ADBE has ROE of 62.95%, ROIC of 60.74%, gross margin of 89.40%, operating margin of 36.72%, profit margin of 28.69%, and FCF margin of 40.80%. It generated $10.28 billion of trailing free cash flow, has interest coverage of 35.45x, and reduced share count by 5.84% year over year.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** The core uncertainty is competitive and technological disruption from AI-native creative tools, plus the possibility that customers resist subscription pricing or bundled economics. Analyst consensus is Hold rather than Buy, so public sell-side expectations remain cautious.

**Notable valuation signals:** FCF yield is 10.90%, buyback yield is 5.84%, and analyst forecasts show 3-year revenue growth of 9.71% and EPS growth of 14.53%. ADBE ranks ahead of several classic value names because business quality is exceptional and the valuation now gives some room for uncertainty.

### 4. The Cigna Group (CI)

**Why it looks undervalued:** CI remains the leading healthcare services value candidate in the current screen. StockAnalysis data checked July 19 shows a trailing P/E of 11.93, forward P/E of 9.11, P/FCF of 9.72, EV/EBITDA of 7.28, and EV/FCF of 12.73. The stock is down 7.97% over the last 52 weeks, and the forward earnings and cash-flow multiples look attractive for a large recurring healthcare franchise.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** CI has ROE of 16.27%, ROIC of 14.34%, $7.66 billion of trailing free cash flow, and a 7.05% shareholder yield from dividends plus buybacks. Share count is down 4.83% year over year. Leverage is meaningful but manageable for the business, with debt/EBITDA of 2.31x and interest coverage of 8.33x.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** Healthcare insurers and pharmacy benefit managers face regulatory, reimbursement, medical-cost, and political risk. Margins are structurally thin, so adverse policy or cost surprises can move earnings quickly. The next confirmed earnings date is July 30, making near-term medical-cost commentary important.

**Notable valuation signals:** FCF yield is 10.29%, earnings yield is 8.45%, dividend yield is 2.22%, analyst consensus is Buy, and the average price target is 21.13% above the current price. CI remains selected because the valuation, cash flow, and shareholder return are compelling, though regulatory risk makes it less clean than the top three.

### 5. PayPal Holdings (PYPL)

**Why it looks undervalued:** PYPL remains a lower-confidence but statistically compelling value idea. StockAnalysis data checked July 19 shows a trailing P/E of 10.60, forward P/E of 10.44, P/FCF of 9.07, EV/EBITDA of 7.95, and EV/FCF of 9.49 after a 22.49% 52-week share-price decline. The valuation is low for a profitable digital payments franchise if transaction margins, branded checkout relevance, and engagement stabilize.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** PYPL has ROE of 25.12%, ROIC of 22.94%, interest coverage of 13.66x, and $5.50 billion of trailing free cash flow. It also has a 16.31% FCF margin and reduced share count by 7.10% year over year.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** The payments market is intensely competitive, branded checkout can lose share to wallets and platform-native solutions, and analyst consensus is Hold. The average analyst price target is below the current price. The next confirmed earnings date is July 28, and the stock's RSI above 80 suggests the recent move has already priced in some near-term optimism.

**Notable valuation signals:** FCF yield is 11.03%, earnings yield is 10.14%, dividend yield is 0.99%, buyback yield is 7.10%, and shareholder yield is 8.09%. PYPL stays fifth because the valuation and buyback support are real, but moat durability is more debated than for the top four.

### 6. Everest Group (EG)

**Why it looks undervalued:** EG remains compelling on insurance valuation metrics. StockAnalysis data checked July 19 shows a trailing P/E of 7.80, forward P/E of 7.25, P/B of 1.00, P/TBV of 0.99, and EV/EBIT of 6.77. The stock is still priced around tangible book despite double-digit returns on equity and a Buy analyst consensus.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** Everest shows ROE of 13.82%, ROIC of 11.50%, debt/equity of 0.23, debt/EBITDA of 1.38, and interest coverage of 17.17x. It also offers a 2.09% dividend yield, 3.70% buyback yield, and 5.79% shareholder yield.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** Underwriting cyclicality and catastrophe losses can distort normalized profitability. Free-cash-flow data are less clean for this insurer, the Piotroski F-score is only 4, and the 3-year revenue forecast is negative. The next confirmed earnings date is July 29, so reserve, pricing, and catastrophe-loss commentary may quickly change the thesis.

**Notable valuation signals:** Earnings yield is 13.28%, the stock trades slightly below tangible book value, and the 3-year EPS growth forecast is 15.11%. EG remains selected, but only sixth because free-cash-flow visibility is weaker than the non-insurance candidates and the stock is already up 15.26% over the last 52 weeks.

## Conclusion

The strongest current quality-value candidates are **Accenture (ACN)**, **Arch Capital Group (ACGL)**, and **Adobe (ADBE)**. ACN screens best today because the stock's sharp decline has created an unusually low FCF and enterprise-value multiple for a high-ROIC, low-leverage services franchise. ACGL remains the most balanced insurance candidate, with excellent valuation, free cash flow, and conservative leverage. ADBE is now cheap enough on earnings and free cash flow to overcome, though not erase, the AI disruption debate.

**Cigna (CI)** remains the best healthcare services value idea in the current ranking. **PayPal (PYPL)** and **Everest Group (EG)** round out the selected list with good valuation support but clearer business-model or underwriting-cycle caveats. The main takeaway is unchanged from Saturday: the best current opportunities are not the statistically cheapest stocks in the market, but quality franchises where the valuation offers enough room for uncertainty.
