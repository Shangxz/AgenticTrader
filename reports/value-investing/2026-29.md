# Value Investing Report - ISO Week 2026-29

Latest run date: 2026-07-16

This report is research support only, not personal financial advice. Each update starts from a fresh quality-value review of US-listed public equities. Earlier entries from the same week are preserved as a record, but they are not used to steer the current ranking.

## Market Context

The US market still looks selectively priced rather than broadly cheap. FactSet's July 10, 2026 Earnings Insight shows the S&P 500 forward 12-month P/E at 20.5, above the 5-year average of 19.9 and the 10-year average of 19.0. It also shows trailing 12-month P/E at 28.0, with Energy and Financials still the lowest-multiple sectors and Consumer Discretionary and Industrials the highest-multiple sectors.

The July 16 backdrop remains earnings-driven. Recent market coverage points to stocks holding near highs after cooler inflation data, strong bank earnings, and a continued shift in attention toward whether large technology and AI-related earnings can justify elevated index multiples. That makes the quality-value bar stricter: low multiples are useful only when paired with resilient cash generation, adequate returns on capital, and balance-sheet risk that looks tolerable under less favorable conditions.

## Current Fresh Ranking - 2026-07-16

The July 16 screen reviewed US-listed large and mid-cap candidates using valuation, free cash flow, returns on capital, leverage, shareholder yield, business durability, analyst forecast risk, and current public evidence. External undervaluation lists from Morningstar/Business Insider, Morningstar, Forbes, Yahoo Finance, and Investing.com were used only as idea sources; today's ranking was formed independently from current quality-value evidence.

### 1. Arch Capital Group (ACGL)

**Why it looks undervalued:** ACGL remains the strongest quality-value blend in today's independent review. StockAnalysis data checked July 16 shows a trailing P/E of 7.55, forward P/E of 10.57, P/FCF of 5.85, EV/EBITDA of 6.12, and EV/FCF of 6.16. That is still a low cash-flow valuation for a specialty insurer with strong recent underwriting profitability.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** ACGL shows ROE of 21.31%, ROIC of 18.41%, operating margin of 28.94%, profit margin of 24.63%, and FCF margin of 29.63%. Balance-sheet risk appears modest for the business model: debt/equity is 0.11, debt/FCF is 0.47, and interest coverage is 38.15x. Share count is down 3.09% year over year.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** Insurance earnings can be cyclical and catastrophe-sensitive. The low multiple may partly reflect concern that underwriting margins, reserve development, and investment income are above normalized levels. Three-year revenue growth forecasts remain negative, so the thesis depends more on underwriting discipline, capital allocation, and valuation support than on growth.

**Notable valuation signals:** Earnings yield is 14.09%, FCF yield is 17.09%, and buyback yield is 3.09%. ACGL remains the top current candidate because valuation, returns, cash generation, and leverage all point in the same direction.

### 2. The Cigna Group (CI)

**Why it looks undervalued:** CI again screens as a strong healthcare services value candidate: trailing P/E of 12.62, forward P/E of 9.64, P/FCF of 10.28, EV/EBITDA of 7.60, and EV/FCF of 13.29. The stock is not optically distressed, but the forward multiple and cash-flow yield are attractive for a large recurring healthcare franchise.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** CI has ROE of 16.27% and ROIC of 14.34%. It generated $7.66 billion of trailing free cash flow, reduced share count by 4.83% year over year, and combines a 2.10% dividend yield with a 4.83% buyback yield. Debt/EBITDA is 2.31 and interest coverage is 8.33x, which is manageable but not trivial.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** Healthcare insurers and pharmacy benefit managers face regulatory, reimbursement, medical-cost, and political risk. Margins are structurally thin, so adverse policy or cost surprises can move earnings quickly.

**Notable valuation signals:** FCF yield is 9.72%, earnings yield is 7.98%, and total shareholder yield is 6.89%. CI keeps the number two spot because the cash-flow profile is clearer than many low-multiple healthcare names and the growth outlook is less impaired than patent-sensitive pharma candidates.

### 3. Everest Group (EG)

**Why it looks undervalued:** EG remains compelling on insurance valuation metrics: trailing P/E of 7.42, forward P/E of 6.90, PEG ratio of 0.57, P/B of 0.95, and P/TBV of 0.94. The stock is priced below tangible book despite double-digit returns on equity.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** Everest shows ROE of 13.82%, ROIC of 11.50%, debt/equity of 0.23, debt/EBITDA of 1.38, and interest coverage of 17.17x. It also has a 2.20% dividend yield and 3.70% buyback yield, for a shareholder yield of 5.85%.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** Underwriting cyclicality and catastrophe losses can distort normalized profitability. Free-cash-flow data are less clean for this insurer, revenue growth forecasts are negative, and the quality signal is not as broad as ACGL's.

**Notable valuation signals:** Earnings yield is 13.95%, the stock trades below tangible book, and analyst consensus remains Buy. EG remains a top-three idea, but I continue to rank it below ACGL because ACGL's free-cash-flow and leverage evidence is cleaner.

### 4. Comcast (CMCSA)

**Why it looks undervalued:** CMCSA remains the most statistically cheap large-cap candidate in the current top list. StockAnalysis shows trailing P/E of 4.58, forward P/E of 6.69, P/B of 0.95, P/FCF of 4.12, EV/EBITDA of 4.78, and EV/FCF of 8.29. The stock is down 34.09% over the last 52 weeks, which has pushed free-cash-flow and shareholder-yield metrics to unusually high levels.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** Comcast generated $20.38 billion of trailing free cash flow on $125.28 billion of revenue, with a 16.27% FCF margin. ROE is 20.92%, the company reduced share count by 4.90% year over year, and the dividend plus buyback yield is 10.51%.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** This is a higher-risk value idea, not a clean compounder. Cable subscriber pressure, broadband competition, media disruption, heavy debt, and weak growth forecasts are real issues. Debt is $94.61 billion, debt/FCF is 4.64, interest coverage is 4.30x, and both revenue and EPS forecasts are negative over three years. The low multiple may reflect structural decline rather than temporary pessimism.

**Notable valuation signals:** FCF yield is 24.29% and earnings yield is 22.40%. CMCSA ranks fourth because the valuation spread is extreme, but confidence is materially lower than ACGL, CI, and EG because the business-quality debate is much more contested.

### 5. Bristol Myers Squibb (BMY)

**Why it looks undervalued:** BMY continues to screen as a cash-flow value candidate. It trades at 16.44x trailing earnings, 9.60x forward earnings, 10.07x P/FCF, 8.19x EV/EBITDA, and 13.06x EV/FCF. The market is still applying a low forward multiple to a large pharma cash-flow base.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** BMY generated $11.91 billion of trailing free cash flow and has ROE of 38.73%, ROIC of 20.88%, operating margin of 31.56%, FCF margin of 24.56%, and a Piotroski F-score of 7. It also offers a 4.29% dividend yield.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** Patent cliffs, pipeline execution, acquisition integration, and leverage remain central risks. Debt/equity is 2.31, debt/FCF is 3.90, and the Altman Z-score is 2.48. StockAnalysis shows negative three-year revenue and EPS growth forecasts and only a Hold analyst consensus.

**Notable valuation signals:** FCF yield is 9.93%, forward P/E is below 10x, and EV/EBITDA is 8.19. BMY remains selected, but it is a lower-confidence idea than the top three because the risks are fundamental rather than merely cyclical.

### 6. Constellation Brands (STZ)

**Why it looks undervalued:** STZ trades at 12.57x trailing earnings, 11.12x forward earnings, 12.25x P/FCF, and 9.52x EV/EBITDA after a 20.01% 52-week decline. The valuation is modest for a branded beverage franchise if beer-category share and cash conversion remain durable.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** STZ has ROE of 23.69%, ROIC of 11.93%, operating margin of 33.60%, profit margin of 20.14%, and FCF margin of 20.25%. The company also has a 3.14% dividend yield, 3.36% buyback yield, and 6.49% shareholder yield.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** Debt is meaningful: total debt is $10.53 billion, debt/equity is 1.23, and debt/FCF is 5.74. Revenue and EPS growth forecasts are modest, and category pressure could make the current multiple a fair discount rather than a bargain.

**Notable valuation signals:** FCF yield is 8.16%, earnings yield is 8.12%, and analyst price-target upside is about 30%. STZ stays on the list, but below BMY today because the cash-flow valuation is less compelling and leverage remains a constraint.

## High-Quality Names Considered But Not Selected

- **Gilead Sciences (GILD):** Strong quality indicators, including ROE of 43.36%, ROIC of 27.70%, FCF margin of 34.40%, Piotroski F-score of 8, Altman Z-score of 4.51, and consensus Buy. It remains on watchlist because P/FCF of 15.98 and EV/FCF of 17.10 are reasonable but not cheap enough versus the selected names.
- **QUALCOMM (QCOM):** Excellent economics, with ROE of 36.08%, ROIC of 32.94%, debt/FCF of 1.22, Altman Z-score of 5.76, 6.66% FCF yield, and 5.71% shareholder yield. It was not selected today because 19.44x trailing earnings, 18.05x forward earnings, and 15.00x P/FCF leave a thinner valuation margin than the selected names.
- **Kroger (KR):** A newly reviewed watchlist candidate after a sharp 52-week decline. Forward P/E is 10.76, P/FCF is 12.24, ROIC is 14.44%, Piotroski F-score is 7, and shareholder yield is 11.21%. It stays below the top list because trailing earnings quality is weak, margins are very thin, debt/FCF is 8.54, and EV/FCF is 19.76.
- **CVS Health (CVS):** Forward P/E of 13.86 and Buy consensus are not enough to overcome low ROE of 3.75%, ROIC of 6.30%, debt/FCF of 10.60, weak margins, and only 5.47% FCF yield after a large stock rebound.
- **Cisco Systems (CSCO):** The business is durable and cash-generative, but the stock no longer fits a strict value screen after a large 52-week gain. P/FCF is 37.37 and EV/FCF is 38.76.
- **Diamondback Energy (FANG):** Forward P/E is low and analyst consensus is Strong Buy, but current free cash flow is negative, ROIC is negative, share count is up sharply year over year, and the Piotroski F-score is only 3.
- **Microsoft (MSFT), Broadcom (AVGO), Charles Schwab (SCHW), Clorox (CLX), and Devon Energy (DVN):** These remain external undervaluation candidates from recent research lists, but the current strict quality-value screen found either richer cash-flow valuation, more financial complexity, weaker free-cash-flow support, or more company-specific risk than the selected names.

## Conclusion

The strongest current quality-value candidates are **Arch Capital Group (ACGL)**, **The Cigna Group (CI)**, and **Everest Group (EG)**. ACGL remains the best overall blend of valuation, returns, free cash flow, and balance-sheet strength. CI is the clearest healthcare services cash-flow value. EG remains attractive near and below tangible book, though underwriting cyclicality and less complete cash-flow evidence keep it behind ACGL and CI.

**Comcast (CMCSA)** remains the most notable higher-risk value candidate: statistically very cheap and highly cash-generative, but with enough leverage and structural pressure that position sizing and disconfirmation monitoring would matter. **Bristol Myers Squibb (BMY)** and **Constellation Brands (STZ)** remain credible lower-ranked value ideas with specific risks that need to be watched.
