# Value Investing Weekly Report - 2026-W26

Run date: 2026-06-22  
Scope: US-listed public equities  
Purpose: Research support only; not personal financial advice.

## Short Market Context

The broad US market still looks expensive on a headline basis. MacroMicro's S&P 500 forward P/E series showed about 22.5x as of 2026-06-18, while recent FactSet earnings commentary pointed to strong expected Q2 earnings growth. That combination leaves less margin of safety in the index and makes single-stock selection more important than simply buying broad market beta.

For this run, I screened across large and mid-cap US-listed companies with a bias toward durable free cash flow, high returns on capital, balance-sheet resilience, and valuation support from earnings, free cash flow, or book value. I treated high-quality but very high-multiple compounders cautiously, and I penalized cheap companies where the discount appears driven by severe structural decline.

## Today's Top Ideas

### 1. Arch Capital Group (ACGL)

**Why it looks undervalued:** ACGL stands out on classic value metrics. The stock traded around $91.18 on 2026-06-22 with a trailing P/E near 7.0, and other public data providers recently showed forward P/E around the high single digits. That is a low multiple for a specialty insurer with a long record of disciplined underwriting and book-value growth.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** Arch's Q1 2026 results showed profitable underwriting across the group, with the reinsurance segment posting a 75.9% combined ratio and favorable prior-year reserve development. Third-party summaries also note strong recent book-value-per-share growth and robust operating returns on equity.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** Insurance profitability is cyclical. Reinsurance pricing can soften, catastrophe losses can be volatile, and reserve releases should not be treated as permanent earnings power. The stock is also not as obviously cheap on normalized earnings if unusually favorable loss experience reverses.

**Notable valuation signals:** Low trailing P/E, forward P/E below the market, and quality evidence from underwriting performance make ACGL the strongest quality-value candidate in this run.

### 2. Meta Platforms (META)

**Why it looks undervalued:** META traded around $577.22 on 2026-06-22, with a trailing P/E around 21.0, slightly below the broad market's forward multiple despite stronger profitability than the average S&P 500 company. StockAnalysis data shows $48.25 billion of trailing free cash flow after very heavy capex.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** The core advertising business remains a rare scaled digital platform with high margins, strong cash generation, and global distribution. Even after elevated AI infrastructure spending, the company continues to produce very large absolute free cash flow.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** The market is correctly focused on AI capex and Reality Labs-style investment discipline. If returns on the new infrastructure spending disappoint, today's apparent value could be less compelling. Regulatory risk remains meaningful.

**Notable valuation signals:** A near-market earnings multiple for an unusually profitable platform creates a credible value case, but the idea depends on capex producing acceptable returns.

### 3. Qualcomm (QCOM)

**Why it looks undervalued:** QCOM traded around $226.11 on 2026-06-22, with a trailing P/E around 24.3 and Yahoo showing a forward P/E near 21.1 as of 2026-06-18. StockAnalysis reports about $12.50 billion in trailing free cash flow, and Morningstar's quote page shows strong profitability metrics, including normalized ROIC above 30%.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** Qualcomm's licensing business and modem/application processor technology give it a durable IP and engineering base. The company has high returns on capital, strong free cash generation, and optionality in automotive, edge AI, and connected devices.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** Handset cyclicality, customer concentration, Apple-related modem risk, and China exposure remain important. The stock is not statistically cheap on trailing earnings today; the value case is stronger on free cash flow quality and durability than on a bargain-basement multiple.

**Notable valuation signals:** QCOM is less cheap than ACGL or CI, but its ROIC and free-cash-flow profile keep it high on a quality-value list.

### 4. The Cigna Group (CI)

**Why it looks undervalued:** CI traded around $279.27 on 2026-06-18/22 data, with a trailing P/E around 11.8. Q1 2026 coverage indicates adjusted EPS of $7.79, revenue of $68.5 billion, and management guidance for at least $30.35 in full-year adjusted EPS, implying a high single-digit to low double-digit multiple on guided earnings.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** Cigna has scale in health benefits and pharmacy services, recurring customer relationships, and meaningful cash generation. The low multiple is attached to a business with real earnings power rather than a balance-sheet distress case.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** PBM scrutiny, healthcare policy risk, reimbursement pressure, and political headline risk are material. The discount may persist if investors assign a permanently lower multiple to managed-care and pharmacy-benefit earnings.

**Notable valuation signals:** CI offers one of the clearest earnings-yield cases in the screen, but it carries above-average regulatory risk.

### 5. Berkshire Hathaway (BRK.B)

**Why it looks undervalued:** BRK.B traded around $489.46 on 2026-06-22 with a trailing P/E around 14.3. StockAnalysis shows Berkshire holding roughly $397 billion in cash against about $129 billion in debt, for net cash around $268.5 billion, and book value per B share around $337. Morningstar notes Berkshire's long-term record of book-value compounding, while valuation services recently described the shares as near fair value to modestly undervalued.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** Berkshire remains a collection of cash-generative operating businesses, insurance float, marketable securities, and a fortress balance sheet. Its cash position gives it optionality when markets become less forgiving.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** Berkshire's size reduces the odds of large excess returns, and leadership transition from Warren Buffett to Greg Abel changes the market's perception of capital allocation. The stock is not deeply discounted versus book value; this is a conservative compounder at a reasonable price, not a classic deep-value setup.

**Notable valuation signals:** Strong balance sheet plus reasonable earnings multiple makes BRK.B a lower-upside but lower-fragility quality-value candidate.

### 6. HCA Healthcare (HCA)

**Why it looks undervalued:** HCA traded around $375.17 on 2026-06-22 with a trailing P/E around 12.9. The stock has fallen sharply from its March 2026 high; MarketWatch noted on 2026-06-18 that HCA was about 32.6% below its 52-week high. GuruFocus and other valuation summaries show moderate multiples on cash flow and EBITDA.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** HCA is a scaled hospital operator with resilient demand, significant operating cash flow, and a long history of using scale and capital allocation to compound per-share value.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** The recent decline followed earnings and guidance concerns. Labor costs, reimbursement risk, payer mix, and policy changes can pressure margins. HCA also has meaningful leverage, so the margin of safety depends on stable cash generation.

**Notable valuation signals:** HCA is attractive on earnings yield after the drawdown, but it ranks below ACGL, META, QCOM, and CI because leverage and policy risk narrow the margin of safety.
