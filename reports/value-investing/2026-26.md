# Value Investing Weekly Report - 2026-W26

Run dates: 2026-06-22, 2026-06-23  
Scope: US-listed public equities  
Purpose: Research support only; not personal financial advice.

## Short Market Context

The broad US equity market continues to price in strong earnings growth and substantial AI-related capital spending. FactSet's recent S&P 500 earnings work showed elevated expected 2026 earnings growth and a forward market multiple near or above long-term averages, so the overall index does not offer an obvious margin of safety. That makes the quality-value screen more selective: the best candidates need either unusually strong cash generation, clearly resilient business economics, or an identifiable reason current multiples may understate medium-term value.

For the 2026-06-23 run, I screened across large and mid-cap US-listed stocks with a bias toward durable free cash flow, returns on capital, balance-sheet resilience, and valuation support from earnings, free cash flow, book value, or normalized cash earnings. I did not use the 2026-06-22 ranking to choose today's list; the comparison section below is only a same-week record after today's independent ranking.

## Today's Top Ideas - 2026-06-23

### 1. Arch Capital Group (ACGL)

**Why it looks undervalued:** ACGL remains the cleanest quality-value candidate in this run. The stock was quoted around $92 on 2026-06-23, with a trailing P/E near 7.1. StockAnalysis shows trailing free cash flow of about $5.86 billion, and GuruFocus shows valuation metrics that remain low for a specialty insurer with a history of underwriting discipline, including price-to-free-cash-flow around the mid-single digits.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** Arch is not just statistically cheap. Its Q1 2026 results showed profitable underwriting, with the reinsurance segment reporting a 75.9% combined ratio and favorable prior-year reserve development. The company's long record of disciplined underwriting and book-value compounding makes the low multiple more interesting than a generic insurance discount.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** Insurance earnings are cyclical. Catastrophe losses, reserve development, and reinsurance pricing can shift quickly. The very low P/E may overstate normalized cheapness if recent underwriting conditions and reserve releases prove unusually favorable.

**Notable valuation signals:** Low earnings multiple, low cash-flow multiple, and strong underwriting quality give ACGL the best combination of valuation support and business quality in today's screen.

### 2. The Cigna Group (CI)

**Why it looks undervalued:** CI was quoted around $282 on 2026-06-23, with a trailing P/E just under 12. Cigna reported Q1 2026 adjusted EPS of $7.79 and raised its full-year 2026 adjusted EPS outlook to at least $30.35, implying a high-single-digit to low-double-digit multiple on management's adjusted earnings outlook. StockAnalysis also shows trailing free cash flow of about $7.66 billion.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** Cigna has scale in health benefits and Evernorth pharmacy services, recurring customer relationships, and strong cash generation. The business is more controversial than pristine, but the discount is attached to real earnings power rather than obvious financial distress.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** PBM scrutiny, drug-pricing policy, healthcare regulation, and leadership transition risk are material. The stock may deserve a lower multiple if the market concludes that pharmacy-benefit-manager economics are structurally less durable than past earnings suggest.

**Notable valuation signals:** CI is one of the clearest earnings-yield ideas in the screen. It ranks below ACGL because the regulatory risk is central to the thesis, not incidental.

### 3. Meta Platforms (META)

**Why it looks undervalued:** META was quoted around $564 on 2026-06-23, with a trailing P/E near 20.5. StockAnalysis shows a forward P/E near 17.2 and very large operating cash flow, although free cash flow is being pressured by heavy AI infrastructure spending. For a global digital advertising platform with high margins, that valuation remains reasonable relative to the broader market.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** Meta's core advertising business remains one of the world's scaled digital platforms, with strong network effects, huge distribution, and high absolute cash generation. The company can fund AI infrastructure, buybacks, and product experiments from internally generated cash rather than relying on fragile financing conditions.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** The market's skepticism is rational. AI capex, Reality Labs losses, regulation, and ad-cycle sensitivity can all reduce the realized value case. If incremental AI spending fails to produce durable revenue or efficiency gains, today's multiple may not be as cheap as it looks.

**Notable valuation signals:** META offers a near-market multiple for a business with above-market profitability and cash generation. The idea is quality-led value rather than deep value.

### 4. Berkshire Hathaway (BRK.B)

**Why it looks undervalued:** BRK.B was quoted around $489 on 2026-06-23, with a trailing P/E around 14.3. StockAnalysis shows roughly $397 billion of cash and $129 billion of debt, leaving net cash of about $268.5 billion and book value per B share around $337. The stock is not deeply discounted, but the valuation is reasonable for a collection of resilient operating businesses, insurance float, and very large liquidity.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** Berkshire remains a fortress-balance-sheet compounder. Its operating businesses, insurance platform, marketable securities, and cash optionality give it durability that many low-multiple stocks lack. Morningstar's Berkshire work also highlights the long-term book-value compounding record while noting size as a constraint.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** Berkshire's size makes excess returns harder. Leadership transition from Warren Buffett to Greg Abel may also change investor perception of capital allocation. The shares are closer to fair value than to a deep bargain.

**Notable valuation signals:** BRK.B is a lower-upside, lower-fragility quality-value candidate. It belongs on the shortlist because the balance sheet and business mix reduce the odds of a value trap.

### 5. Qualcomm (QCOM)

**Why it looks undervalued:** QCOM was quoted around $222 on 2026-06-23, with a trailing P/E near 24. StockAnalysis shows trailing free cash flow of about $12.50 billion and price-to-free-cash-flow around 18.7. That is not statistically cheap in isolation, but it is reasonable for a company with strong returns on capital and meaningful technology optionality.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** Morningstar shows normalized ROIC above 30%, and Qualcomm's licensing and chipset assets remain valuable. The Q2 fiscal 2026 release showed $10.6 billion of revenue, non-GAAP EPS of $2.65, record quarterly QCT automotive revenue, 20% year-over-year growth in combined automotive and IoT revenue, and continued buybacks.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** Handset cyclicality, Apple modem risk, China exposure, inventory pressure, and the sustainability of diversification growth all matter. The Q2 headline GAAP EPS included a large tax benefit, so normalized earnings power should be judged more conservatively.

**Notable valuation signals:** QCOM is a quality-at-a-reasonable-price idea. It is less compelling than ACGL or CI on pure valuation but stronger than many low-multiple stocks on business quality and free-cash-flow durability.

### 6. Alphabet (GOOGL)

**Why it looks undervalued:** GOOGL was quoted around $350 on 2026-06-23, with a trailing P/E around 26.7. That is not optically cheap, but the valuation looks more reasonable when measured against Alphabet's scale, balance sheet, and cash generation. Alphabet reported trailing twelve-month free cash flow of $64.4 billion as of Q1 2026, even after substantial AI-related capital spending.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** Alphabet's core search, YouTube, and cloud assets remain high-quality businesses with enormous data, distribution, and infrastructure advantages. Q1 2026 results showed consolidated operating income up 30% and operating margin of 36.1%, while management continued investing aggressively behind AI infrastructure.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** The value case is sensitive to AI capex discipline and regulatory outcomes. Q1 net income was boosted by unrealized gains on non-marketable securities, so the headline EPS growth should not be treated as clean recurring earnings growth. The stock also screens less cheap than the higher-ranked names.

**Notable valuation signals:** GOOGL earns a place as a high-quality, potentially undervalued compounder, but confidence is lower than for the cheaper names because the margin of safety depends on long-term AI and cloud returns.

## Watchlist and Exclusions From Today's Main Ranking

- **HCA Healthcare (HCA):** Still interesting after a drawdown and with a P/E near 13, but reimbursement risk, labor pressure, and leverage make the margin of safety narrower than the main-list candidates today.
- **Gilead Sciences (GILD):** Strong free cash flow and a potentially attractive cash-flow yield, but pipeline concentration and patent-cycle uncertainty require deeper drug-by-drug work before it clears the quality hurdle.
- **CVS Health (CVS):** The turnaround may be improving, but the current trailing P/E is distorted and balance-sheet/regulatory complexity keep it off the main list.
- **JPMorgan Chase (JPM):** High-quality franchise and strong profitability, but the stock appears closer to a fair premium valuation than a clear bargain after its recent strength.
- **Apple (AAPL) and many AI-led mega-cap leaders:** Quality remains high, but current multiples generally leave less valuation support than the shortlist above.

The strongest current quality-value candidate is **Arch Capital Group (ACGL)**, followed by **Cigna (CI)** for investors willing to underwrite healthcare regulatory risk. **Meta (META)** and **Alphabet (GOOGL)** are quality-led value ideas rather than deep-value names; their upside depends on disciplined AI spending. **Berkshire Hathaway (BRK.B)** remains the most conservative candidate, while **Qualcomm (QCOM)** offers attractive technology cash flow with more cyclical and customer-specific risk.
