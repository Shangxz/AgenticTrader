# Value Investing Weekly Report - 2026-W30

Run date: 2026-07-20

This report is research support only, not personal financial advice. The shortlist below was built from a fresh quality-value review of the US public equity market for this run, emphasizing durable business quality, cash generation, balance-sheet resilience, and valuation that appears reasonable relative to current earnings power and medium-term prospects.

## Market Context

The broad US market still leaves room for select value opportunities, but index-level valuation is not especially cheap. Morningstar's US market page shows the overall market at roughly a mid-single-digit discount to analyst fair values, with small-value stocks more discounted than large-growth stocks. FactSet's latest earnings snapshot puts the S&P 500 forward 12-month P/E above both its 5-year and 10-year averages, which argues for selectivity rather than broad beta exposure.

AI-related capital spending remains the biggest cross-current. It is pressuring near-term free cash flow for hyperscalers, yet some of the same companies still have very high-margin core franchises and strong balance sheets. Today's ranking therefore favors companies where the valuation looks reasonable even after haircutting optimistic growth assumptions.

## Company Notes

### 1. Microsoft (MSFT)

**Why it looks undervalued:** Microsoft is no longer priced like a flawless mega-cap momentum stock despite continuing to grow revenue and operating income at high-teens to low-20s rates. At roughly 23x trailing earnings, the valuation looks reasonable for a business with dominant positions in enterprise software, cloud infrastructure, productivity software, and AI tooling.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** In fiscal Q3 2026, Microsoft reported revenue of $82.9 billion, up 18%, and operating income of $38.4 billion, up 20%. Microsoft Cloud revenue increased 29%, Azure and other cloud services increased 40%, and the AI business surpassed a $37 billion annual revenue run rate. That combination supports a high-return, recurring-revenue quality profile.

**Main risks:** The central risk is that AI infrastructure spending depresses free cash flow for longer than investors expect, or that AI revenue does not produce adequate returns on capital. Microsoft also has a very large market cap, so even good execution may not translate into outsized returns if the multiple compresses.

**Valuation signals:** The stock screens materially cheaper than it did during peak AI enthusiasm, while operating growth remains strong. Morningstar-linked coverage also recently highlighted Microsoft as undervalued, but the thesis should be tested against capex intensity and free-cash-flow conversion in upcoming results.

### 2. Alphabet (GOOGL)

**Why it looks undervalued:** Alphabet trades at a mid-20s earnings multiple despite very strong revenue growth, high margins, and a large net liquidity position. The market appears to be discounting elevated AI capital spending and uncertainty around the durability of search economics, creating a plausible quality-value setup.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** Alphabet's Q1 2026 operating income rose 30% to $39.7 billion, with a 36.1% operating margin. Google Services produced a 45.3% operating margin, while Google Cloud revenue rose 63% to $20 billion and Cloud operating margin expanded to 32.9%. The company also reported $64.4 billion of trailing-12-month free cash flow and $126.8 billion of cash and marketable securities.

**Main risks:** AI capex is the disconfirming factor. Q1 capex was $35.7 billion, and free cash flow was only $10.1 billion in the quarter despite enormous operating cash flow. Search disruption, regulatory pressure, and possible lower returns on AI infrastructure spending remain real concerns.

**Valuation signals:** A roughly 26.5x trailing P/E is not statistically cheap, but it is modest relative to the quality and growth of the underlying businesses. The idea ranks below Microsoft because the free-cash-flow pressure from AI infrastructure is more pronounced.

### 3. Berkshire Hathaway (BRK.B)

**Why it looks undervalued:** Berkshire's valuation is supported by a diversified collection of operating businesses, a large public-equity portfolio, and substantial cash. Recent reporting indicates the stock traded around 1.4x book value, near a level where prior buybacks have occurred, and Barron's estimated Berkshire may have materially increased repurchases in Q2 2026.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** Berkshire remains one of the cleanest balance-sheet stories in US equities. Its insurance float, railroad, utility, energy, manufacturing, service, retail, and investment operations provide unusually diversified earnings streams. The cash balance gives management optionality if markets become more attractive.

**Main risks:** Succession and capital allocation are now more central to the thesis. A large cash balance can become a drag if attractive reinvestment opportunities are scarce. Berkshire also has exposure to equity-market marks and insurance underwriting volatility.

**Valuation signals:** The combination of a roughly 14x earnings multiple, about 1.4x book value based on recent reporting, and potential repurchases makes Berkshire attractive as a lower-volatility value candidate rather than a deep-value turnaround.

### 4. Accenture (ACN)

**Why it looks undervalued:** Accenture's current earnings multiple screens unusually low for a global consulting and technology services leader. The market appears to be pricing in weak discretionary IT demand and pressure in US federal work, but the company continues to produce strong profitability and free cash flow.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** Accenture's Q3 fiscal 2026 release described solid revenue, strong profitability and EPS growth, and robust free cash flow. Management guided fiscal 2026 local-currency revenue growth to 3%-4%, or 4%-5% excluding the estimated US federal impact. Third-party reporting on the same release cited 17.0% operating margin and continued free-cash-flow guidance of $10.8 billion to $11.5 billion.

**Main risks:** Consulting is cyclical when clients delay transformation work. AI could also pressure lower-value services if Accenture cannot keep moving up the value chain. The thesis needs confirmation that bookings and organic growth stabilize rather than merely cost controls supporting EPS.

**Valuation signals:** At roughly 11.5x trailing earnings, the market is assigning a challenged-services multiple to a business that historically earned a quality premium. This is attractive, but confidence stays medium until revenue momentum improves.

### 5. Zoetis (ZTS)

**Why it looks undervalued:** Zoetis has been repriced sharply as growth slowed, yet it remains the leading animal-health company with a diversified companion-animal and livestock portfolio. At roughly 12.6x trailing earnings, the stock now prices in a lot of disappointment.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** Zoetis still earns high margins, produces durable demand through animal health and veterinary channels, and has a broad innovation pipeline. In Q1 2026, revenue was $2.3 billion, net income was $601 million, and adjusted EPS was $1.53. Management also pointed to more than 12 potential blockbuster pipeline opportunities.

**Main risks:** This is the least clean quality story in the top five today. US companion-animal revenue declined, pet-owner price sensitivity increased, veterinary visits softened, and competition intensified in dermatology and parasiticides. Management reduced 2026 guidance to 2%-5% organic operational revenue growth and adjusted EPS of $6.85-$7.00.

**Valuation signals:** The low earnings multiple creates a recovery setup, but the company needs to show that current weakness is cyclical and competitive rather than structural. Size this lower than the higher-confidence names unless the next updates show improving US companion-animal trends.

## Watchlist / Not Selected Today

- **Danaher (DHR):** Excellent business quality and free-cash-flow conversion, but the current earnings multiple is still relatively high versus subdued core growth, so it did not clear the valuation hurdle today.

## Conclusion

The strongest current quality-value candidates are **Microsoft**, **Alphabet**, and **Berkshire Hathaway**. Microsoft and Alphabet offer the best blend of business quality and growth-adjusted valuation, but both require close monitoring of AI capex returns. Berkshire is less explosive but provides the cleanest downside-oriented value profile. Accenture and Zoetis are more contrarian ideas where the valuation is compelling, but the near-term operating evidence is mixed enough to keep confidence at medium.
