# Value Investing Weekly Report - 2026-W30

Run dates: 2026-07-20, 2026-07-22, 2026-07-23, 2026-07-24

This report is research support only, not personal financial advice. Each run starts from a fresh quality-value review of the US public equity market, emphasizing durable business quality, cash generation, balance-sheet resilience, and valuation that appears reasonable relative to current earnings power and medium-term prospects. Point-in-time prices and valuation multiples can change quickly and should be refreshed before any investment decision.

## Market Context

The broad US market still does not look cheap in aggregate. Advisor Perspectives' July market-valuation work, using June 2026 monthly data, placed the S&P 500 well above long-term valuation norms, with the four-indicator average more than 3 standard deviations above its historical mean. Recent market action also reinforces the risk of concentration: large AI-linked technology stocks sold off sharply after Alphabet and Tesla earnings renewed investor concern about capital intensity and cash conversion.

That backdrop argues for selectivity rather than broad-market enthusiasm. Today's best opportunities are not simply the lowest P/E stocks; they are de-rated businesses where current valuation appears to discount a severe or prolonged setback, while the company still shows durable margins, strong cash generation, brand strength, recurring revenue, or balance-sheet resilience.

## Company Notes - 2026-07-24

### 1. Adobe (ADBE)

**Why it looks undervalued:** Adobe moved to the top of today's list because the stock declined again while the underlying business still looks far healthier than the valuation implies. A roughly 12.1x trailing earnings multiple is unusually low for a recurring-revenue software company with strong margins, high cash generation, and entrenched positions in creative, document, and marketing workflows.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** Adobe reported record fiscal Q2 2026 revenue of $6.62 billion, up about 13% year over year, and raised full-year fiscal 2026 revenue and non-GAAP EPS targets. Those facts do not prove Adobe will avoid AI disruption, but they show that customer demand and monetization have not yet broken.

**Main risks:** The risk is fundamental, not cosmetic. Generative AI could weaken pricing power, change creative workflows, or pressure legacy bundle economics. Recent analyst concerns around AI disruption, pricing/packaging, and management transition risk deserve weight. Confidence stays at medium because the discount is large, but the disconfirming scenario is credible.

**Valuation signals:** The current setup appears to price in a severe deterioration. The key confirms from here are continued ARR growth, stable or improving margins, and evidence that AI features increase customer value rather than cannibalize paid workflows.

### 2. Accenture (ACN)

**Why it looks undervalued:** Accenture remains one of the cleanest quality-value ideas in the large-cap universe. At roughly 11.1x trailing earnings, the stock is priced like a structurally impaired consulting business even though the company is still producing growth, margin resilience, and strong free cash flow.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** Accenture's fiscal Q3 2026 release reported revenue of $18.7 billion, up 6% in US dollars and 3% in local currency; diluted EPS rose 9% to $3.80; and free cash flow was $3.6 billion for the quarter. Management also raised full-year free-cash-flow guidance to $10.8 billion-$11.5 billion. The company remains deeply embedded in enterprise technology, managed services, cybersecurity, data, and AI implementation work.

**Main risks:** Consulting demand can remain soft if clients delay discretionary projects. AI may also compress lower-value services revenue if Accenture cannot keep moving work toward higher-value implementation, managed services, security, and industry-specific AI programs.

**Valuation signals:** Low earnings multiple plus strong free cash flow gives Accenture one of the best current margins of safety among high-quality service businesses. Bookings, organic growth, and pricing are the key evidence to monitor.

### 3. Lululemon Athletica (LULU)

**Why it looks undervalued:** Lululemon is today's main new addition. A roughly 9.0x trailing earnings multiple is a large reset for a premium global brand that historically earned high margins and returns on capital. The valuation now appears to price in a long brand fade or a structurally weaker North American business.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** The brand remains differentiated, store productivity has historically been strong, and the company continues to grow internationally. Fiscal Q1 2026 revenue rose 4% to $2.5 billion, and international revenue increased 22%, partly offsetting Americas weakness.

**Main risks:** The risk case is obvious and must be respected. Americas net revenue fell 3%, Americas comparable sales fell 5%, and diluted EPS declined to $1.69 in Q1. If US brand heat, product cadence, or category demand remains weak, the low multiple could be a value trap rather than an opportunity.

**Valuation signals:** The stock is cheap enough to earn a top-three position, but confidence is only medium. The thesis needs stabilization in Americas comps, evidence that inventories and markdowns remain controlled, and continued international growth.

### 4. Berkshire Hathaway (BRK.B)

**Why it looks undervalued:** Berkshire is not a deep-discount stock, but a roughly 14.3x trailing earnings multiple is reasonable for a diversified, lower-volatility business with insurance float, operating subsidiaries, public equities, and a large cash/T-bill reserve. That liquidity is especially valuable when broad market valuations are stretched.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** Berkshire's Q1 2026 release reported operating earnings of $11.346 billion versus $9.641 billion a year earlier, with contributions from insurance underwriting, BNSF, Berkshire Hathaway Energy, and manufacturing/service/retailing. The balance sheet remains unusually resilient.

**Main risks:** Succession and capital allocation are central. The large cash balance can drag returns if markets remain expensive and buyback or acquisition opportunities are limited. Insurance underwriting, energy regulation, and equity holdings can also create quarterly volatility.

**Valuation signals:** Berkshire remains the defensive anchor in the shortlist. It ranks below Adobe, Accenture, and Lululemon only because the discount is less obvious, not because quality is weaker.

### 5. Constellation Brands (STZ)

**Why it looks undervalued:** Constellation entered the top ideas today because the stock's roughly 12.3x trailing earnings multiple looks modest for a company with strong beer brands, high operating margins, and meaningful free cash flow. The market is discounting pressure in alcohol demand and the company's category exposure.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** In fiscal Q1 2027, Constellation reported net sales of about $2.43 billion, operating income of $845 million, and free cash flow of $485 million. Comparable EPS increased despite lower reported sales, helped by margin performance.

**Main risks:** Beer and broader alcohol demand may remain pressured by consumer trade-down, moderation trends, input costs, or retailer inventory adjustments. Balance-sheet leverage and capital allocation also matter more when growth is not robust.

**Valuation signals:** The combination of cash generation and a low-teens P/E is attractive, but confidence is medium-low because the demand picture is less durable than the best software, services, or insurance-franchise candidates.

### 6. Zoetis (ZTS)

**Why it looks undervalued:** Zoetis remains a contrarian quality-value candidate. At roughly 12.2x trailing earnings after a steep decline, the market is pricing in weak companion-animal demand and competitive pressure. That is a harsh valuation for the leading animal-health company if the slowdown proves cyclical.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** Zoetis reported Q1 2026 revenue of $2.3 billion, up 3% reported but flat organically, with net income of $601 million and adjusted EPS of $1.53. The animal-health category still benefits from durable pet-care and livestock-health demand, specialized channels, and attractive margins.

**Main risks:** Zoetis has one of the clearest disconfirming risks on the list. US companion-animal weakness, veterinary visit softness, and competition in dermatology/parasiticides could be more structural than temporary. Management's 2026 outlook for 2%-5% organic operational revenue growth and adjusted EPS of $6.85-$7.00 leaves limited room for disappointment.

**Valuation signals:** The stock's decline has made the margin of safety more visible, but confirmation requires stabilization in US companion-animal trends and evidence that pricing, pipeline, and international growth can offset competitive pressure.

### 7. Alphabet (GOOGL)

**Why it looks undervalued:** Alphabet's sharp post-earnings selloff makes it more interesting on price, and the operating franchise remains exceptional. Google Services, YouTube, Android, Cloud, and AI infrastructure form one of the strongest digital ecosystems in public markets.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** Alphabet's Q2 2026 results showed revenue of $119.8 billion, up 24% year over year, with Google Cloud revenue up 82% to $24.8 billion. The cloud result supports the view that Alphabet is not merely defending search, but also building another scaled profit engine.

**Main risks:** The valuation signal is unusually noisy. Reported EPS was boosted by a large unrealized investment gain, and multiple market reports highlighted negative free cash flow in Q2 after heavy AI-related capital spending. Alphabet also raised 2026 capex expectations to about $195 billion-$205 billion. Search regulation and AI-driven search disruption remain additional risks.

**Valuation signals:** Alphabet stays on the list because the price reset and operating momentum are both meaningful. It ranks seventh because the current P/E understates normalized valuation risk, and free-cash-flow visibility is weaker than for the higher-ranked names.

## Watchlist / Not Selected Today

- **Microsoft (MSFT):** Still a world-class cloud/software franchise, but after a fresh scan it is less compelling than Adobe, Accenture, and Lululemon on valuation. The next earnings report is the key checkpoint for AI capex and free-cash-flow conversion.
- **Danaher (DHR):** Q2 results showed revenue growth, strong free cash flow, and raised adjusted EPS guidance, but the stock rebounded sharply today and the trailing P/E remains materially higher than the strongest value candidates. Bioprocessing softness also keeps the setup mixed.
- **Thermo Fisher Scientific (TMO):** Q2 results were strong and management raised outlook, but the stock's immediate jump and roughly 31x trailing P/E make it more of a quality/recovery watchlist name than a top value idea today.
- **Campbell's (CPB):** Low P/E keeps it statistically interesting, but leverage, packaged-food volume pressure, and weaker long-term growth keep it below the quality threshold.
- **APA Corp. (APA):** Cheap and cash-generative on current energy economics, but commodity exposure makes the quality-value case less durable than today's top-ranked franchises.
- **Medtronic (MDT) and Abbott (ABT):** Medtech looks more interesting after sector weakness, but current multiples and company-specific growth signals were not strong enough to displace the top ideas today.

Earlier July 20, July 22, and July 23 conclusions remain preserved here as weekly history, but today's ranking was formed independently from current valuation, current prices, and the latest public evidence.

## Conclusion

The strongest current quality-value candidates are **Adobe**, **Accenture**, and **Lululemon**. Adobe has the most striking software-franchise valuation reset but real AI disruption risk. Accenture is the cleanest free-cash-flow compounder at a depressed multiple. Lululemon offers a large brand-recovery discount, though it needs evidence that Americas weakness is stabilizing. Berkshire remains the defensive anchor, while Constellation, Zoetis, and Alphabet are credible but carry clearer near-term disconfirming factors.
