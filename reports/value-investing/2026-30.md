# Value Investing Weekly Report - 2026-W30

Run dates: 2026-07-20, 2026-07-22, 2026-07-23

This report is research support only, not personal financial advice. Each run starts from a fresh quality-value review of the US public equity market, emphasizing durable business quality, cash generation, balance-sheet resilience, and valuation that appears reasonable relative to current earnings power and medium-term prospects. Point-in-time prices and valuation multiples can change quickly and should be refreshed before any investment decision.

## Market Context

The US market remains expensive in aggregate, so today's scan continues to require both valuation support and clear business quality. Advisor Perspectives' July 22 market-valuation update reported that four long-term valuation indicators showed the S&P 500 overvalued by 116% to 207% using June 2026 monthly data, with the four-indicator average at 155% and more than 3 standard deviations above its historical mean. That does not predict short-term returns, but it argues against treating broad-market exposure as cheap.

Near-term earnings quality is mixed but still supportive for selected large caps. Alphabet's July 22 Q2 release showed strong operating results and accelerating cloud demand, while market reaction focused on heavier AI capital spending. That pattern remains a useful lens for this report: companies with durable franchises can look statistically cheap when investors worry that AI spending, demand cyclicality, or business-model transitions will consume future free cash flow.

Morningstar's July 2026 undervalued-stock work also suggests the opportunity set is selective rather than market-wide. The best hunting ground today remains de-rated quality: companies with strong margins, high returns on capital, recurring or resilient demand, and a plausible path for current concerns to prove temporary rather than structural.

## Company Notes - 2026-07-23

### 1. Accenture (ACN)

**Why it looks undervalued:** Accenture remains the cleanest quality-value setup in today's screen. At roughly 11.2x trailing earnings, the stock is priced like a structurally impaired consulting business, but the latest fiscal Q3 numbers still show growth, margin expansion, and strong cash conversion.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** Accenture's June 2026 fiscal Q3 release reported revenue of $18.7 billion, up 6% in US dollars and 3% in local currency; diluted EPS rose 9% to $3.80; free cash flow was $3.6 billion; and the company returned $2.2 billion to shareholders through repurchases and dividends. The business remains deeply embedded in enterprise technology, managed services, cybersecurity, and AI implementation work.

**Main risks:** Consulting and transformation spend can remain weak if enterprise clients delay discretionary projects. AI may also compress lower-value services revenue if Accenture cannot keep moving work toward higher-value implementation, managed services, security, and industry-specific AI programs. Bookings and organic growth are the key confirms or disconfirms from here.

**Valuation signals:** The combination of a low earnings multiple, strong free cash flow, and still-positive revenue/EPS growth makes ACN today's highest-ranked idea.

### 2. Adobe (ADBE)

**Why it looks undervalued:** Adobe moved into the top five today after a sharp share-price decline left the stock near 12.5x trailing earnings. That is a low multiple for a software company with recurring subscription economics, strong margins, and still-growing revenue. Morningstar's post-earnings view described Adobe as significantly undervalued versus its fair value estimate, while the market is clearly discounting AI disruption risk.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** Adobe reported record Q2 fiscal 2026 revenue of $6.62 billion, up 13% year over year, with GAAP EPS of $4.25 and non-GAAP EPS of $5.96. The company also raised fiscal 2026 revenue and non-GAAP EPS guidance. Those facts do not remove AI risk, but they show the core business has not yet broken.

**Main risks:** Adobe has a real disconfirming factor: generative AI could pressure pricing power, creative workflows, and customer willingness to pay for legacy bundles. Morgan Stanley's July downgrade highlighted simultaneous risks from Adobe's pricing/packaging transition, executive changes, and AI investment needs. Because that risk is fundamental rather than merely cyclical, confidence is medium rather than high.

**Valuation signals:** The setup is attractive because the current multiple appears to price in a severe deterioration. The investment case needs continued ARR growth, durable margins, and evidence that AI features strengthen rather than cannibalize the franchise.

### 3. Berkshire Hathaway (BRK.B)

**Why it looks undervalued:** Berkshire is not a deep discount statistically, but about 14.3x trailing earnings is reasonable for a diversified, lower-volatility business with insurance float, operating subsidiaries, public equities, and a large cash/T-bill reserve. The cash reserve is especially useful in a market where broad valuations are stretched.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** Berkshire's Q1 2026 release reported operating earnings of $11.346 billion versus $9.641 billion a year earlier, with contributions from insurance underwriting, BNSF, Berkshire Hathaway Energy, and manufacturing/service/retailing. The balance sheet remains unusually resilient.

**Main risks:** Succession and capital allocation are now central. The large cash balance can drag returns if markets stay expensive and repurchase or acquisition opportunities remain limited. Insurance underwriting and equity holdings can also create quarterly volatility.

**Valuation signals:** Barron's reported that Berkshire may have accelerated Q2 repurchases after only modest Q1 buybacks, estimating possible second-quarter repurchases in the billions. That is not official confirmation, but if Berkshire is buying near roughly 1.4x book, it supports the defensive value case.

### 4. Zoetis (ZTS)

**Why it looks undervalued:** Zoetis remains a contrarian quality-value candidate. At about 12.2x trailing earnings after a steep decline from its 52-week high, the market is pricing in weak companion-animal demand and competitive pressure. That is a harsh valuation for the leading animal-health company if the slowdown proves cyclical.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** Zoetis reported Q1 2026 revenue of $2.3 billion, up 3% reported but flat organically, with net income of $601 million and adjusted EPS of $1.53. The animal-health category still benefits from durable pet-care and livestock-health demand, specialized sales channels, and attractive margins.

**Main risks:** This is still the riskiest top-five idea. Zoetis lowered or narrowed 2026 expectations earlier this year, with guidance calling for 2%-5% organic operational revenue growth and adjusted EPS of $6.85-$7.00. US companion-animal weakness, veterinary visit softness, and competition in dermatology/parasiticides could be more structural than temporary.

**Valuation signals:** The stock's decline has made the margin of safety more visible, but confirmation requires stabilization in US companion-animal trends and evidence that pricing, pipeline, and international growth can offset competitive pressure.

### 5. Microsoft (MSFT)

**Why it looks undervalued:** Microsoft is not optically cheap, but it remains attractive on a quality-adjusted basis. A roughly 23.2x trailing P/E is close enough to broad-market valuation that investors are not paying an excessive premium for one of the highest-quality software/cloud franchises in the US market.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** Fiscal Q3 2026 results showed revenue up 18% to $82.9 billion and operating income up 20% to $38.4 billion. Microsoft Cloud revenue increased 29% to $54.5 billion, Azure and other cloud services grew 40%, and commercial remaining performance obligation nearly doubled to $627 billion. Those numbers support durability, not just near-term enthusiasm.

**Main risks:** AI capex remains the core issue. The market wants proof that spending on data centers, chips, and AI products turns into higher free-cash-flow margins rather than a permanent capital-intensity reset. The July 29 earnings report is the next major checkpoint.

**Valuation signals:** Microsoft fell from rank 1 earlier this week to rank 5 today only because Adobe's valuation reset and Alphabet's post-earnings capex picture changed the relative quality-value ranking. Microsoft remains a high-quality candidate, but not the cheapest one.

### 6. Alphabet (GOOGL)

**Why it looks undervalued:** Alphabet's Q2 results strengthened the business-quality case: revenue rose 24% to $119.8 billion, Google Services revenue rose 15% to $94.5 billion, Google Cloud revenue rose 82% to $24.8 billion, operating income rose 30%, and operating margin expanded to 34%. A roughly 26.1x trailing P/E is not extreme for that growth profile.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** Search, YouTube, Android, Cloud, and AI infrastructure form one of the strongest digital ecosystems in public markets. The Q2 release also showed Cloud operating income of $8.8 billion, reinforcing that cloud scale is now a major profit contributor rather than only a revenue story.

**Main risks:** Alphabet's reported EPS was heavily boosted by a $98.0 billion net unrealized gain on equity securities, so GAAP earnings are less useful than usual this quarter. More importantly, investors remain focused on AI capex intensity. Market reports after earnings cited higher full-year capex expectations and pressure on free cash flow. Search regulation and AI-driven search disruption remain additional risks.

**Valuation signals:** Alphabet stays on the list because the operating momentum is exceptional, but it ranks below cleaner value candidates today because free-cash-flow visibility is weaker and the latest earnings include a large non-operating gain.

- **Zoetis remained in the shortlist** as a higher-risk recovery idea, with valuation support offset by unresolved US companion-animal and competitive concerns.

Earlier July 20 and July 22 entries remain preserved in substance through the company history and change notes above, but today's rankings were formed independently from current valuation and business evidence.

## Conclusion

The strongest current quality-value candidates are **Accenture**, **Adobe**, and **Berkshire Hathaway**. Accenture is the cleanest de-rated quality business on current earnings and free-cash-flow evidence; Adobe offers the largest software-franchise valuation reset but carries real AI disruption risk; Berkshire is the most defensive value candidate in an expensive market. Zoetis, Microsoft, and Alphabet remain credible but each has a clearer near-term disconfirming factor: Zoetis around demand/competition, Microsoft around AI capex and free-cash-flow conversion, and Alphabet around capex intensity plus unusually distorted Q2 GAAP earnings.
