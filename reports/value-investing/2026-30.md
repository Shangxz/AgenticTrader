# Value Investing Weekly Report - 2026-W30

Run dates: 2026-07-20, 2026-07-22

This report is research support only, not personal financial advice. Each run starts from a fresh quality-value review of the US public equity market, emphasizing durable business quality, cash generation, balance-sheet resilience, and valuation that appears reasonable relative to current earnings power and medium-term prospects. Point-in-time prices and valuation multiples can change quickly and should be refreshed before any investment decision.

## Market Context

The US market is not broadly cheap, so the bar remains high for new ideas. FactSet's July 17 Earnings Insight showed the S&P 500 at a 20.3x forward 12-month P/E, above both its 5-year average of 19.9x and 10-year average of 19.0x. Only 10% of S&P 500 companies had reported Q2 results at that point, but early EPS and revenue beat rates were above historical averages, which helps explain why the index still commands an elevated multiple.

Morningstar's July 2026 undervalued-stock update described US equities as trading around an 8% discount to its aggregate fair value estimate, but the discount is uneven. The best hunting ground remains selective: high-quality businesses where temporary growth concerns, AI capex fears, industry cyclicality, or post-selloff sentiment have pushed valuation below what the underlying business quality may justify.

For the July 22 run, the screen favored companies with strong returns on capital, resilient cash generation, and identifiable reasons for investor skepticism. The resulting shortlist is not a pure low-P/E list; it deliberately avoids companies that look cheap mainly because the business model appears structurally impaired.

## Company Notes

### 1. Microsoft (MSFT)

**Why it looks undervalued:** Microsoft is not statistically cheap in an absolute sense, but it looks inexpensive relative to the quality and durability of the franchise. At roughly 23.7x trailing earnings and 21.5x forward earnings, the stock trades near the market's broad forward multiple even though Microsoft has a better balance of recurring revenue, margins, and reinvestment runway than most large-cap peers.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** Fiscal Q3 2026 revenue rose 18% to $82.9 billion and operating income rose 20% to $38.4 billion. Microsoft Cloud revenue increased 29% to $54.5 billion, Azure and other cloud services grew 40%, and commercial remaining performance obligation nearly doubled to $627 billion. StockAnalysis also shows ROIC around 27%, moderate debt metrics, and very strong interest coverage.

**Main risks:** AI infrastructure spending remains the key disconfirming factor. Microsoft's P/FCF is much higher than its earnings multiple because capex is elevated, so the thesis depends on AI spend producing durable revenue and margin expansion rather than simply absorbing cash. The upcoming July 29 earnings report is also a near-term event risk.

**Valuation signals:** Microsoft ranks first because the current multiple looks reasonable for a business still compounding revenue and operating income at a high rate. The idea is less about deep value and more about paying a fair-to-low price for one of the highest-quality US businesses.

### 2. Accenture (ACN)

**Why it looks undervalued:** Accenture's valuation has fallen to levels that look unusually low for a global consulting and technology services leader. At roughly 11.3x trailing earnings, 9.8x forward earnings, and about 6.9x free cash flow, the market appears to be pricing in a prolonged downturn in discretionary technology-services demand.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** Accenture's June 2026 fiscal Q3 update still described solid revenue, strong profitability and EPS growth, and robust free cash flow. Management expects fiscal 2026 local-currency revenue growth of 3%-4%, or 4%-5% excluding the estimated US federal impact. StockAnalysis shows ROIC around 27%, low debt-to-EBITDA, and strong interest coverage.

**Main risks:** Consulting demand is cyclical, and enterprise clients may continue delaying transformation projects. AI could pressure lower-value labor-based services if Accenture fails to keep moving toward higher-value implementation, managed services, cybersecurity, and industry-specific AI work. The short interest increase is a useful warning that skepticism has intensified.

**Valuation signals:** Accenture moved up in today's ranking because the share price declined further since the July 20 update while the core quality markers remain intact. The main thing to monitor is whether bookings and organic growth stabilize, not just whether cost controls support EPS.

### 3. Berkshire Hathaway (BRK.B)

**Why it looks undervalued:** Berkshire trades at about 14.3x trailing earnings and around 1.45x book value, with a lower-volatility profile than most US equities. The valuation is supported by diversified operating businesses, insurance float, public equities, and a large cash/T-bill reserve that can become valuable if markets pull back.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** Berkshire's Q1 2026 release reported operating earnings of $11.346 billion versus $9.641 billion a year earlier. Insurance underwriting, BNSF, Berkshire Hathaway Energy, and manufacturing/service/retailing all contributed to operating earnings. StockAnalysis also shows a large net cash position, low debt-to-equity, and beta well below the market.

**Main risks:** Succession and capital allocation now matter more, with Greg Abel's early decisions under closer scrutiny. The large cash balance can be a drag if attractive deployment opportunities do not appear. Berkshire also has mark-to-market noise from equity holdings and periodic insurance underwriting volatility.

**Valuation signals:** Recent Barron's reporting estimated Berkshire may have meaningfully accelerated buybacks in Q2, though official confirmation should come with Q2 earnings. If repurchases are occurring near roughly 1.4x book value, that supports the downside-oriented value case.

### 4. Zoetis (ZTS)

**Why it looks undervalued:** Zoetis has been repriced sharply after slower companion-animal demand and competitive concerns. The stock now trades near 12.4x trailing earnings, 10.8x forward earnings, and 14.1x free cash flow, which is a low valuation for a company that historically deserved a premium because of durable animal-health economics.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** Zoetis remains the leading animal-health company and still shows high profitability. Q1 2026 revenue was $2.3 billion, net income was $601 million, adjusted EPS was $1.53, and StockAnalysis shows ROIC around 28%, operating margin around 38%, and free cash flow of about $2.24 billion over the last 12 months.

**Main risks:** This is the most contrarian idea in the top five. Management revised 2026 guidance to 2%-5% organic operational revenue growth and adjusted EPS of $6.85-$7.00. Weak veterinary visits, pet-owner price sensitivity, and competition in dermatology and parasiticides could prove more structural than cyclical.

**Valuation signals:** The valuation is now compelling enough to keep Zoetis on the shortlist, but confidence stays at medium until the next earnings update shows whether the US companion-animal slowdown is stabilizing.

### 5. Alphabet (GOOGL)

**Why it looks undervalued:** Alphabet's valuation is not obviously low on free cash flow because AI infrastructure spending is heavy, but the earnings multiple still looks reasonable for a dominant search, video, cloud, and AI platform business. The market appears to be discounting risks around search disruption, capex intensity, and regulatory pressure.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** Q1 2026 operating income rose 30% to $39.7 billion, with a 36.1% operating margin. Google Services generated a 45.3% operating margin, Google Cloud revenue rose 63% to $20 billion, Cloud operating margin expanded to 32.9%, and trailing-12-month free cash flow was $64.4 billion. StockAnalysis shows ROIC above 28% and a net cash position.

**Main risks:** Alphabet reports Q2 earnings after market close on July 22, making this a higher near-term-risk idea today. Capex was $35.7 billion in Q1, and market reports ahead of Q2 earnings emphasized expectations for another large increase in AI-related spending. Search disruption, regulation, and uncertain AI returns remain the core risks.

**Valuation signals:** Alphabet remains on the list because the business quality is exceptional and the multiple is not demanding versus its growth history. It ranks below Microsoft and Accenture today because the P/FCF multiple is elevated and the earnings event could materially change the setup.

## Watchlist / Not Selected Today

- **NVIDIA (NVDA):** The business quality is extraordinary, with very high ROIC, a net cash balance, and a forward P/E around 20.8x despite rapid expected growth. It did not enter the top five because the valuation depends heavily on sustained AI accelerator demand, hyperscaler capex discipline, and continued peak-like margins. It is a quality-growth idea with value characteristics, but not a clean value candidate.
- **Atmus Filtration Technologies (ATMU):** Strong filtration niche, good ROIC, and recurring replacement demand make it interesting. However, leverage and a valuation around 20x trailing earnings/17x forward earnings make the margin of safety less obvious than ACN or ZTS.
- **CBRE Group (CBRE):** A credible cyclical recovery candidate in commercial real estate services, but current trailing earnings and free-cash-flow multiples are less compelling, and ROIC is below the standard preferred for this report.
- **Constellation Brands (STZ):** Beer-market share and cash generation remain attractive, but leverage and consumer-demand uncertainty leave it just below the top five.
- **Danaher (DHR):** Excellent long-term business system and life-science-tooling quality, but the current multiple remains high versus subdued near-term core growth.

## Changes From Earlier Updates This Week

The July 20 update also ranked Microsoft, Alphabet, Berkshire Hathaway, Accenture, and Zoetis as the top five. Today's independent scan reached a similar universe, but the order changed meaningfully:

- **Accenture moved up to rank 2** because the stock declined further and now screens especially cheap on earnings and free cash flow while still showing high ROIC and strong cash conversion.
- **Alphabet moved down to rank 5** because its Q2 earnings report is due after market close today and AI capex expectations have become an even more immediate source of uncertainty.
- **Microsoft stayed rank 1** because the valuation remains reasonable relative to cloud, AI, backlog, margin, and ROIC evidence.
- **Berkshire and Zoetis remain in the shortlist** for different reasons: Berkshire as a defensive capital-allocation/value candidate, Zoetis as a higher-risk recovery candidate.
- **NVIDIA was added to the watchlist** because current forward earnings valuation is no longer extreme, but the idea still relies too much on continued AI cycle strength to qualify as a top quality-value pick today.

## Conclusion

The strongest current quality-value candidates are **Microsoft**, **Accenture**, and **Berkshire Hathaway**. Microsoft offers the best balance of durable growth and reasonable valuation; Accenture is the cleanest de-rated quality business on current multiples; Berkshire offers the most defensive margin-of-safety profile. Zoetis and Alphabet remain attractive but carry more visible near-term uncertainty: Zoetis around demand/competition, Alphabet around AI capex and today's earnings event.
