## Latest Update - 2026-07-12

Run date: Sunday, 2026-07-12, using public information available after the Friday, 2026-07-10 US market close. This is research support only, not personal financial advice.

### Short Market Context

The broad US market still does not look cheap. World PE Ratio estimated the US stock market P/E at 26.81 on 2026-07-10 and classified it as overvalued versus its five-year average range. Recent market commentary also shows a leadership tug-of-war: MarketWatch noted a defensive rotation toward financials, healthcare, consumer staples, lower volatility, and dependable cash flows, while Barron's midyear roundtable highlighted opportunities outside the most crowded AI and momentum trades.

Today's screen was run independently from earlier weekly entries. I looked across large and mid-cap US-listed public companies for a quality-value mix: modest earnings or cash-flow multiples, evidence of durable profitability, balance-sheet risk that appears manageable, and a plausible reason the current price may understate medium-term value. The strongest current cluster is in financial infrastructure, selected banks/insurers, and healthcare services rather than yesterday's more software-heavy shortlist.

### Today's Top Ideas

#### 1. Fidelity National Information Services (FIS)

**Why it looks undervalued:** FIS screens as one of the better combinations of low multiple and improving operating evidence. StockAnalysis showed a trailing P/E of 8.19, forward P/E of 6.55, PEG of 0.50, P/FCF of 8.34, and EV/FCF of 16.18 as of the latest July 2026 data. MarketWatch also reported that FIS shares were roughly 50% below their 52-week high during the July 6-9 trading week.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** The business is not just statistically cheap. FIS reported first-quarter 2026 adjusted EPS of $1.36, up 12% year over year; revenue up 30% on a GAAP basis; free cash flow up 111%; Banking Solutions adjusted EBITDA margin of 43.7%; and Capital Market Solutions adjusted EBITDA margin of 51.6%. Those are attractive margins for a core banking, payments, and capital-markets technology provider with recurring customer relationships.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** Debt is the key issue. FIS disclosed $21.1 billion of debt outstanding at March 31, 2026, so the valuation discount is not clean. The quality case depends on continued margin expansion, integration discipline, and proof that free cash flow can support debt reduction, dividends, and selective buybacks.

**Notable valuation signals:** Forward P/E 6.55; P/FCF 8.34; PEG 0.50; Q1 2026 free cash flow up 111%; shares about half off their 52-week high during the week.

#### 2. The Cigna Group (CI)

**Why it looks undervalued:** Cigna remains one of the more compelling defensive value candidates. StockAnalysis showed a trailing P/E of 12.43, forward P/E of 9.50, P/FCF of 10.13, and PEG of 0.94. Kiplinger's value screen also included Cigna with a forward P/E of 9.1 and PEG below 1.0.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** First-quarter 2026 results support the business-quality case. Cigna reported revenue growth of 5%, adjusted income from operations of $2.1 billion, adjusted EPS of $7.79, and 12% growth in adjusted income from operations, driven by both Cigna Healthcare and Evernorth Health Services. The business is defensive, scaled, and cash-generative, with pharmacy-benefit and healthcare-service operations that remain difficult to replicate.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** Managed care and PBM regulation remain substantial risks. Margins are structurally thinner than software or data businesses, and earnings quality can be affected by medical-cost trends, working capital, reimbursement pressure, and political scrutiny. The cheap multiple is attractive, but not free of policy risk.

**Notable valuation signals:** Forward P/E 9.50; P/FCF 10.13; PEG below 1.0; Q1 adjusted EPS up year over year; 2026 outlook raised in the April 30 earnings release.

#### 3. Bank of America (BAC)

**Why it looks undervalued:** Bank of America is not the cheapest bank, but it offers a strong quality-value mix among large US financials. StockAnalysis showed a trailing P/E of 14.83, forward P/E of 12.85, PEG of 0.86, P/FCF of 7.49, and EV/FCF of 6.58. Kiplinger's value screen also listed BAC with a forward P/E of 11.5, PEG of 0.779, and dividend yield above 2%.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** BAC's latest investor-relations summary showed Q1 2026 revenue, net of interest expense, of $30.3 billion, net income of $8.6 billion, diluted EPS of $1.11, and 16.0% return on tangible common equity. That level of ROTCE suggests the franchise is earning a healthy return despite normal credit and rate-cycle uncertainty. The scale of deposits, payments, consumer banking, wealth, and capital markets also gives the company a broader earnings base than smaller regional banks.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** The next confirmed earnings date is Tuesday, 2026-07-14, so this idea has near-term event risk. Credit costs, deposit betas, net interest income guidance, capital rules, and commercial loan quality could all shift the thesis quickly. Banks can look optically cheap late in a cycle if credit quality is about to weaken.

**Notable valuation signals:** Forward P/E 12.85; P/FCF 7.49; PEG 0.86; Q1 ROTCE 16.0%; Q2 earnings due 2026-07-14.

#### 4. MetLife (MET)

**Why it looks undervalued:** MetLife offers a more income-and-capital-return-oriented value case. Kiplinger's financial-stock screen listed MetLife with long-term EPS growth of 11.3%, ROE of 12.5%, and a 2.6% dividend yield. Kiplinger's broader value screen also showed MetLife at a forward P/E of 8.1. StockAnalysis showed a PEG of 0.82, while Macrotrends showed MetLife's P/E at 9.74 as of 2026-07-11.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** MetLife's Q1 2026 release showed adjusted earnings up 18% to $1.6 billion, adjusted EPS up 23% to $2.42, premiums, fees, and other revenues up 5%, and more than $1.1 billion returned to shareholders through buybacks and dividends. The company also reported holding-company cash and liquid assets of $3.9 billion, at the top of its target range.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** Insurance earnings can be affected by investment returns, mortality, underwriting, interest-rate movements, and capital-market volatility. ROIC is not directly comparable to industrial or software companies, and the balance-sheet complexity deserves a discount. This is a quality-value income candidate, not a high-return compounder in the same sense as an asset-light software business.

**Notable valuation signals:** Forward P/E around 8.1 in Kiplinger's screen; PEG 0.82; adjusted EPS up 23% in Q1; more than $1.1 billion returned to shareholders in Q1.

#### 5. MarketAxess (MKTX)

**Why it looks undervalued:** MarketAxess earns the fifth spot as the highest-quality contrarian idea in today's screen. StockAnalysis showed a trailing P/E of 13.47, forward P/E of 14.32, P/FCF of 14.95, EV/FCF of 14.27, and EV/EBITDA of 8.80. MarketWatch reported that shares were about 48% below their 52-week high on July 9, while Morningstar's June 2026 wide-moat screen listed MarketAxess among undervalued wide-moat stocks.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** MarketAxess has a differentiated electronic fixed-income trading network. Q1 2026 revenue rose 11%-12% year over year to about $233 million, operating margin improved to 43.2%, and non-GAAP EPS rose to $2.25. The company also completed a $300 million accelerated share repurchase that reduced share count by about 6%. Separately, June and Q2 trading statistics showed strong portfolio-trading growth, including a 71% increase in total portfolio trading ADV and higher estimated US credit portfolio-trading market share.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** The PEG ratio is not attractive, and fixed-income trading volumes can be cyclical. If market-share gains fade or pricing pressure intensifies, the apparent discount to historical multiples may be deserved. This is lower confidence than FIS or Cigna because the valuation is less statistically cheap and the growth path is more specialized.

**Notable valuation signals:** Forward P/E 14.32; EV/FCF 14.27; EV/EBITDA 8.80; operating margin 43.2% in Q1; shares roughly 48% below their 52-week high during the week.

### Watchlist / Near Misses

- **Alphabet (GOOGL):** Still a superb business, with Morningstar naming it a core stock and StockAnalysis showing ROE of 38.88% and ROIC of 28.34%. Q1 2026 revenue grew 22% and trailing-12-month free cash flow was $64.4 billion. It stays on the watchlist rather than the top five because current valuation evidence is mixed: Macrotrends showed a P/E above 27, and GuruFocus flagged valuation/FCF-yield concerns.
- **Lowe's (LOW):** A high-quality retail/home-improvement franchise with Q1 2026 sales of $23.1 billion, comparable sales up 0.6%, adjusted EPS of $3.03, and full-year operating-margin guidance around 11.2%-11.4%. It needs a more obvious valuation discount to displace today's top five.
- **Nike (NKE):** Morningstar's wide-moat undervalued screen and the brand franchise keep Nike on the watchlist. However, the latest results remain mixed: Q4 revenue was $11.0 billion and Nike Direct revenue fell 7% reported, while the gross-margin jump was heavily helped by tariff-recovery effects. Current P/FCF evidence looks expensive, so confidence is lower.
- **Adobe (ADBE), Accenture (ACN), and PayPal (PYPL):** These were strong earlier-week quality-value ideas and remain credible. Today's independent screen gave preference to companies with fresher public operating catalysts and sector diversification away from software/IT services.
- **Comcast (CMCSA):** Still statistically cheap, but the structural broadband, cable, and media risks remain large enough that it belongs below cleaner quality names.

### Changes From Earlier Updates This Week

- **New top five today:** FIS, Cigna, Bank of America, MetLife, and MarketAxess.
- **Removed from the top five:** Accenture, Adobe, PayPal, Zoetis, and Broadridge. This is not a rejection of those theses; it reflects today's fresh ranking and the stronger current evidence in financial infrastructure, healthcare services, and selected financials.
- **Meaningful rotation:** Today's report is more cyclical/financial and healthcare-oriented than the July 11 update, consistent with recent market commentary pointing to interest in lower-volatility, cash-flow, financial, and healthcare names.
- **Lower-confidence watchlist additions:** Alphabet, Lowe's, and Nike have clear quality characteristics, but current valuation or turnaround evidence was not strong enough to rank above the top five.

### Short Conclusion

The strongest current candidates are **FIS** and **Cigna**: both combine clear valuation support with fresh operating evidence. **Bank of America** and **MetLife** add large, profitable financial franchises at reasonable multiples, though both carry financial-cycle and balance-sheet complexity. **MarketAxess** is the higher-quality contrarian idea: less statistically cheap, but supported by a large share-price reset, strong margins, and evidence of trading-platform momentum.
