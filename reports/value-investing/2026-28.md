# Value Investing Weekly Report - 2026-W28

Run date: 2026-07-06, before the US market open. This is research support only, not personal financial advice.

## Short Market Context

The broad US market is not obviously cheap. World PE Ratio estimated the S&P 500 trailing P/E at 26.73 on 2026-07-02 and marked it overvalued versus its five-year average range. At the same time, large-cap value has had a strong year: iShares reported IWD, the Russell 1000 Value ETF, up 16.75% YTD as of 2026-07-01. That combination argues for selectivity: focus on companies where the cash-flow yield and return profile still give a margin of safety, rather than just buying statistically cheap stocks.

Today's scan was run fresh across the US public equity opportunity set, with emphasis on valuation versus earnings/free cash flow, returns on capital, balance-sheet risk, and whether the business still has durable economics.

## Today's Top Ideas

### 1. Adobe (ADBE)

**Why it looks undervalued:** Adobe screens unusually cheap for a high-margin software franchise: StockAnalysis showed a trailing P/E of 12.57, forward P/E of 8.47, P/FCF of 8.50, EV/FCF of 8.64, and FCF yield of 11.77%. The stock was down 43.96% over the prior 52 weeks, suggesting the market is discounting a material growth or competitive reset.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** The business still shows elite software economics: ROIC of 60.74%, operating margin of 36.72%, FCF margin of 40.80%, and $10.28B of trailing free cash flow. Buybacks also matter, with shares outstanding down 5.84% year over year.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** The core concern is whether generative AI, changing creative workflows, or slower enterprise demand permanently reduce Adobe's pricing power. If the apparent forward multiple reflects a one-time or cyclical estimate distortion, the valuation signal should be discounted. Customer retention, net new ARR, and AI monetization need close monitoring.

**Notable valuation signals:** P/FCF 8.50; EV/FCF 8.64; ROIC 60.74%; FCF yield 11.77%.

### 2. Accenture (ACN)

**Why it looks undervalued:** Accenture appears inexpensive relative to its long record as a global consulting and outsourcing compounder. StockAnalysis showed a trailing P/E of 10.99, forward P/E of 9.58, P/FCF of 6.68, EV/FCF of 6.54, and FCF yield of 14.97%. The stock was down 54.61% over the prior 52 weeks.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** Accenture remains asset-light and cash generative, with ROIC of 27.09%, $12.58B of trailing free cash flow, a 17.21% FCF margin, net cash of $1.78B, and interest coverage above 40x. The dividend yield was 4.75%, supported by a 52.15% payout ratio on earnings.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** Consulting demand can be cyclical, and AI could pressure labor-based delivery models faster than it creates new advisory work. The thesis needs evidence that bookings, pricing, and utilization are stabilizing, not merely that the multiple is low.

**Notable valuation signals:** P/FCF 6.68; EV/FCF 6.54; ROIC 27.09%; FCF yield 14.97%; net cash balance sheet.

### 3. PayPal (PYPL)

**Why it looks undervalued:** PayPal remains a classic quality-at-a-discount candidate. StockAnalysis showed a trailing P/E of 8.52, forward P/E of 8.39, P/FCF of 7.29, EV/FCF of 7.71, and FCF yield of 13.72%. The stock was down 39.61% over the prior 52 weeks.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** Despite competitive pressure in digital payments, PayPal still has strong financial efficiency: ROIC of 22.94%, ROE of 25.12%, operating margin of 18.18%, and $5.50B of trailing free cash flow. Buybacks are meaningful, with a 7.10% buyback yield.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** The risk is that branded checkout share, transaction margins, or user engagement continue to erode. PayPal is cheap enough to be interesting, but it needs proof that product execution and profitable growth are improving.

**Notable valuation signals:** P/FCF 7.29; EV/FCF 7.71; ROIC 22.94%; FCF yield 13.72%; buyback yield 7.10%.

### 4. Qualcomm (QCOM)

**Why it looks undervalued:** Qualcomm is not as optically cheap as Adobe, Accenture, PayPal, or Comcast, but it offers a better balance of quality, technology relevance, and valuation than many semiconductor peers. StockAnalysis showed a forward P/E of 17.91, P/FCF of 14.86, EV/FCF of 15.30, and FCF yield of 6.73%.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** The company continues to show strong returns and cash generation: ROIC of 32.94%, ROE of 36.08%, operating margin of 25.68%, FCF margin of 28.10%, and $12.50B of trailing free cash flow. Balance-sheet risk looks manageable, with debt/FCF of 1.22 and interest coverage of 16.82x.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** Smartphone cyclicality, customer concentration, China/geopolitical exposure, and execution risk in automotive, IoT, and AI edge chips remain central. The valuation is reasonable, not obviously distressed, so the margin of safety depends on continued durable earnings power.

**Notable valuation signals:** Forward P/E 17.91; P/FCF 14.86; ROIC 32.94%; FCF yield 6.73%.

### 5. Comcast (CMCSA)

**Why it looks undervalued:** Comcast is the deepest value candidate in today's shortlist. StockAnalysis showed a trailing P/E of 4.64, forward P/E of 6.75, P/FCF of 4.17, EV/FCF of 8.35, PB of 0.96, dividend yield of 5.55%, and FCF yield of 23.98%. The stock was down 34.52% over the prior 52 weeks.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** The cash engine is still real: trailing free cash flow was $20.38B, operating margin was 15.28%, gross margin was 70.13%, and ROE was 20.92%. Shareholder returns are meaningful, with a 10.44% shareholder yield from dividends plus buybacks.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** Comcast has the highest value-trap risk on this list. Broadband competition, cord-cutting, media asset pressure, and leverage are material. Debt/FCF was 4.64 and the Altman Z-Score was 1.43, so this is a cash-flow value case that requires confidence in debt service and business stabilization.

**Notable valuation signals:** P/FCF 4.17; EV/FCF 8.35; FCF yield 23.98%; dividend yield 5.55%; debt/FCF 4.64.

## Short Conclusion

The strongest current quality-value candidates are **Adobe** and **Accenture** because their cash-flow multiples look unusually low while returns on capital remain excellent. **PayPal** is also compelling, but requires more evidence that competitive pressure is stabilizing. **Qualcomm** is a more balanced quality idea with less extreme cheapness. **Comcast** has the largest statistical discount and cash yield, but also the clearest value-trap risk due to leverage and structural pressure.
