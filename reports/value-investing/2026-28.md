## Latest Update - 2026-07-11

### Short Market Context

The broad US market still requires selectivity. WSJ/Birinyi data for Friday, July 10, 2026 showed the S&P 500 at a trailing P/E of 25.62 and forward P/E of 21.68, while the Nasdaq 100 traded at a trailing P/E of 34.50. Value has also already had a strong year: iShares showed the Russell 1000 Value ETF (IWD) at a July 10 NAV of $246.90, near the top of its 52-week range of $192.86-$247.72, with YTD NAV total return of 18.00% as of July 9. Finviz's July 10 market snapshot showed US stocks closing higher on AI-chip enthusiasm, but breadth was mixed, with 48.5% of listed names advancing and 47.1% declining.

Today's scan was run fresh across US public stocks using a quality-value lens: free-cash-flow yield, earnings valuation, ROIC/ROE, balance-sheet resilience, margin durability, shareholder returns, and whether the discount appears tied to a solvable cyclical or execution issue rather than permanent impairment. I cross-checked the previous quality-value universe against current data, then added a separate quality-company screen from Morningstar's undervalued Best Companies to Own list. That check elevated Broadridge and reduced confidence in several statistically cheap consumer-defensive names whose current free-cash-flow and leverage profiles look less compelling.

### Today's Top Ideas

#### 1. Accenture (ACN)

**Why it looks undervalued:** Accenture remains the cleanest large-cap quality-value candidate in today's scan. StockAnalysis showed a July 10 close of $135.23, trailing P/E of 10.82, forward P/E of 9.43, P/FCF of 6.58, EV/FCF of 6.44, and FCF yield of 15.20%. The stock was down 54.53% over the prior 52 weeks, so the market is pricing in a severe consulting and IT-services slowdown.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** The company remains asset-light, cash-generative, and financially resilient. ROIC was 27.09%, ROE was 24.41%, free cash flow was $12.58B, FCF margin was 17.21%, debt/FCF was only 0.67, interest coverage was 43.37x, and the balance sheet carried net cash of $1.78B. The 4.69% dividend yield and 52.15% payout ratio add a tangible return component while investors wait for demand stabilization.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** Consulting demand can stay weak longer than expected. AI may also pressure labor-heavy delivery models before enough new advisory and implementation work offsets it. The evidence to watch is bookings, pricing, utilization, headcount discipline, and whether clients keep delaying transformation spending.

**Notable valuation signals:** P/FCF 6.58; EV/FCF 6.44; ROIC 27.09%; FCF yield 15.20%; net cash balance sheet.

#### 2. Adobe (ADBE)

**Why it looks undervalued:** Adobe still screens unusually cheap for a dominant software franchise. StockAnalysis showed a July 10 close of $223.64, trailing P/E of 12.79, forward P/E of 8.62, P/FCF of 8.65, EV/FCF of 8.79, and FCF yield of 11.56%. The stock was down 40.10% over 52 weeks, and the consensus rating was only Hold despite the low cash-flow multiple.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** Adobe's economics remain elite. ROIC was 60.74%, ROE was 62.95%, gross margin was 89.40%, operating margin was 36.72%, FCF margin was 40.80%, and trailing free cash flow was $10.28B. Shares outstanding declined 5.84% year over year.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** Generative AI is still the central uncertainty. If cheaper AI-native tools permanently weaken Adobe's pricing power, workflow control, or enterprise seat growth, the low multiple may be justified. Current ratio of 0.75 and net debt of $1.45B are watch items, though debt/FCF of 0.69 and interest coverage of 35.45x keep balance-sheet risk manageable.

**Notable valuation signals:** P/FCF 8.65; EV/FCF 8.79; ROIC 60.74%; FCF yield 11.56%; 52-week price change -40.10%.

#### 3. PayPal (PYPL)

**Why it looks undervalued:** PayPal remains inexpensive on both earnings and cash flow. StockAnalysis showed a July 10 close of $46.32, trailing P/E of 8.68, forward P/E of 8.54, P/FCF of 7.42, EV/FCF of 7.85, and FCF yield of 13.47%. The stock was down 38.10% over 52 weeks.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** The moat is more contested than Adobe's or Accenture's, but PayPal still generates meaningful cash and returns. ROIC was 22.94%, ROE was 25.12%, operating margin was 18.18%, free cash flow was $5.50B, debt/FCF was 2.12, and interest coverage was 13.66x. Shares outstanding fell 7.10% year over year, supporting an 8.34% shareholder yield.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** The key risk is that branded checkout share, transaction margin, or user engagement continue to erode. The valuation leaves room for a stabilization case, but the quality case depends on product execution and profitable growth, not buybacks alone. The next confirmed earnings date is July 28, 2026, so the thesis is close to a fresh test.

**Notable valuation signals:** P/FCF 7.42; EV/FCF 7.85; ROIC 22.94%; FCF yield 13.47%; buyback yield 7.10%.

#### 4. Zoetis (ZTS)

**Why it looks undervalued:** Zoetis remains attractive because its valuation is much lower than normally expected for a durable animal-health franchise. StockAnalysis showed a July 10 close of $75.56, trailing P/E of 12.37, forward P/E of 10.83, P/FCF of 14.16, EV/FCF of 17.43, and FCF yield of 7.06%. The stock was down 52.26% over 52 weeks.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** The business retains strong profitability and defensible end markets in livestock and companion-animal health. ROIC was 27.80%, ROE was 67.75%, gross margin was 71.84%, operating margin was 38.12%, FCF margin was 23.47%, Altman Z-Score was 6.21, and shareholder yield was 6.08%.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** Zoetis is not a deep-value stock on enterprise free cash flow. Debt/FCF was 4.14 and debt/equity was 2.86, while three-year revenue growth was forecast at only 3.87%. The thesis requires confidence that the franchise deserves a premium multiple again, not an assumption of fast growth recovery.

**Notable valuation signals:** Forward P/E 10.83; P/FCF 14.16; EV/FCF 17.43; ROIC 27.80%; FCF yield 7.06%; 52-week price change -52.26%.

#### 5. Broadridge Financial Solutions (BR)

**Why it looks undervalued:** Broadridge is today's main addition. It is less statistically cheap than Comcast or PayPal, but the quality-adjusted setup improved enough to enter the top five. StockAnalysis showed a July 10 close of $147.47, trailing P/E of 15.76, forward P/E of 14.77, P/FCF of 12.95, EV/FCF of 15.30, and FCF yield of 7.72%. The stock was down 38.24% over 52 weeks. Morningstar's April 2026 Best Companies to Own screen also listed Broadridge as one of the most undervalued high-quality companies, citing a 0.54 price/fair value ratio and a low uncertainty rating.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** Broadridge has a durable position in investor communications, proxy processing, and securities-processing technology. Morningstar described its proxy and interim services business as a dominant crown-jewel franchise. Current metrics support the quality case: ROIC was 16.75%, ROE was 42.31%, FCF margin was 17.99%, debt/FCF was 2.59, interest coverage was 11.10x, Altman Z-Score was 4.64, Piotroski F-Score was 7, and the company had 18 years of dividend growth.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** Broadridge's valuation is not a screaming bargain on EV/FCF, and debt is meaningful relative to cash. Organic growth, acquisition discipline, and margin execution matter. It should be penalized if recurring revenue growth slips materially below the mid-single-digit to high-single-digit framework Morningstar cited from management's investor-day targets.

**Notable valuation signals:** Forward P/E 14.77; P/FCF 12.95; EV/FCF 15.30; ROIC 16.75%; FCF yield 7.72%; 52-week price change -38.24%.

### Watchlist / Near Misses

- **Comcast (CMCSA):** Still the deepest statistical value candidate, with trailing P/E of 4.60, forward P/E of 6.72, P/FCF of 4.13, EV/FCF of 8.31, PB of 0.96, and $20.38B of trailing free cash flow. It moves to the watchlist today because ROIC was only 8.00%, debt/FCF was 4.64, interest coverage was 4.30x, and the structural risks in broadband, cable, and media remain more severe than the quality case for Broadridge.
- **Salesforce (CRM):** Still attractive for software, with forward P/E of 11.72, P/FCF of 9.12, EV/FCF of 11.22, and FCF of $14.66B. It stays below the top five because ROIC was only 11.07%, net debt was $30.71B, and the balance sheet changed materially.
- **Cigna (CI):** Cheap and defensive, with forward P/E of 9.50, P/FCF of 10.13, EV/FCF of 13.14, and beta of 0.30. Managed-care/PBM regulation, thin margins, working-capital intensity, and debt/FCF of 4.03 keep it below the cleaner quality names.
- **Campbell's (CPB) and Clorox (CLX):** Both appeared in Morningstar's undervalued quality-company work, but today's fundamental cross-check kept them out of the top five. Campbell's had debt/FCF of 10.93 and ROIC of 9.10%; Clorox had P/FCF of 30.73 and EV/FCF of 39.40 despite strong brands and ROIC.
- **Qualcomm (QCOM):** Excellent quality metrics, including ROIC of 32.94% and debt/FCF of 1.22, but valuation is less compelling after a positive 52-week move: forward P/E was 19.22, P/FCF was 15.95, and EV/FCF was 16.39.

### Changes From Earlier Updates This Week

- **Added to top five:** Broadridge enters because its current discount, resilient franchise, dividend record, and balance-sheet/quality metrics look more balanced than Comcast's deeper but riskier discount.
- **Moved down:** Comcast moves from the top five to the watchlist. The value signal is very strong, but today's fresh ranking gave more weight to quality and disconfirming structural risk.
- **Retained:** Accenture, Adobe, PayPal, and Zoetis remain in the top five after a fresh scan. Their valuation support and business-quality metrics still stand out despite different risk profiles.
- **Rejected despite external screens:** Campbell's and Clorox were reviewed because Morningstar flags them as cheap high-quality companies, but current FCF/leverage and growth-quality checks were not strong enough for today's top five.

### Short Conclusion

The strongest current candidates are **Accenture** and **Adobe**, where low cash-flow multiples are paired with unusually strong returns on capital. **PayPal** remains attractive but needs proof that competitive pressure is stabilizing. **Zoetis** adds a high-quality non-tech franchise with a major share-price reset. **Broadridge** replaces Comcast today as the fifth idea because its discount is smaller but its quality case looks cleaner. Comcast remains the most statistically cheap watchlist name, with the clearest value-trap risk.
