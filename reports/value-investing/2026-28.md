## Latest Update - 2026-07-10

Run date: 2026-07-10, before the US market open. This is research support only, not personal financial advice.

### Short Market Context

The broad US market still looks expensive enough that the value screen needs a strong quality filter. WSJ/Birinyi data showed the S&P 500 at a trailing P/E of 25.37 and forward P/E of 21.74 as of 2026-07-02, while the Nasdaq 100 traded at a trailing P/E of 35.22. Large-cap value has also already rallied: iShares showed IWD, the Russell 1000 Value ETF, with NAV total return of 17.44% YTD as of 2026-07-08 and NAV close to the top of its 52-week range on 2026-07-09.

Current earnings expectations also raise the bar. Axios, citing FactSet, reported that analysts expected S&P 500 second-quarter EPS growth of 22.5% year over year, with Q2 earnings season beginning July 14. That means a cheap-looking stock should be penalized if the thesis depends mainly on optimistic market-wide earnings growth rather than company-specific cash-flow support.

Today's scan was run fresh across the US public equity opportunity set. I emphasized free-cash-flow yield, earnings valuation, ROIC/ROE, balance-sheet risk, margin durability, shareholder returns, and whether the discount appears tied to a solvable cyclical or execution issue rather than permanent impairment. As a cross-check, Investing.com's July 2026 undervalued-stock screen again highlighted Accenture, Zoetis, PayPal, Adobe, Comcast, Intuit, and Cigna among S&P 500 companies with positive model-based upside and good or better financial health scores.

### Today's Top Ideas

#### 1. Accenture (ACN)

**Why it looks undervalued:** Accenture remains the cleanest large-cap quality-value candidate in today's scan. StockAnalysis showed a trailing P/E of 11.12, forward P/E of 9.70, P/FCF of 6.76, EV/FCF of 6.62, and FCF yield of 14.79%. The stock was down 54.16% over the prior 52 weeks, so the market is pricing in a serious consulting and IT-services slowdown.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** The company remains asset-light, cash generative, and financially resilient. ROIC was 27.09%, ROE was 24.41%, free cash flow was $12.58B, FCF margin was 17.21%, and Accenture had net cash of $1.78B. Interest coverage was 43.37x, debt/FCF was only 0.67, and the dividend yield was 4.69% with a 52.15% payout ratio.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** Consulting demand can stay weak longer than expected. AI may also pressure labor-heavy delivery models before it creates enough new advisory or implementation work. The evidence to watch is bookings, utilization, pricing, headcount discipline, and whether clients keep delaying transformation spending.

**Notable valuation signals:** P/FCF 6.76; EV/FCF 6.62; ROIC 27.09%; FCF yield 14.79%; net cash balance sheet.

#### 2. Adobe (ADBE)

**Why it looks undervalued:** Adobe still screens unusually cheap for a dominant software franchise. StockAnalysis showed a trailing P/E of 12.73, forward P/E of 8.59, P/FCF of 8.61, EV/FCF of 8.75, and FCF yield of 11.62%. The stock was down 41.75% over the prior 52 weeks, and the consensus rating was only Hold despite the low cash-flow multiple.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** Adobe's economics remain elite. ROIC was 60.74%, ROE was 62.95%, gross margin was 89.40%, operating margin was 36.72%, FCF margin was 40.80%, and trailing free cash flow was $10.28B. Shares outstanding declined 5.84% year over year.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** Generative AI is the central uncertainty. If cheaper AI-native tools permanently weaken Adobe's pricing power, creative workflow control, or enterprise seat growth, the low multiple may be justified. Current ratio of 0.75 and net debt of $1.45B are watch items, though debt/FCF of 0.69 and interest coverage of 35.45x keep balance-sheet risk manageable.

**Notable valuation signals:** P/FCF 8.61; EV/FCF 8.75; ROIC 60.74%; FCF yield 11.62%; 52-week price change -41.75%.

#### 3. PayPal (PYPL)

**Why it looks undervalued:** PayPal remains inexpensive on both earnings and cash flow. StockAnalysis showed a trailing P/E of 8.49, forward P/E of 8.35, P/FCF of 7.26, EV/FCF of 7.69, and FCF yield of 13.77%. The stock was down 39.60% over the prior 52 weeks.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** The moat is more contested than Adobe's or Accenture's, but PayPal still generates meaningful cash and returns. ROIC was 22.94%, ROE was 25.12%, operating margin was 18.18%, free cash flow was $5.50B, debt/FCF was 2.12, and interest coverage was 13.66x. Shares outstanding fell 7.10% year over year, supporting an 8.34% shareholder yield.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** The key risk is that branded checkout share, transaction margin, or user engagement continue to erode. The current valuation leaves room for a stabilization case, but the business-quality case depends on product execution and profitable growth, not buybacks alone.

**Notable valuation signals:** P/FCF 7.26; EV/FCF 7.69; ROIC 22.94%; FCF yield 13.77%; buyback yield 7.10%.

#### 4. Zoetis (ZTS)

**Why it looks undervalued:** Zoetis again ranks in the top five because its valuation is much lower than normally expected for a durable animal-health franchise. StockAnalysis showed a trailing P/E of 12.29, forward P/E of 10.76, P/FCF of 14.07, EV/FCF of 17.34, and FCF yield of 7.11%. The stock was down 52.42% over the prior 52 weeks, and Investing.com's July 2026 screen listed Zoetis among the highest model-upside undervalued S&P 500 names.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** The business retains strong profitability and defensible end markets in livestock and companion-animal health. ROIC was 27.80%, ROE was 67.75%, gross margin was 71.84%, operating margin was 38.12%, FCF margin was 23.47%, and shareholder yield was 6.08%.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** Zoetis is not a deep-value stock on enterprise free cash flow. Debt/FCF was 4.14, debt/equity was 2.86, and three-year revenue growth was forecast at only 3.87%, so the thesis requires confidence that the franchise deserves a premium multiple again rather than assuming a fast growth rebound.

**Notable valuation signals:** Forward P/E 10.76; P/FCF 14.07; EV/FCF 17.34; ROIC 27.80%; FCF yield 7.11%; 52-week price change -52.42%.

#### 5. Comcast (CMCSA)

**Why it looks undervalued:** Comcast remains the deepest statistical value candidate in today's top list. StockAnalysis showed a trailing P/E of 4.56, forward P/E of 6.65, P/FCF of 4.09, EV/FCF of 8.27, PB of 0.95, dividend yield of 5.65%, and FCF yield of 24.44%. The stock was down 35.15% over the prior 52 weeks.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** The cash engine remains substantial: trailing free cash flow was $20.38B, operating cash flow was $32.24B, gross margin was 70.13%, ROE was 20.92%, and shareholder yield was 10.55% from dividends and buybacks.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** Comcast has the clearest value-trap risk among today's top five. Broadband competition, cord-cutting, media pressure, weak expected revenue/EPS growth, and leverage all matter. Debt/FCF was 4.64, interest coverage was 4.30x, current ratio was 0.87, and three-year EPS growth was forecast at -1.50%, so the thesis depends on cash-flow durability and disciplined capital allocation.

**Notable valuation signals:** P/FCF 4.09; EV/FCF 8.27; FCF yield 24.44%; dividend yield 5.65%; debt/FCF 4.64.

### Watchlist / Near Misses

- **Salesforce (CRM):** Current valuation is attractive for a software business, with forward P/E of 11.67, P/FCF of 9.08, EV/FCF of 11.17, FCF yield of 11.02%, gross margin of 77.64%, and FCF margin of 34.23%. It stays just outside the top five because ROIC was only 11.07%, debt rose materially, net debt was $30.71B, and Altman Z-Score was 2.8.
- **Cigna (CI):** Cheap and steady, with forward P/E of 9.45, P/FCF of 10.08, FCF yield of 9.92%, shareholder yield of 6.97%, and beta of 0.30. It remains on watch because managed-care/PBM regulation, thin margins, working-capital risk, and debt/FCF of 4.03 reduce the quality score.
- **Gilead Sciences (GILD):** Still a high-quality defensive cash generator, with ROIC of 27.70%, debt/FCF of 2.17, and a low beta. It stays below the top five because the valuation is less forceful after a 21.41% 52-week gain: P/FCF was 16.36, EV/FCF was 17.48, and FCF yield was about 6.1%.
- **Qualcomm (QCOM):** Strong ROIC of 32.94%, debt/FCF of 1.22, and durable semiconductor IP keep it on the quality watchlist. It misses today's value hurdle because P/FCF was 16.11, EV/FCF was 16.55, and the stock was up 19.86% over 52 weeks.

### Changes From Earlier Updates This Week

- **Retained:** Accenture, Adobe, PayPal, Zoetis, and Comcast remain today's top five after a fresh scan. Their current valuation and quality signals still stand out more than the broader watchlist.
- **Ranking stability:** Accenture remains first because its combination of low cash-flow multiple, high ROIC, net cash, and dividend support is cleaner than Adobe's AI-disruption debate.
- **No new top-five addition today:** Salesforce and Cigna are credible alternatives, but Salesforce's lower ROIC and recent balance-sheet change, and Cigna's regulatory/margin risk, keep them below Zoetis and Comcast.
- **Risk emphasis:** Comcast still has the biggest headline discount, but it also has the highest value-trap risk. Its placement in the top five is based on cash-flow yield, not a high-confidence growth thesis.

### Short Conclusion

The strongest current candidates are **Accenture** and **Adobe**, where low cash-flow multiples are paired with unusually strong returns on capital. **PayPal** remains attractive but needs evidence that competitive pressure is stabilizing. **Zoetis** adds a higher-quality non-tech franchise with a major share-price reset. **Comcast** offers the largest statistical discount, but its leverage and structural pressures make it the least clean idea in the top five.
