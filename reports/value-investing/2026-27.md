# Value Investing Weekly Report - 2026-W27

Updated through: 2026-07-04  
Scope: fresh quality-value review of US-listed public equities.  
Important note: this is research support, not personal financial advice. Valuation conclusions are probabilistic and may be wrong if fundamentals deteriorate or if the market is correctly discounting risks not yet visible in reported numbers.

## Short Market Context

US equity markets were closed on Friday, July 3, 2026 for the Independence Day observance and today is Saturday, July 4, 2026. NYSE and Nasdaq calendars both list July 3, 2026 as closed, so today's update uses the latest available July 2 closing prices and company statistics checked on July 4.

The broad market is still not obviously cheap. FactSet's July 2 Earnings Insight put the S&P 500 forward 12-month P/E at 20.4x, above both the 5-year average of 19.9x and the 10-year average of 19.0x. FactSet also showed the S&P 500 closing level at 7,483.23 and a trailing 12-month P/E of 27.8x. That backdrop leaves limited margin for error and favors company-specific quality-value ideas rather than broad index exposure.

For the July 4 run, I started from a fresh quality-value screen across US-listed public equities. I prioritized low or reasonable earnings/free-cash-flow/enterprise-value multiples, high or resilient returns on capital, strong cash generation, manageable debt, and a plausible reason the current market price may be too skeptical. Prior same-week entries were reviewed only after today's ranking was complete.

## Today's Top Ideas - 2026-07-04

### 1. Arch Capital Group (ACGL)

**Why it looks undervalued:** ACGL last closed at $102.20 on July 2. StockAnalysis shows a market cap around $35.6 billion, trailing P/E of 7.83x, forward P/E of 10.94x, price/free-cash-flow of 6.07x, price/book of 1.54x, EV/EBITDA of 6.34x, and EV/free-cash-flow of 6.38x. Those multiples remain modest for a specialty insurer with strong underwriting results and high return metrics.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** StockAnalysis shows ROE of 21.31%, ROIC of 18.41%, debt/equity of 0.11, debt/free-cash-flow of 0.47x, and interest coverage of 38.15x. ACGL also generated about $5.86 billion of trailing free cash flow. Arch's Q1 2026 materials showed book value per share of $66.19, a combined ratio of 81.7%, and an underlying combined ratio excluding catastrophe activity and prior-year development of 82.3%.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** Insurance earnings are cyclical. Catastrophe losses, adverse reserve development, softer pricing, or investment losses could make today's low earnings multiple less durable than it appears. The screen still ranks ACGL first, but underwriting margins should not be treated as permanently fixed.

**Notable valuation signals:** ACGL combines low earnings and cash-flow multiples, high return metrics, low leverage, and a still-reasonable book multiple. It remains the cleanest quality-value candidate in today's run.

### 2. Adobe (ADBE)

**Why it looks undervalued:** ADBE last closed at $219.72 on July 2. StockAnalysis shows a market cap around $87.3 billion, trailing P/E of 12.57x, forward P/E of 8.47x, price/free-cash-flow of 8.50x, EV/EBITDA of 9.13x, and EV/free-cash-flow of 8.64x. These are unusually low multiples for a scaled software subscription franchise.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** Adobe reported record Q2 FY2026 revenue of $6.62 billion, up 13% year over year, and raised FY2026 revenue and non-GAAP EPS targets. The company also said AI-first ARR tripled year over year and exceeded $500 million. StockAnalysis shows ROIC of 60.74%, ROE of 62.95%, gross margin of 89.40%, free-cash-flow margin of 40.80%, trailing free cash flow of $10.28 billion, and debt/free-cash-flow of 0.69x.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** The market's concern is real: AI-native creative and marketing tools could pressure Adobe's pricing, seat growth, or retention. The thesis depends on AI becoming a demand amplifier for Adobe's platform rather than a long-term moat destroyer.

**Notable valuation signals:** ADBE still screens as a rare high-margin, high-ROIC software business at single-digit forward earnings and free-cash-flow multiples. That discount is attractive, but it is compensation for genuine disruption risk.

### 3. Intuit (INTU)

**Why it looks undervalued:** INTU last closed at $275.35 on July 2. StockAnalysis shows a trailing P/E of 16.84x, forward P/E of 10.28x, price/free-cash-flow of 9.71x, EV/EBITDA of 11.77x, and EV/free-cash-flow of 9.73x. The stock remains down about 64.7% over the past 52 weeks, so the valuation reset remains large despite the July 2 rebound.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** Intuit's fiscal Q3 2026 release showed total revenue of $8.6 billion, up 10%, with Global Business Solutions revenue up 15% and Online Ecosystem revenue up 19%. The company raised full-year fiscal 2026 revenue and non-GAAP guidance, repurchased $1.6 billion of stock in the quarter, and authorized a new $8 billion buyback. StockAnalysis shows ROIC of 20.63%, ROE of 22.50%, trailing free cash flow of $7.76 billion, free-cash-flow margin of 37.06%, current ratio of 1.45, and debt/free-cash-flow of 0.89x.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** AI tax-preparation and small-business finance tools may pressure TurboTax and QuickBooks over time. Intuit's announced 17% workforce reduction may improve efficiency, but it also adds execution and culture risk. This is a high-quality franchise at a distressed multiple, not a low-risk idea.

**Notable valuation signals:** INTU's roughly 10x forward P/E and EV/free-cash-flow are too low if double-digit revenue growth and high free-cash-flow conversion remain intact. The ranking stays behind ADBE because the AI disruption question is arguably more direct in tax preparation.

### 4. MarketAxess Holdings (MKTX)

**Why it looks undervalued:** MKTX last closed at $116.53 on July 2. StockAnalysis shows a market cap around $4.14 billion, trailing P/E of 13.79x, forward P/E of 14.31x, price/free-cash-flow of 15.30x, EV/EBITDA of 9.02x, and EV/free-cash-flow of 14.62x. The stock is down about 47.8% over the past 52 weeks, so the market is no longer valuing it like a premium market-infrastructure compounder.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** MarketAxess reported Q1 2026 record total revenue of $233.4 million, up 12%, with record total credit and total rates commission revenue. StockAnalysis shows ROIC of 32.43%, ROE of 24.30%, current ratio of 4.94, net cash of about $185.7 million, debt/free-cash-flow of 1.08x, interest coverage of 93.99x, operating margin of 41.43%, and free-cash-flow margin of 31.07%.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** Competition is the core risk. Q1 2026 materials showed pressure in fee capture, including lower total credit variable transaction fee per million. If dealers, competitors, or alternative protocols erode MarketAxess' network advantage, historical return metrics may overstate future quality.

**Notable valuation signals:** MKTX remains a credible quality-value idea because the balance sheet is strong, returns remain high, and the market is already pricing in a much tougher electronic credit-trading environment.

### 5. Bristol Myers Squibb (BMY)

**Why it looks undervalued:** BMY last closed at $58.13 on July 2. StockAnalysis shows a market cap around $118.7 billion, trailing P/E of 16.28x, forward P/E of 9.50x, price/free-cash-flow of 9.97x, EV/EBITDA of 8.13x, EV/free-cash-flow of 12.96x, and a dividend yield above 4%. The multiple remains inexpensive if the Growth Portfolio can offset legacy-product erosion.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** Bristol Myers' Q1 2026 release showed revenue up 3% to $11.5 billion, with Growth Portfolio revenue up 12% to $6.2 billion. StockAnalysis shows trailing free cash flow of $11.91 billion, ROIC of 20.88%, ROE of 38.73%, operating margin of 31.56%, free-cash-flow margin of 24.56%, and interest coverage of 8.48x.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** BMY is the least clean top-five idea. Debt is meaningful, with StockAnalysis showing debt/equity of 2.31 and debt/free-cash-flow of 3.90x. Patent-cliff pressure, generic erosion, pipeline setbacks, pricing pressure, and acquisition integration risk could overwhelm the cash-flow case.

**Notable valuation signals:** A sub-10x forward P/E, roughly 10x price/free-cash-flow, and 4%+ dividend yield provide valuation support. This remains a cash-flow-supported pharmaceutical transition story, not a pristine compounder.

## Watchlist, Not Top Ideas Today

### Qualcomm (QCOM)

QCOM remains a good business with substantial free cash flow, but it does not rank ahead of today's top five after the fresh screen. The latest quote showed a price of $176.25 and P/E near 18.9x. That is reasonable, not clearly distressed. Handset cyclicality, customer concentration, and the need to prove durable automotive, IoT, and AI-device diversification keep it on the watchlist.

### Elevance Health (ELV)

ELV remains defensive and large-scale, but its valuation and return profile are less compelling than the selected names. The latest quote showed a price of $417.89 and P/E around 17.7x. The managed-care group also carries regulatory, utilization, and reimbursement uncertainty, so ELV does not clear the quality-value threshold today.

### Gilead Sciences (GILD)

GILD still has strong cash generation, but the stock is not as asymmetric after a stronger 52-week move. The HIV franchise and oncology pipeline remain strengths, while pipeline uncertainty and acquired R&D volatility keep confidence moderate.

### Alphabet (GOOGL)

GOOGL is a high-quality business, but it did not qualify as a top value idea today. The latest quote showed a price of $359.91 and P/E near 27.5x. The business quality is excellent, yet the valuation is not as clearly discounted as ADBE or INTU.

### Comcast (CMCSA)

CMCSA remains statistically cheap, but I continue to exclude it from the top ideas because the cheapness is tied to structural concerns: broadband competition, video subscriber losses, leverage, and low expected growth. The cash-flow yield is eye-catching, but the quality-value test requires clearer evidence that the market is over-discounting those pressures.

## Conclusion

The strongest current candidates are ACGL, ADBE, and INTU. ACGL has the cleanest low-valuation, high-return, low-leverage profile. ADBE offers a large quality-franchise discount if AI disruption fears prove overdone. INTU has the biggest valuation reset relative to franchise quality, but it requires confidence that AI deepens the platform rather than replacing parts of it. MKTX remains compelling if electronic credit-trading competition stabilizes. BMY is a cash-flow-supported pharmaceutical transition story with enough valuation support to include, but with above-average balance-sheet and patent-cycle risk.
