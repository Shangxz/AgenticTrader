# Value Investing Weekly Report - 2026-W27

Updated through: 2026-07-02  
Scope: fresh quality-value review of US-listed public equities.  
Important note: this is research support, not personal financial advice. Valuation conclusions are probabilistic and may be wrong if fundamentals deteriorate or if the market is correctly discounting risks not yet visible in reported numbers.

## Short Market Context

US equities remain selective rather than broadly cheap. StreetStats showed the S&P 500 at 7,483.23 as of the July 1 close, with a forward P/E near 20.16x and trailing P/E near 24.82x. FactSet's latest Earnings Insight similarly put the S&P 500 forward 12-month P/E around 20.1x, modestly above both its 5-year and 10-year averages. The setup is better than the most expensive parts of 2025, but still leaves limited margin for error at the index level.

Style leadership has broadened. MarketWatch's July 1 value-stock review reported that the Russell 1000 Value Index returned about 16.3% in the first half of 2026, ahead of the S&P 500's roughly 10.2% and the Russell 1000 Growth Index's roughly 5.3%. Schwab's mid-year outlook also notes that earnings expectations have improved sharply, while sticky inflation, fiscal concerns, oil prices, and long-rate risk remain important constraints. This backdrop favors company-specific quality-value gaps over mechanical low-multiple buying.

For the July 2 run, I started from a fresh screen of US-listed equities using low valuation as the entry point, then required evidence of durable cash generation, high or resilient returns on capital, manageable leverage, and a plausible reason the market may be over-discounting the business. Prior same-week ideas were reviewed only after today's ranking was complete.

## Today's Top Ideas - 2026-07-02

### 1. Arch Capital Group (ACGL)

**Why it looks undervalued:** ACGL closed July 1 near $98.55. StockAnalysis, last checked July 2, shows a market cap around $34.3 billion, trailing P/E of 7.55x, forward P/E of 10.55x, price/free-cash-flow of 5.85x, price/book of 1.48x, EV/EBITDA of 6.12x, and EV/free-cash-flow of 6.16x. Those remain unusually modest multiples for a specialty insurer with strong underwriting results.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** StockAnalysis shows ROE of 21.31%, ROIC of 18.41%, debt/equity of 0.11, debt/free-cash-flow of 0.47x, and interest coverage of 38.15x. ACGL also generated about $5.86 billion of trailing free cash flow, and its Q1 2026 materials showed book value per share of $66.19 and an underlying combined ratio excluding catastrophe activity and prior-year development of 82.3%.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** Insurance earnings are cyclical and can be lumpy. Catastrophe losses, adverse reserve development, falling pricing power, or investment losses could make the current low earnings multiple less reliable. This is a high-quality underwriting thesis, not a guarantee that current margins are permanent.

**Notable valuation signals:** ACGL remains the cleanest all-around quality-value candidate in today's review: low earnings and cash-flow multiples, strong returns, low leverage, and shareholder shrink through buybacks.

### 2. Adobe (ADBE)

**Why it looks undervalued:** ADBE closed July 1 near $210.98 and traded around $214.50 pre-market on July 2. StockAnalysis shows a market cap around $83.9 billion, trailing P/E of 12.07x, forward P/E of 8.14x, price/free-cash-flow of 8.16x, EV/EBITDA of 8.77x, and EV/free-cash-flow of 8.30x. For a scaled subscription software franchise, those multiples imply a large discount to historical quality.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** Adobe reported record Q2 FY2026 revenue of $6.62 billion, up 13% year over year, raised FY2026 revenue and non-GAAP EPS targets, and said AI-first ARR tripled year over year and exceeded $500 million. StockAnalysis shows ROIC of 60.74%, ROE of 62.95%, gross margin of 89.40%, free-cash-flow margin of 40.80%, trailing free cash flow of $10.28 billion, debt/free-cash-flow of 0.69x, and a 5.84% year-over-year decline in share count.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** This remains a disruption-sensitive idea. AI-native creative and marketing tools could pressure Adobe's pricing, seat growth, or customer retention. Adobe's CFO transition also adds a governance and execution item to monitor. The valuation is compelling only if AI becomes more of a demand amplifier than a moat destroyer.

**Notable valuation signals:** ADBE screens as a rare case where a high-margin, high-ROIC software business is priced at single-digit forward earnings and free-cash-flow multiples. That is the opportunity, but also a sign that the market is pricing in real risk.

### 3. Intuit (INTU)

**Why it looks undervalued:** INTU closed July 1 near $267.08 and traded around $269.01 pre-market on July 2. StockAnalysis shows a 52-week price decline of about 66.09%, trailing P/E of 16.33x, forward P/E of 9.97x, price/free-cash-flow of 9.42x, EV/EBITDA of 11.42x, and EV/free-cash-flow of 9.44x. The valuation reset is now large enough to merit a top-tier quality-value ranking despite legitimate disruption concerns.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** Intuit's fiscal Q3 2026 release showed total revenue of $8.6 billion, up 10%, with Global Business Solutions revenue up 15% and Online Ecosystem revenue up 19%. The company raised full-year fiscal 2026 revenue and non-GAAP guidance, expected full-year revenue growth of roughly 13% to 14%, repurchased $1.6 billion of stock in the quarter, and authorized a new $8 billion buyback. StockAnalysis shows ROIC of 20.63%, ROE of 22.50%, trailing free cash flow of $7.76 billion, free-cash-flow margin of 37.06%, current ratio of 1.45, and debt/free-cash-flow below 1x.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** AI tax-preparation and small-business finance tools may pressure TurboTax and QuickBooks over time. Intuit also announced a 17% workforce reduction, which can improve efficiency but raises execution and morale risk. This is a high-quality franchise at a distressed multiple, but the disruption case has substance.

**Notable valuation signals:** INTU's sub-10x forward P/E and roughly 9x EV/free-cash-flow valuation look too low if the company can sustain double-digit revenue growth and high free-cash-flow conversion.

### 4. MarketAxess Holdings (MKTX)

**Why it looks undervalued:** MKTX closed July 1 near $114.49 and traded around $115.49 pre-market on July 2. StockAnalysis shows a market cap around $4.1 billion, a 52-week price decline of about 48.74%, trailing P/E of 13.55x, forward P/E of 14.06x, price/free-cash-flow of 15.03x, EV/EBITDA of 8.85x, and EV/free-cash-flow of 14.35x. The valuation is no longer pricing the company like a premium market-infrastructure compounder.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** MarketAxess reported Q1 2026 record total revenue of $233.4 million, up 12%, with record total credit and total rates commission revenue. It also reported operating margin of 43.2%, or 44.2% excluding notable items. StockAnalysis shows ROIC of 32.43%, ROE of 24.30%, current ratio of 4.94, net cash of about $185.7 million, debt/free-cash-flow of 1.08x, and interest coverage of 93.99x.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** Competitive pressure is the central risk. In Q1 2026, total credit variable transaction fee per million fell 5% year over year, a reminder that volume growth may not fully offset mix and pricing pressure. If dealers, competitors, or alternative protocols erode MarketAxess' network advantage, historical return metrics may overstate future quality.

**Notable valuation signals:** MKTX remains a credible quality-value idea because the balance sheet is strong, returns remain high, and the market is already discounting a much tougher electronic credit-trading environment.

### 5. Bristol Myers Squibb (BMY)

**Why it looks undervalued:** BMY closed July 1 near $56.44 and traded around $56.10 pre-market on July 2. StockAnalysis shows a market cap around $115.3 billion, trailing P/E of 15.80x, forward P/E of 9.22x, price/free-cash-flow of 9.68x, EV/EBITDA of 7.95x, EV/free-cash-flow of 12.67x, and a dividend yield around 4.47%. The multiple remains inexpensive if the Growth Portfolio can offset legacy-product erosion.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** Bristol Myers' Q1 2026 release showed revenue up 3% to $11.5 billion, with Growth Portfolio revenue up 12% to $6.2 billion. StockAnalysis shows trailing free cash flow of $11.91 billion, ROIC of 20.88%, ROE of 38.73%, operating margin of 31.56%, and interest coverage of 8.48x.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** BMY is the least clean top-five idea. Debt is meaningful, with StockAnalysis showing debt/equity of 2.31 and debt/free-cash-flow of 3.90x. Patent-cliff pressure, generic erosion, pipeline setbacks, and acquisition integration risk could overwhelm the cash-flow case. This is a discounted transition story, not a pristine compounder.

**Notable valuation signals:** A sub-10x forward P/E and roughly 10x price/free-cash-flow multiple provide a visible valuation cushion if the Growth Portfolio continues scaling and management uses free cash flow to strengthen the balance sheet.

## Watchlist, Not Top Ideas Today

### Elevance Health (ELV)

ELV improved enough to revisit, especially after a strong July 1 move, but it stays below the top five. StockAnalysis shows a forward P/E of 15.86x, price/free-cash-flow of 14.01x, ROIC of 8.63%, and EV/free-cash-flow of 17.45x. The business is defensive and large-scale, but current returns and valuation do not compare as favorably with ACGL, ADBE, INTU, MKTX, or BMY.

### Qualcomm (QCOM)

QCOM remains a credible quality watchlist name. Prior checks showed strong ROIC, substantial free cash flow, and manageable leverage, but the valuation is less clearly discounted than today's top software and insurer ideas. Handset cyclicality, customer concentration, and the need to prove durable automotive, IoT, and AI-related diversification keep it just outside the top group.

### Gilead Sciences (GILD)

GILD still has attractive profitability and cash generation, but after a stronger 52-week move its valuation is not as asymmetric as the top five. The HIV franchise and oncology pipeline remain strengths, while acquired R&D charges and long-term pipeline uncertainty keep confidence moderate.

### Comcast (CMCSA)

CMCSA remains statistically cheap, but I continue to exclude it from the top ideas because the cheapness is tied to structural concerns: broadband competition, video subscriber losses, leverage, and low expected growth. The cash-flow yield is eye-catching, but the quality-value test requires clearer evidence that the market is over-discounting those pressures.

## Conclusion

The strongest current candidates are ACGL, ADBE, and INTU. ACGL has the cleanest low-valuation, high-return, low-leverage profile. ADBE offers a large quality-franchise discount if AI disruption fears prove overdone. INTU has the biggest valuation reset relative to franchise quality, but it requires confidence that AI deepens the platform rather than replacing parts of it. MKTX remains compelling if electronic credit-trading competition stabilizes. BMY is a cash-flow-supported pharmaceutical transition story with enough valuation support to include, but with above-average balance-sheet and patent-cycle risk.
