# Value Investing Weekly Report - 2026-W27

Run date: 2026-06-29  
Scope: fresh quality-value review of US-listed public equities.  
Important note: this is research support, not personal financial advice. Valuation conclusions are probabilistic and may be wrong if fundamentals deteriorate or if the market is correctly discounting risks not yet visible in reported numbers.

## Short Market Context

US equities remain a selective market for value investors. Recent market commentary points to a still-rising index supported by strong earnings and AI-related capital spending, but also to elevated concentration and limited room for broad multiple expansion. That backdrop makes pure index-level cheapness hard to find; today's screen therefore emphasizes company-specific valuation gaps, free-cash-flow support, return quality, and balance-sheet resilience.

For this run, I treated low multiples as only a starting point. I favored businesses where current valuation appears modest relative to earning power and where the quality case is supported by durable franchises, strong cash generation, or improving operating evidence.

## Today's Top Ideas

### 1. Arch Capital Group (ACGL)

**Why it looks undervalued:** ACGL trades near $97.54 with a market cap around $35.1 billion and a trailing P/E near 7.5x. StockAnalysis also shows a forward P/E around 10.4x, price/free-cash-flow around 5.8x, and EV/EBITDA around 6.1x. Those are unusually modest multiples for a specialty insurer that is still producing strong underwriting returns.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** Arch's Q1 2026 underwriting results were strong. The company reported an 81.7% consolidated combined ratio, while its reinsurance segment combined ratio was 75.9%. Book value per common share was $66.19 at March 31, 2026, up 1.7% from year-end 2025. Reported return measures also remain attractive, with StockAnalysis showing ROE around 21.3% and ROIC around 18.4%.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** Insurance earnings can be lumpy. Catastrophe losses, reserve errors, softening pricing, or investment portfolio losses could quickly make a low P/E misleading. A large part of the thesis depends on underwriting discipline persisting through the cycle.

**Notable valuation signals:** Low earnings and cash-flow multiples, high ROE, and book-value growth make ACGL the strongest current quality-value candidate in today's screen.

### 2. Adobe (ADBE)

**Why it looks undervalued:** ADBE trades near $202.73 with a market cap around $81.6 billion and a trailing P/E near 11.6x. StockAnalysis shows forward P/E around 7.8x, price/free-cash-flow around 7.8x, and EV/free-cash-flow around 8.0x. For a high-margin software franchise, those multiples imply significant market skepticism about AI disruption, management transition, and growth durability.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** Adobe's latest reported quarter does not look like a broken business. The company reported record Q2 FY2026 revenue of $6.62 billion, up 13% year over year, or 11% in constant currency. Its subscription ecosystem, creative tooling, document workflow, and enterprise marketing products remain high-quality recurring-revenue assets.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** The bear case is serious: generative AI could pressure Adobe's creative moat, freemium AI adoption could dilute near-term monetization, and leadership changes add execution risk. The valuation is attractive only if Adobe can convert AI usage into durable paid demand without permanently impairing pricing power.

**Notable valuation signals:** The combination of double-digit revenue growth and a single-digit forward earnings/cash-flow multiple creates a favorable asymmetric setup, but confidence is lower than ACGL because the competitive transition is unusually important.

### 3. MarketAxess Holdings (MKTX)

**Why it looks undervalued:** MKTX trades near $112.54 with a market cap around $4.0 billion and a trailing P/E near 13.3x. StockAnalysis shows forward P/E around 13.8x, price/free-cash-flow around 14.8x, EV/EBITDA around 8.7x, and EV/free-cash-flow around 14.1x. The stock is far cheaper than its own historical market-implied quality profile.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** MarketAxess has a specialized electronic bond-trading network with network-effect characteristics. Q1 2026 results showed record total revenue of $233 million, up 12%, with growth outside US credit products and strong activity in block and portfolio trading. That supports the view that the franchise remains relevant despite competitive pressure.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** The key risk is structural competition. If electronic credit trading margins compress or if large dealers and rival platforms reduce MKTX's pricing power, historical returns may not be a good guide. Its quality case is strong but less bulletproof than in prior years.

**Notable valuation signals:** A durable market-infrastructure business at roughly mid-teens earnings and free-cash-flow multiples is compelling if revenue growth and take-rate pressure stabilize.

### 4. Bristol Myers Squibb (BMY)

**Why it looks undervalued:** BMY trades near $57.52 with a market cap around $117.5 billion and a trailing P/E near 16.1x. StockAnalysis shows trailing free cash flow of roughly $11.9 billion, or about $5.83 per share. The stock's cash-flow yield remains meaningful for a large pharmaceutical company with multiple late-stage and recently launched products.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** Q1 2026 revenue increased 3% to $11.5 billion, while the Growth Portfolio rose 12% to $6.2 billion, driven by newer products including Camzyos, Breyanzi, and Reblozyl. The quality case rests on BMY using its cash generation and pipeline to offset legacy-product erosion.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** This is not a clean compounder. The patent-cliff and generic-pressure risks are real, especially in the legacy portfolio. Debt from acquisitions and pipeline execution risk also reduce confidence. BMY belongs on the shortlist because the cash-flow support is tangible, but it should be sized mentally as a transition story rather than a no-drama franchise.

**Notable valuation signals:** Strong free cash flow and improving mix from the Growth Portfolio support the value case, but the market may be correct to demand a discount until the post-patent-cliff earnings base is clearer.

### 5. Elevance Health (ELV)

**Why it looks undervalued:** ELV trades near $395.18 with a market cap around $85.8 billion and a trailing P/E near 16.7x. StockAnalysis shows forward P/E around 15.1x, price/free-cash-flow around 13.3x, and price/sales around 0.4x. Those valuation levels are not deep-value cheap, but they look reasonable for a large managed-care and healthcare-services company if earnings quality remains intact.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** Elevance reported Q1 2026 operating cash flow of $4.3 billion, up $3.3 billion year over year, and raised full-year adjusted EPS guidance to at least $26.75. Its scale, Blue Cross Blue Shield affiliations in many markets, and Carelon services platform give it more quality than a simple low-multiple healthcare payer screen would show.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** Managed-care stocks face policy, utilization, reimbursement, and medical-cost-cycle risk. If medical cost trends worsen or regulation becomes less favorable, today's mid-teens multiple may not be cheap enough.

**Notable valuation signals:** ELV is a lower-upside but steadier quality-value candidate. It ranks below ACGL, ADBE, and MKTX because the discount is less dramatic, but the cash-flow and guidance support are solid.

## Watchlist, Not Top Ideas Today

### Nike (NKE)

Nike remains a high-quality global brand and appears on several moat-oriented value lists, but it did not make today's top shortlist. Its fiscal Q3 2026 revenue was flat at $11.3 billion, gross margin declined to 40.2%, and diluted EPS was $0.35. StockAnalysis shows a forward P/E around 25x and price/free-cash-flow above 50x. That combination makes the stock more of a turnaround watchlist name than a top quality-value idea today. The setup could improve if cash flow normalizes and evidence of the comeback plan becomes clearer.

## Conclusion

The strongest current candidates are ACGL and ADBE. ACGL has the cleanest mix of low valuation, underwriting quality, and high returns on equity. ADBE offers the largest quality-franchise discount if AI disruption fears prove overdone. MKTX is a smaller, more cyclical network-effect candidate with meaningful upside if competitive pressure stabilizes. BMY and ELV are lower-conviction healthcare value ideas supported by cash flow and scale, but both carry sector-specific risks that deserve a higher margin of safety.
