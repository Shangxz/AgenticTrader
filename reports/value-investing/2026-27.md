## Today's Top Ideas - 2026-07-05

### 1. Arch Capital Group (ACGL)

**Why it looks undervalued:** ACGL last closed at $102.20 on July 2. StockAnalysis shows a market cap of about $35.6 billion, trailing P/E of 7.83x, forward P/E of 10.94x, price/free-cash-flow of 6.07x, price/book of 1.54x, EV/EBITDA of 6.34x, and EV/free-cash-flow of 6.38x. Those multiples remain modest for a specialty insurer with strong recent underwriting results.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** StockAnalysis shows ROE of 21.31%, ROIC of 18.41%, debt/equity of 0.11, debt/free-cash-flow of 0.47x, interest coverage of 38.15x, and trailing free cash flow of about $5.86 billion. Arch's Q1 2026 release showed book value per share of $66.19, share repurchases of $783 million, and a combined ratio excluding catastrophe activity and prior-year development of 82.3%.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** Insurance earnings are cyclical. Catastrophe losses, adverse reserve development, weaker pricing, or investment losses could make today's low earnings multiple less durable than it appears. The current screen ranks ACGL first, but underwriting margins should not be treated as permanently fixed.

**Notable valuation signals:** ACGL combines low earnings and cash-flow multiples, high return metrics, low leverage, and a still-reasonable book multiple. It remains the cleanest quality-value candidate in today's run.

### 2. Adobe (ADBE)

**Why it looks undervalued:** ADBE last closed at $219.72 on July 2. StockAnalysis shows a market cap of about $87.3 billion, trailing P/E of 12.57x, forward P/E of 8.47x, price/free-cash-flow of 8.50x, EV/EBITDA of 9.13x, and EV/free-cash-flow of 8.64x. These are unusually low multiples for a scaled software subscription franchise.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** Adobe reported record Q2 FY2026 revenue of $6.62 billion, up 13% year over year, and raised FY2026 revenue and non-GAAP EPS targets. StockAnalysis shows ROIC of 60.74%, ROE of 62.95%, gross margin near 89%, trailing free cash flow of about $10.28 billion, debt/free-cash-flow of 0.69x, and a 5.84% year-over-year share-count reduction.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** The discount is not random. AI-native creative and marketing tools could pressure Adobe's pricing, seat growth, retention, or near-term monetization. The thesis depends on Adobe turning AI into a demand amplifier and workflow moat rather than allowing AI to erode the value of its core products.

**Notable valuation signals:** ADBE screens as a rare high-margin, high-ROIC software business at single-digit forward earnings and free-cash-flow multiples. That discount is attractive, but it is compensation for genuine disruption and execution risk.

### 3. Intuit (INTU)

**Why it looks undervalued:** INTU last closed at $275.35 on July 2. StockAnalysis shows trailing P/E of 16.84x, forward P/E of 10.28x, price/free-cash-flow of 9.71x, EV/EBITDA of 11.77x, and EV/free-cash-flow of 9.73x. The stock remains down sharply over the past year, so the valuation reset is still large relative to the company's historical franchise quality.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** Intuit's fiscal Q3 2026 release showed total revenue of $8.6 billion, up 10%, Global Business Solutions revenue up 15%, and Online Ecosystem revenue up 19%. The company raised full-year fiscal 2026 revenue and non-GAAP guidance, repurchased $1.6 billion of stock in the quarter, and authorized a new $8 billion buyback. StockAnalysis shows ROIC of 20.63%, ROE of 22.50%, trailing free cash flow of $7.76 billion, free-cash-flow margin of 37.06%, and net debt of only about $120 million.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** AI tax-preparation and small-business finance tools may pressure TurboTax, QuickBooks, or Credit Karma over time. Intuit's announced workforce reduction and restructuring may improve efficiency, but it adds execution and culture risk. This is a high-quality franchise at a distressed multiple, not a low-risk idea.

**Notable valuation signals:** INTU's roughly 10x forward P/E and EV/free-cash-flow are too low if double-digit revenue growth and high free-cash-flow conversion remain intact. It ranks behind Adobe because the disruption question in consumer tax preparation appears especially direct.

### 4. MarketAxess Holdings (MKTX)

**Why it looks undervalued:** MKTX last closed at $116.53 on July 2. StockAnalysis shows a market cap around $4.1 billion, trailing P/E of 13.79x, forward P/E of 14.31x, price/free-cash-flow of 15.30x, EV/EBITDA of 9.02x, and EV/free-cash-flow of 14.62x. The stock is down about 47.8% over the past 52 weeks, so the market is no longer valuing it like a premium market-infrastructure compounder.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** MarketAxess reported Q1 2026 revenue of $233.4 million, up about 12%, with record total commission revenue driven by record total credit and rates commission revenue. StockAnalysis shows ROIC of 32.43%, ROE of 24.30%, current ratio of 4.94, debt/free-cash-flow of 1.08x, interest coverage of 93.99x, operating margin above 41%, and free-cash-flow margin above 31%.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** Competition is the core risk. Dealer platforms, all-to-all alternatives, pricing pressure, and lower fee capture could erode the network advantage. If the market structure shift is permanent, historical return metrics may overstate future quality.

**Notable valuation signals:** MKTX remains a credible quality-value idea because the balance sheet is strong, returns remain high, and the market is already pricing in a much tougher electronic credit-trading environment.

### 5. Bristol Myers Squibb (BMY)

**Why it looks undervalued:** BMY last closed at $58.13 on July 2. StockAnalysis shows a market cap around $118.7 billion, trailing P/E of 16.28x, forward P/E of 9.50x, price/free-cash-flow of 9.97x, EV/EBITDA of 8.13x, EV/free-cash-flow of 12.96x, and a dividend yield above 4%. The multiple remains inexpensive if the Growth Portfolio can offset legacy-product erosion.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** Bristol Myers' Q1 2026 release showed revenue up 3% to $11.5 billion, with Growth Portfolio revenue up 12% to $6.2 billion. StockAnalysis shows trailing free cash flow of about $11.91 billion, ROIC of 20.88%, ROE of 38.73%, operating margin of 31.56%, free-cash-flow margin of 24.56%, and interest coverage of 8.48x.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** BMY is the least clean top-five idea. Debt is meaningful, with StockAnalysis showing debt/equity of 2.31 and debt/free-cash-flow of 3.90x. Patent-cliff pressure, generic erosion, pipeline setbacks, pricing pressure, and acquisition integration risk could overwhelm the cash-flow case.

**Notable valuation signals:** A sub-10x forward P/E, roughly 10x price/free-cash-flow, and 4%+ dividend yield provide valuation support. This remains a cash-flow-supported pharmaceutical transition story, not a pristine compounder.

## Watchlist, Not Top Ideas Today

### Qualcomm (QCOM)

QCOM remains a good business with substantial free cash flow, but it did not rank ahead of today's top five. The valuation is reasonable rather than clearly distressed, and handset cyclicality, customer concentration, licensing/regulatory risk, and the need to prove durable automotive, IoT, and AI-device diversification keep it on the watchlist.

### Gilead Sciences (GILD)

GILD still has strong cash generation and defensive qualities, but it is less asymmetric after a stronger 52-week move. The HIV franchise and oncology pipeline remain strengths, while pipeline uncertainty, acquired R&D volatility, and health-care sector earnings revisions keep confidence moderate.

### Alphabet (GOOGL)

GOOGL remains a very high-quality business, but its current valuation is not as clearly discounted as ADBE or INTU. AI search disruption and regulatory pressure are real risks, while the core business quality and cash generation remain exceptional.

### Elevance Health (ELV)

ELV remains defensive and large-scale, but its valuation and return profile were less compelling than the selected names. Managed-care regulation, utilization trends, reimbursement uncertainty, and political risk keep it below the top-five threshold today.

### Comcast (CMCSA)

CMCSA remains statistically cheap, but I continue to exclude it from the top ideas because the cheapness is tied to structural concerns: broadband competition, video subscriber losses, leverage, and low expected growth. The cash-flow yield is notable, but the quality-value test requires clearer evidence that the market is over-discounting those pressures.

## Conclusion

The strongest current candidates are ACGL, ADBE, and INTU. ACGL has the cleanest low-valuation, high-return, low-leverage profile. ADBE offers a large quality-franchise discount if AI disruption fears prove overdone. INTU has the biggest valuation reset relative to franchise quality, but it requires confidence that AI deepens the platform rather than replacing parts of it. MKTX remains compelling if electronic credit-trading competition stabilizes. BMY is a cash-flow-supported pharmaceutical transition story with enough valuation support to include, but with above-average balance-sheet and patent-cycle risk.
