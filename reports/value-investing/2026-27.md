# Value Investing Weekly Report - 2026-W27

Updated through: 2026-06-30  
Scope: fresh quality-value review of US-listed public equities.  
Important note: this is research support, not personal financial advice. Valuation conclusions are probabilistic and may be wrong if fundamentals deteriorate or if the market is correctly discounting risks not yet visible in reported numbers.

## Short Market Context

US equities remain a selective market for value investors. The broad market is not obviously cheap: StreetStats showed the S&P 500 at 7,354.02 as of the June 26 close, with a forward P/E of 19.9x and a trailing P/E of 24.5x. Schwab's mid-year outlook also notes that 2026 earnings estimates have surged, but that estimate strength has been uneven and concentrated in AI infrastructure, technology, communication services, energy, and materials.

That backdrop argues against buying low multiples mechanically. For this run, I used low valuation only as a starting filter, then favored companies with strong or defensible returns on capital, healthy free cash flow, reasonable debt, and a plausible reason why current market pricing may understate medium-term value.

## Today's Top Ideas - 2026-06-30

### 1. Arch Capital Group (ACGL)

**Why it looks undervalued:** ACGL trades near $98.06, with a market cap around $35.3 billion and a trailing P/E near 7.5x. StockAnalysis shows a forward P/E around 10.5x, price/free-cash-flow around 5.8x, price/book around 1.5x, and EV/EBITDA around 6.1x. Those valuation levels remain modest for a specialty insurer producing strong underwriting returns.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** Arch's Q1 2026 results showed an 81.7% consolidated combined ratio and book value per common share of $66.19, up 1.7% from year-end 2025. StockAnalysis shows ROE around 21.3%, ROIC around 18.4%, debt/equity around 0.11, and interest coverage above 38x. The quality case rests on underwriting discipline, conservative balance-sheet risk, and long-term compounding of book value.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** Insurance earnings can be cyclical and volatile. Catastrophe losses, reserve errors, pricing softness, or investment losses could make the current low P/E less meaningful. The thesis depends on underwriting discipline persisting through the cycle.

**Notable valuation signals:** ACGL has the cleanest blend in today's screen: low earnings and cash-flow multiples, high return metrics, low leverage, and a stock that has not been pulled into the highest-multiple parts of the market.

### 2. Adobe (ADBE)

**Why it looks undervalued:** ADBE trades near $206.43, with a market cap around $83.1 billion and a trailing P/E near 11.8x. StockAnalysis shows a forward P/E around 8.0x, price/free-cash-flow around 8.0x, EV/EBITDA around 8.6x, and EV/free-cash-flow around 8.1x. Those are unusually low multiples for a scaled software franchise with high recurring revenue and strong margins.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** Adobe's Q2 FY2026 release reported record revenue of $6.62 billion, up 13% year over year, or 11% in constant currency. StockAnalysis shows ROIC around 60.7%, ROE around 63.0%, interest coverage above 35x, and trailing free cash flow of roughly $10.3 billion. The business still looks highly cash-generative even while the market worries about generative AI disruption.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** This is the highest-disruption idea on the list. If AI-native tools compress Adobe's pricing power or if freemium AI adoption fails to convert into durable paid demand, the low multiple may be justified. The thesis requires Adobe to defend workflow lock-in and monetize AI usage without permanently impairing margins.

**Notable valuation signals:** Adobe's combination of double-digit revenue growth, high ROIC, shrinking share count, and single-digit forward earnings/free-cash-flow multiples creates one of the more compelling quality-franchise discounts in the US market today.

### 3. MarketAxess Holdings (MKTX)

**Why it looks undervalued:** MKTX trades near $109.93, with a market cap around $3.9 billion and a trailing P/E near 13.0x. StockAnalysis shows a forward P/E around 13.5x, price/free-cash-flow around 14.4x, EV/EBITDA around 8.5x, and EV/free-cash-flow around 13.8x. The stock has fallen roughly 50% over the past year, leaving a formerly premium market-infrastructure franchise priced much more like a challenged cyclical.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** MarketAxess reported Q1 2026 revenue of $233 million, up 12%, driven by growth outside US credit products and stronger block and portfolio trading activity. StockAnalysis shows ROIC around 32.4%, ROE around 24.3%, a current ratio near 4.9, net cash, and interest coverage near 94x. The company still has attractive economics if its electronic bond-trading network remains competitively relevant.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** Competitive pressure is the central risk. If rivals, large dealers, or alternative protocols permanently reduce MarketAxess' take rates and liquidity advantage, historical return metrics may overstate future quality. This is a quality-value idea, but not a low-risk one.

**Notable valuation signals:** MKTX ranks highly because the balance sheet is strong, returns remain high, and the current valuation already discounts a much tougher competitive backdrop.

### 4. Bristol Myers Squibb (BMY)

**Why it looks undervalued:** BMY trades near $58.56, with a market cap around $119.6 billion and a trailing P/E near 16.4x. StockAnalysis shows a forward P/E around 9.6x, price/free-cash-flow around 10.0x, EV/EBITDA around 8.2x, and EV/free-cash-flow around 13.0x. The valuation remains inexpensive for a large pharmaceutical company if the Growth Portfolio can offset legacy-product erosion.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** BMY's Q1 2026 revenue increased 3% to $11.5 billion, while Growth Portfolio revenue rose 12% to $6.2 billion. StockAnalysis shows trailing free cash flow of roughly $11.9 billion, ROIC around 20.9%, and interest coverage around 8.5x. The quality case is not pristine, but the company has meaningful cash generation and a transition portfolio with observable growth.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** The patent-cliff risk remains real. Generic pressure, pipeline disappointments, acquisition debt, and integration risk could overwhelm the cash-flow case. BMY should be viewed as a discounted transition story rather than a simple high-quality compounder.

**Notable valuation signals:** A sub-10x forward P/E and roughly 10x price/free-cash-flow multiple give investors a tangible margin of safety if the growth portfolio continues to build enough scale.

### 5. Qualcomm (QCOM)

**Why it looks undervalued:** QCOM trades near $188.72, with a market cap around $202.3 billion and a trailing P/E around 20x. StockAnalysis shows a forward P/E around 19.2x, price/free-cash-flow around 15.9x, EV/EBITDA around 15.7x, and EV/free-cash-flow around 16.4x. This is not the cheapest stock in the screen, but the valuation looks reasonable relative to its return profile and optionality outside handsets.

**Why it still qualifies as quality:** Qualcomm reported fiscal Q2 2026 revenue of $10.6 billion and non-GAAP EPS of $2.65. The company also highlighted record quarterly QCT automotive revenue, 20% year-over-year growth in combined QCT Automotive and IoT revenue, $5.4 billion of share repurchases in the first half of fiscal 2026, and a new $20 billion authorization. StockAnalysis shows ROIC around 32.9%, ROE around 36.1%, debt/EBITDA around 1.2x, and trailing free cash flow of roughly $12.5 billion.

**Main risks or disconfirming factors:** Handset concentration and customer concentration remain important risks. Q2 commentary and third-party summaries point to softer handset demand, while AI/data-center and automotive opportunities still need to prove durable scale. A mid-teens free-cash-flow multiple is attractive only if diversification can keep offsetting mature smartphone-cycle pressure.

**Notable valuation signals:** QCOM earns today's final slot because its high returns, strong cash generation, active buybacks, and automotive/IoT diversification outweigh the fact that it is less statistically cheap than ACGL, ADBE, MKTX, or BMY.

## Watchlist, Not Top Ideas Today

### Elevance Health (ELV)

ELV remains a credible value watchlist name, but it dropped out of today's top five. The shares trade near $388.03, with StockAnalysis showing a forward P/E around 14.8x and price/free-cash-flow around 13.1x. Q1 2026 results were solid, including raised adjusted EPS guidance to at least $26.75. However, today's fresh quality-value ranking penalized its lower ROIC, higher debt/FCF, and managed-care policy/utilization risk relative to Qualcomm's stronger return profile and cash-generation metrics.

### Nike (NKE)

Nike remains a high-quality global brand and appears on moat-oriented value screens, but it still does not qualify as a top quality-value idea today. The turnaround needs clearer free-cash-flow normalization and margin recovery before the current valuation offers enough margin of safety.

## Conclusion

The strongest current candidates are ACGL and ADBE. ACGL has the cleanest low-valuation, high-return, low-leverage profile. ADBE offers the largest quality-franchise discount if AI disruption fears prove overdone. MKTX remains compelling but depends on competitive stabilization in electronic credit trading. BMY is a cash-flow-supported pharmaceutical transition story. QCOM is the highest-quality addition today, with stronger return metrics than most large-cap value candidates, but it requires confidence that automotive, IoT, and AI-related diversification can offset handset-cycle risk.
