# Value Investing Weekly Report - 2026-W26

_Run date: 2026-06-24_

This report is research support, not personal financial advice. The shortlist below was built from a fresh quality-value review of U.S.-listed public equities using current public information available during this run. Earlier reports, if any, were not used to anchor today's selection.

## Short Market Context

U.S. equities remain strong but concentrated. S&P Dow Jones Indices showed the S&P 500 at 7,365.46 on June 23, 2026, with a year-to-date gain still positive despite a weak daily move. Recent market commentary continues to flag AI- and mega-cap concentration risk, while value has had periods of relative strength in 2026 but still contains uneven quality. That makes stock-specific quality control important: the best candidates today are not simply the lowest multiples, but companies where cash generation, competitive position, and balance-sheet durability reduce the odds of a value trap.

Today's screen emphasized: valuation versus earnings, free cash flow, fair-value estimates where available, return on capital or underwriting quality, balance-sheet resilience, and a plausible medium-term reason for market pessimism to fade.

## Today's Top Ideas

### 1. Thermo Fisher Scientific (TMO)

- **Why it looks undervalued:** TMO traded around $469 on June 24, about 27% below its January 2026 52-week high of $643.99. A recent cash-flow valuation cited by Simply Wall St indicated roughly 20% undervaluation, and Morningstar has recently highlighted Thermo Fisher as a 4-star stock. The market appears to be discounting a slower life-science tools cycle.
- **Why it still qualifies as quality:** Thermo Fisher remains a scale leader in life-science tools and diagnostics. Q1 2026 revenue grew 6% to $11.01 billion, adjusted operating margin held near 21.8%, and adjusted EPS grew 6%. The business has high switching costs, recurring consumables exposure, and a long record of disciplined acquisitions.
- **Risks / disconfirming factors:** Organic growth was only 1% in Q1, debt is meaningful after acquisitions, and biopharma funding weakness could last longer than expected. A higher-quality business can still be dead money if end-market recovery is delayed.
- **Valuation signals:** Current P/E near 25.8 on trailing earnings, but adjusted EPS guidance around the mid-$24 to low-$25 range implies a materially lower forward multiple. Current price is well below prior high and below several external fair-value estimates.

### 2. Danaher (DHR)

- **Why it looks undervalued:** Morningstar's recent blue-chip screen described Danaher as about 13% below its $270 fair value estimate. The current market price around $179 on June 23-24 leaves a wider apparent gap, though differences may reflect timing and data updates.
- **Why it still qualifies as quality:** Danaher combines life-science and diagnostics exposure with a long-running operating system, recurring revenue components, and strong cash conversion. Q1 2026 operating cash flow was $1.3 billion and non-GAAP free cash flow was $1.1 billion, with free cash flow roughly 105% of net earnings.
- **Risks / disconfirming factors:** Core revenue growth was modest, Q1 revenue missed some expectations, and acquisition integration risk is elevated, including the announced Masimo deal. The case depends on end-market normalization and continued capital allocation discipline.
- **Valuation signals:** Normalized P/E and price/cash-flow metrics appear reasonable for a wide-moat compounder, but trailing GAAP P/E is less optically cheap. This is a quality-at-a-discount idea, not a deep-value idea.

### 3. Arch Capital Group (ACGL)

- **Why it looks undervalued:** ACGL traded around $93.71 on June 24 with a trailing P/E near 7.2. GuruFocus recently estimated fair value near $124 and described the stock as modestly undervalued.
- **Why it still qualifies as quality:** Arch has a strong specialty insurance and reinsurance franchise. Q1 2026 showed solid underwriting discipline: the insurance segment combined ratio improved to 96.5%, while the reinsurance segment combined ratio was 75.9%, helped by lower catastrophe losses and favorable prior-year development.
- **Risks / disconfirming factors:** Insurance earnings are inherently cyclical and catastrophe-sensitive. Favorable reserve development and lower catastrophe losses may not repeat. The stock can look statistically cheap near the top of a pricing cycle.
- **Valuation signals:** Low P/E relative to both the market and the insurance industry median supports the valuation case, but book-value growth and reserve quality should be monitored more closely than earnings multiple alone.

### 4. Medtronic (MDT)

- **Why it looks undervalued:** MDT traded around $80.63 on June 24. Barron's recently highlighted the stock at roughly 14 times earnings with a dividend yield around 3.7% and argued that the market is underpricing a rebound.
- **Why it still qualifies as quality:** Medtronic has broad medical-device scale, large installed bases, and improving innovation momentum. FY2026 revenue grew 8.4% to $36.4 billion, the strongest annual revenue growth in a decade, while Q4 sales rose 9.9% and organic sales rose 6.6%. The company also continues to return cash to shareholders.
- **Risks / disconfirming factors:** Medtronic has a history of investor disappointment, guidance for the coming year was slightly below some analyst expectations, and execution in diabetes separation, pulsed-field ablation, and robotics remains important.
- **Valuation signals:** The trailing GAAP P/E looks higher because GAAP EPS is depressed, but the adjusted earnings multiple and dividend yield are attractive for a durable healthcare franchise.

### 5. Alphabet (GOOGL)

- **Why it looks undervalued:** GOOGL traded around $346.13 on June 24 with a trailing P/E near 26.4. Morningstar recently rated Alphabet 4 stars and estimated fair value at $433, implying roughly 20% upside to that estimate.
- **Why it still qualifies as quality:** Alphabet retains dominant positions in search, YouTube, Android, cloud infrastructure, and AI research. Morningstar data shows very high profitability metrics, including normalized ROIC above 30% and strong interest coverage.
- **Risks / disconfirming factors:** Antitrust pressure, AI-driven search disruption, and heavy AI capital expenditure could reduce future free cash flow conversion. This is not classic low-multiple value; it is a high-quality compounder whose valuation still appears reasonable relative to durability.
- **Valuation signals:** The multiple is below many mega-cap AI peers while profitability and balance-sheet strength remain exceptional. Alphabet screens best as quality value within large-cap technology.

### 6. Elevance Health (ELV)

- **Why it looks undervalued:** ELV traded around $395.10 on June 24 with a trailing P/E near 16.7. Management reaffirmed FY2026 adjusted EPS guidance of at least $26.75, implying a lower forward earnings multiple if guidance holds.
- **Why it still qualifies as quality:** Elevance is a major health insurer with scale, diversified health-benefits exposure, and Carelon growth optionality. Q1 2026 operating revenue was $49.5 billion, up 1.5%, and the company reaffirmed operating cash flow of at least $5.5 billion.
- **Risks / disconfirming factors:** Medical cost pressure, Medicaid margin weakness, Medicare Advantage scrutiny, and regulatory risk are real. This idea has a bigger disconfirming-risk load than TMO, DHR, or GOOGL.
- **Valuation signals:** Forward earnings yield is attractive if the company can stabilize medical costs and execute on Carelon, but the low valuation is partly compensation for genuine policy and utilization risk.

### 7. Mondelez International (MDLZ)

- **Why it looks undervalued:** MDLZ traded around $61.06 on June 24. Morningstar recently showed a $73 fair value estimate and price/fair value around 0.84. Another recent cash-flow analysis started from roughly $2.53 billion of trailing free cash flow.
- **Why it still qualifies as quality:** Mondelez owns durable global snack brands with scale, distribution advantages, and resilient demand. The business is defensive relative to many cyclicals and can protect margins over time through pricing and mix.
- **Risks / disconfirming factors:** Cocoa and other input costs, currency, emerging-market volatility, and volume elasticity can pressure margins. The trailing P/E appears high, so the thesis depends on normalized earnings and cash flow rather than headline GAAP multiple.
- **Valuation signals:** The fair-value discount is moderate, but the quality and defensive profile make MDLZ a cleaner value candidate than many lower-multiple consumer names with weaker brands.

## Not Selected / Watchlist

- **Exxon Mobil (XOM):** Strong asset base, shareholder returns, and likely free cash flow resilience, but current valuation depends heavily on oil and gas assumptions. Recent Q1 2026 earnings were pressured, and the stock is less clearly mispriced after the energy rally.
- **NXP Semiconductors (NXPI):** Q1 2026 results were strong and free cash flow was healthy, but DCF evidence is mixed, including at least one recent analysis suggesting overvaluation after the stock's rebound. Keep on watch rather than top shortlist today.
- **Campbell's / Kraft Heinz / Bath & Body Works:** These screen optically cheap in some external value lists, but the quality-value threshold is less compelling today because brand, growth, leverage, or structural concerns increase value-trap risk.

## Conclusion

The strongest current quality-value candidates are **Thermo Fisher Scientific (TMO)** and **Danaher (DHR)** among healthcare tools, **Arch Capital (ACGL)** among financials, and **Medtronic (MDT)** among defensive healthcare turnarounds. **Alphabet (GOOGL)** remains the best large-cap technology quality-value candidate despite not being a traditional low-multiple stock. **Elevance (ELV)** and **Mondelez (MDLZ)** are attractive but require closer monitoring of medical-cost and input-cost risks, respectively.
