_Last updated: 2026-06-25_

This report is research support, not personal financial advice. ## 2026-06-25 Fresh Market Context

U.S. equities remain strong but narrow, with the market still highly sensitive to AI-related earnings, rates, inflation, and geopolitical energy prices. On June 25, stock futures were rebounding after three lower closes for the S&P 500 and Nasdaq, helped by Micron's AI-driven earnings beat and Qualcomm's sharply higher long-term non-handset targets. At the same time, inflation risk remains present, with the market watching May PCE data and the possibility of tighter Federal Reserve policy. J.P. Morgan also raised its S&P 500 year-end target while warning about crowding and flash-crash risk in speculative AI trades.

That backdrop argues for a quality-value filter rather than pure low multiples: durable cash flow, defensible market position, manageable balance-sheet risk, and a valuation that does not already require heroic growth assumptions.

## Today's Top Ideas

### 1. Thermo Fisher Scientific (TMO)

- **Why it looks undervalued:** TMO traded around $492 on June 25, still well below its January 2026 52-week high cited in the prior run. Its current trailing P/E is about 27, but management's 2026 adjusted EPS guidance range of $24.64-$25.12 implies a materially lower forward multiple for a scaled life-science leader. The stock remains a quality-at-a-discount candidate rather than a deep-value security.
- **Why it still qualifies as quality:** Q1 2026 revenue rose 6% to $11.01 billion, adjusted EPS rose 6% to $5.44, and adjusted operating margin held at 21.8%. The business has scale, recurring consumables exposure, sticky customer workflows, and a long record of disciplined capital allocation.
- **Risks / disconfirming factors:** Organic growth was still modest, biopharma funding and academic research budgets can remain soft, and acquisition-related debt raises the need for continued cash conversion. If the life-science tools cycle does not normalize, the valuation gap may close slowly.
- **Valuation signals:** About $492 current price, trailing P/E near 27, and forward adjusted EPS guidance near $25 support a forward earnings multiple around the high teens to low 20s, depending on the exact EPS point used.

### 2. Danaher (DHR)

- **Why it looks undervalued:** DHR traded around $189 on June 25. The trailing GAAP P/E is not optically cheap, but adjusted earnings and free cash flow remain much stronger than GAAP presentation suggests. This is a high-quality compounder priced for a slower bioprocessing and diagnostics cycle, not a cigar-butt value idea.
- **Why it still qualifies as quality:** Q1 2026 revenue increased 3.5% to $6.0 billion, adjusted EPS rose 9.5% to $2.06, operating cash flow was $1.3 billion, and non-GAAP free cash flow was $1.1 billion. Free cash flow was roughly 105% of net earnings, a useful quality signal.
- **Risks / disconfirming factors:** Core revenue growth was only 0.5%, diagnostics weakness remains a drag, and the Masimo acquisition adds integration and capital allocation risk. The valuation case depends on normalizing end-market demand and continued Danaher Business System execution.
- **Valuation signals:** Current price near $189, with management raising full-year adjusted EPS guidance after Q1. The multiple is not statistically cheap, but appears reasonable for a wide-moat cash generator if growth returns.

### 3. Arch Capital Group (ACGL)

- **Why it looks undervalued:** ACGL traded around $95 on June 25 with a trailing P/E near 7.3. The discount is more obvious than in many higher-quality names, though insurance earnings require cycle adjustment.
- **Why it still qualifies as quality:** Arch has a strong specialty insurance and reinsurance franchise. Q1 2026 net income rose to about $1.04 billion, book value per share increased to $66.19, and the combined ratio improved to 81.7%, helped by lower catastrophe losses and favorable reserve development.
- **Risks / disconfirming factors:** Insurance earnings can be flattered by benign catastrophe periods and reserve releases. Pricing cycles, catastrophe losses, and reserve adequacy matter more than the headline P/E. A low multiple alone is not enough.
- **Valuation signals:** Current price near $95, trailing P/E around 7, and price/book around the mid-1x range using March book value. The underwriting record supports keeping ACGL on the top list, but book-value growth should be monitored closely.

### 4. Medtronic (MDT)

- **Why it looks undervalued:** MDT traded around $80 on June 25. Based on FY2026 non-GAAP EPS of $5.53, the stock trades at roughly 14-15 times adjusted earnings, with a dividend yield that remains attractive for a durable medical-device franchise.
- **Why it still qualifies as quality:** FY2026 revenue grew 8.4% to $36.4 billion, or 5.8% organically, the company's strongest annual revenue growth in a decade. Q4 organic sales rose 6.6%, cardiovascular remained a clear growth driver, and the company continues to invest around cardiac ablation and other procedure-driven platforms.
- **Risks / disconfirming factors:** Management's FY2027 adjusted EPS guidance of $5.90-$6.00 was slightly below some analyst expectations, and Medtronic has a history of execution disappointments. The diabetes separation, robotics progress, pulsed-field ablation competition, and tariff impacts all matter.
- **Valuation signals:** Current price near $80 and FY2026 adjusted EPS of $5.53 imply a mid-teens adjusted earnings multiple. The valuation looks reasonable if the revenue reacceleration proves durable.

### 5. Alphabet (GOOGL)

- **Why it looks undervalued:** GOOGL traded around $345 on June 25 with a trailing P/E near 26.3. That is not classic low-multiple value, but it remains reasonable relative to the company's profitability, balance sheet, and AI/search/cloud optionality.
- **Why it still qualifies as quality:** Q1 2026 operating income increased 30%, operating margin expanded to 36.1%, free cash flow was $10.1 billion for the quarter and $64.4 billion for the trailing 12 months, and Alphabet ended the quarter with $126.8 billion in cash and marketable securities. The core franchise remains one of the highest-quality assets in public equities.
- **Risks / disconfirming factors:** AI could pressure search monetization, capital expenditures are very large, and antitrust risk remains a serious overhang. Reported Q1 EPS also benefited heavily from unrealized gains, so operating income and free cash flow are cleaner signals than net income growth.
- **Valuation signals:** Current price near $345, trailing P/E around 26, and TTM free cash flow above $64 billion. Alphabet remains the best large-cap technology quality-value candidate in this scan, despite not being a traditional value stock.

### 6. Qualcomm (QCOM)

- **Why it looks undervalued:** QCOM traded around $197 on June 25 with a trailing P/E near 21. The stock has rallied on its investor-day update, but the valuation still looks moderate if management's diversification targets are even partly credible.
- **Why it still qualifies as quality:** Qualcomm remains a dominant wireless technology and mobile-chip IP company with substantial licensing economics. On June 25, the company raised its fiscal 2029 non-handset revenue target to $40 billion, including a data-center AI infrastructure target above $15 billion, expanded its auto design-win pipeline to $65 billion, and announced Meta as a data-center CPU customer.
- **Risks / disconfirming factors:** The new data-center roadmap is execution-heavy and arrives late against entrenched competitors. Handset cyclicality, customer concentration, Apple-related risk, and AI infrastructure spending assumptions all remain real. The stock's sharp investor-day move reduces the margin of safety.
- **Valuation signals:** Current price near $197 and trailing P/E near 21. QCOM is newly added to the shortlist today because the business-quality and diversification case improved, but it ranks below TMO/DHR/ACGL/MDT/GOOGL because much of the upside case depends on targets several years out.

### 7. Elevance Health (ELV)

- **Why it looks undervalued:** ELV traded around $384 on June 25, down from the prior day's level, with a trailing P/E near 16.3. Management's FY2026 adjusted EPS guidance of at least $26.75 implies a materially lower forward multiple if the guidance is achieved.
- **Why it still qualifies as quality:** Elevance is a scaled health benefits and services company. Q1 2026 operating revenue was $49.5 billion, adjusted diluted EPS was $12.58, and management raised full-year adjusted EPS guidance, citing underlying business strength, medical-cost actions, and better visibility.
- **Risks / disconfirming factors:** This is the highest-risk top idea today. Medicaid and Medicare Advantage margins, medical cost trend, CMS/regulatory matters, and utilization volatility can overwhelm headline cheapness. The stock deserves a lower confidence score than the higher-quality healthcare tools names.
- **Valuation signals:** Current price near $384, trailing P/E near 16, and forward adjusted EPS guidance above $26.75. The valuation is attractive if medical-cost pressures stabilize, but the risk load is meaningful.

## Not Selected / Watchlist

- **Mondelez International (MDLZ):** Still a high-quality snacks franchise with resilient brands, and management expects about $3 billion of 2026 free cash flow. However, Q1 EPS declined 14.9% in constant currency largely because of cocoa cost phasing, and Q1 free cash flow was only $0.2 billion. With the stock near $62 and trailing P/E above 30, the quality is clear but the current valuation signal is less clean than yesterday's shortlist suggested.
- **NXP Semiconductors (NXPI):** Still worth watching as a quality cyclical semiconductor name, but today's Qualcomm update provides a more compelling fresh catalyst within reasonably valued semis.
- **Campbell's, Kraft Heinz, and other packaged-food deep-value names:** Several remain optically cheap in external screens, but the quality threshold is not as strong as TMO, DHR, GOOGL, ACGL, or MDT. Brand strength, leverage, growth, and margin pressure require a higher burden of proof.
- **Micron and memory peers:** Today's AI-driven earnings momentum is impressive, but the stocks are no longer clean value candidates after very large moves and a highly cyclical profit surge.

## Conclusion

The strongest current quality-value candidates are **Thermo Fisher Scientific (TMO)** and **Danaher (DHR)** because they combine durable life-science franchises with depressed cycle expectations and strong cash conversion. **Arch Capital (ACGL)** offers the clearest statistical undervaluation among high-quality financials, though insurance-cycle risk needs respect. **Medtronic (MDT)** is a credible defensive turnaround at a mid-teens adjusted earnings multiple. **Alphabet (GOOGL)** remains a high-quality large-cap technology value candidate. **Qualcomm (QCOM)** is newly interesting after its investor-day reset, while **Elevance (ELV)** screens inexpensive but carries the heaviest regulatory and medical-cost risk.
